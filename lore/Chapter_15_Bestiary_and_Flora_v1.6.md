## Master Lore: Ash Bison

# The Ash-Bison

**Common Name:** Grey-Back / The Living Rock **Scientific Designation:** _Bison Cinereus_ **Role:** Primary Food Source, Leather, Bone **Status:** Wild (Migratory)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance

- **Physiology:** Massive bovines, roughly 1.5 times the size of a standard ox. They possess four horns—two curving up for goring, and two curving down to guard the throat.
    
- **The Concrete Coat:** They do not groom themselves. Instead, they wallow in the volcanic ash and mud of the steppes. This mixture mats into their wool and hardens, creating a natural, stone-like armor plating that is arrow-proof.
    
- **Behavior:** They travel in "Thunder-Herds" of thousands. When threatened, they do not scatter; they form a tighter circle, creating a wall of horns and stone-wool.
    

## 2.0 Utility

- **The Meat:** The meat is tough, lean, and naturally salty due to the mineral-rich diet of the bison. It preserves exceptionally well as jerky.
    
- **The Hides:** The "Stone-Hide" (the tanned skin with the ash-wool still attached) is used to make the Hounds' heavy shields and yurt coverings. It is effectively light plate armor.
    
- **The Bones:** In the treeless steppes, Bison ribs are the primary source of "lumber" for tent frames and tool handles.
    

## 3.0 The Hunt

- **Tactics:** Because arrows bounce off their flanks, the Hounds must engage in the **"Spine-Leap."** Riders on Thunder-Striders pull alongside the galloping herd, and a hunter leaps from the saddle onto the Bison's back, driving a dagger into the gap between the armor plates at the spine. It is the most dangerous rite of passage for a young Hound.

**1. The Apex Opportunist: The "Cloud-Cutter Ray"**

• **Taxonomy:** _Aether-Manta_ (Colloquially: "Cutters," "Sky-Dolphins," or "Storm-Stingers").

• **Biology:**

    ◦ **Size:** massive, spanning 2 to 3 times the size of a human.

    ◦ **The Crest:** A reinforced, bony ridge along the nose and leading edge of the wings. This is used for **ramming** rival males or stunning prey, but it can easily crack the hull of a light skiff.

    ◦ **The Chaos-Gland:** Located at the base of its tail, this gland transmutes ambient chaos-bleed into a highly volatile, necrotizing fluid. While the severed stingers were once used as crude weapons, the underworld’s elite assassins have discovered a far more insidious application. The unruptured glands are harvested by poachers and submerged in vats of **Aether-Clear** to extract the concentrated toxin known as "The Red Tear" or "Ray-Blight."

    ◦ **Camouflage:** They possess **Active Chromatophores** (Chameleon Skin). They perfectly match the time of day—azure at noon, bruised purple at twilight, starry black at night. The only "tell" is the shimmer of their wake.

• **Behavior: "The Sailor’s Omen":**

    ◦ **Fair Weather:** In calm skies, they are playful. They ride the "pressure wake" of airships like dolphins, breaching and chattering. Crews often toss them scraps of meat for good luck.

    ◦ **Storm Weather:** When the barometric pressure drops (Chaos Storms or standard weather), their temperament flips. The static charge in the air makes them hyper-aggressive and territorial. They view the airship not as a playmate, but as a rival predator intruding on their "Cloud-Nest."

• **Nesting:** They act like **Bubble-Nesting Fish**. They secrete a mucus that hardens the water vapor of a cloud into a semi-solid foam. A "Hard Cloud" is a warning sign—it means a Ray nest is inside. Disturbing a cloud bank is a gamble; you might fly straight into a nursery and face the wrath of a pod.

### 2.0 Underworld Alchemy & The Ray-Gland Toxin

*   **The Extraction ("The Red Tear"):** Aether-Clear is typically used to halt chaos mutations. However, when a raw Chaos-Gland is preserved within it, the medicine acts as a slow, hyper-efficient solvent. It extracts the raw, necrotizing chaos-fluid from the gland, creating a concentrated, clear, and odorless liquid toxin often referred to in the underworld as "The Red Tear" or "Ray-Blight." Because it is odorless and clear, it is easily slipped into heavy, strong-tasting alcohols like Fir-Gin, Mandible-Mash, or Scale-Cracker to entirely avoid detection.
*   **The Dosage Mechanics (The Assassin's Calculus):** The lethality of this toxin relies entirely on the precise volumetric measurement of the dose. It is famously touchy, and a miscalculation yields drastically different—but uniformly horrific—results.
    *   **The Under-Dose (The Survivor's Curse):** If too little of the toxin is administered, the victim does not die. Instead, the localized chaos induces a horrifying, agonizing disfigurement. The victim's flesh melts, warps, and resettles, leaving them permanently scarred and twisted by the diluted chaos-bleed, serving as a gruesome, living warning to others.
    *   **The Over-Dose (Catastrophic Rupture):** If an assassin is heavy-handed and administers too much, the victim's internal "Aetheric pressure" violently overloads. This results in a catastrophic and immediate destruction of the body, causing the victim to violently explode in a shower of gore. This is not merely messy; the resulting biological shrapnel is saturated with raw chaos. The splatter causes instant, agonizing burns and necrotizes any living tissue it makes contact with, often killing or maiming bystanders and the assassin alike.
    *   **The Perfect Dose (The Calcified Husk):** The mark of a true master assassin is the perfect dose. When administered exactly, the toxin swiftly and silently twists the victim's internal biology. The necrosis rapidly consumes their organs, causing the victim to curl in on themselves in a rictus of silent agony. The body is quickly reduced to a mummified husk, which then rapidly calcifies. The victim is left as a weird, mineral-like, shriveled horror—a petrified statue of their own agonizing death.

**3. The Sailor's Taboo: "Killing the Pilot"**

• **The Superstition:** Sailors believe Cloud-Cutters are "Pilots of the Wind." Because they ride pressure wakes and predict storms, killing one is seen as **"Blinding the Ship."**

• **The Consequence:** If a crewman kills a Ray, he is often marooned at the next port. No captain will sail with a "Ray-Killer" for fear that the wind will die or the storms will target them specifically.

## Master Lore: Cloud Ram



**Common Name:** The Panda-Goat, The Red Hopper **Scientific Name:** _Capra ailurus_ **Habitat:** The High Peaks (Engineer’s Mountain Range) **Role:** Primary Livestock / Textile Resource

> **"It looks like a child's toy—soft ears, red fur, cute face. Then you see it jump thirty feet up a vertical granite slab, and you realize it’s pure muscle wrapped in a blanket."** — _Summit-Walker Shepherd_

### 1.0 Biology & Physiology

A chimera-like herbivore perfectly adapted to the thin air and freezing temperatures of the high peaks.

- **Chimera Morphology:**
    
    - **The Head:** Resembles a Red Panda ([[Red Pandas]] fulgens), featuring distinct white "tear track" markings and large, intelligent eyes. It possesses curling, majestic **Ram Horns** used for mating dominance and defense.
        
    - **The Legs:** The rear legs are disproportionately long and powerful, similar to a Mountain Hare or Kangaroo, allowing for explosive vertical leaps. The front legs end in split, hardened hooves (like a Mountain Goat) designed to grip millimeter-thin ledges.
        
- **The Coat (Thermal Wool):**
    
    - It is not magical; it is simply biologically perfect. The fur is a dual-coat system. The underlayer is incredibly dense, while the outer guard hairs are hollow (similar to a Polar Bear).
        
    - **Properties:** It retains heat even when wet and is windproof. It is the only material, other than magical **[[Voltaic Fleece]]**, that allows survival on the highest peaks during **Dimfreeze** (Winter).
        

### 2.0 Economic Importance

The Cloud-Ram is the economic engine of the **Summit-Walkers**.

- **The Wool Trade:** "Summit-Red" wool is a valuable export. It is prized by the **[[River Folk]]** and **[[Heartland Alliance]]** for winter coats. It doesn't have the static danger of Sky-Grazer fleece, making it safe for commoners to wear.
    
- **Dietary Staples:** They provide a rich, fatty milk used to make "Peak-Cheese" (a hard, salty cheese that never spoils) and their meat is a staple protein.

## Master Lore: Draft Beetle

# The Draft-Beetle (Rhina-Beetle)
**Scientific Designation:** *Coleoptera Gigas*
**Primary Rancher:** The Marsupials (Opossums) & [[Simians]]
**Farm Location:** Everywhere (Used for transport)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **Size:** The size of a pony or small car. Encased in iridescent green/black chitin.
* **The Grip:** Their legs have thousands of microscopic hooks that allow them to walk vertically up tree trunks while carrying a load.
* **The Horn:** Males have a massive forked horn used for lifting pallets.

## 2.0 Industrial Utility
* **Transport:** They are the trucks of the Canopy. They pull sleds across branch-highways and haul crates up vertical shafts.
* **Eco-Friendly:** They run on "Bio-Fuel" (Green-Coal and rotten fruit).
* **The Shell:** When they die of old age, their shell is harvested to make shields, roofing tiles, and instrument bodies.

## 3.0 Temperament & Danger
* **Aggression:** Low. They are stubborn but gentle.
* **Handling:** They are guided by "Clickers" (small noise-makers) or pheromone-wands. If they get scared, they just stop moving and lock their legs to the tree. You cannot move a scared Draft-Beetle with anything less than a crane.

# Dune Dogs ("The Living Velcro")

**Core Concept:** A lethargic, highly efficient desert insectivore. They are round, hedgehog-meerkat hybrids that hunt by trapping bugs in their velcro-like fur while they sleep.

## I. Biology & The "Velcro-Trap"

**Classification:** Desert Insectivore. **Appearance:** A cross between a kitten and a hedgehog, but round and pudgy. **Locomotion:** They possess short legs and poor agility.

- **"Tumble-Travel":** To descend dunes or escape, they tuck their heads and roll. They are surprisingly durable, bouncing off rocks like rubber balls.
    

### 1. The Hunting Mechanism: "The Shimmer-Lure"

They do not chase prey; they invite it.

- **The Lure:** During the heat of the day, they lay motionless on the dune crests. Their specialized quills excrete a trace pheromone that refracts light, creating a **"Heat-Haze"** that looks exactly like evaporating moisture.
    
- **The Trap:** Thirsty desert insects (and **[[Gem-Shell Beetles]]**) swarm the "water."
    
- **The Velcro:** The bugs land on the fur/quills. The quills are hooked at a microscopic level (like Velcro). The bugs get tangled instantly. The more they struggle, the deeper they stick.
    
- **The Feast:** At sundown, the Dune Dog wakes up. It acts as a self-cleaning plate, grooming the bugs out of its fur for breakfast.
    

### 2. The 100 Coats (Regional Adaptation)

Their fur and quills are highly absorbent of environmental minerals, creating distinct regional variants valued by breeders.

- **The Rust-Coats (Reds/Oranges):** Native to the borders of the **[[Iron Caldera]]**. Their fur is stained by iron oxide.
    
- **The Slag-Coats (Grays/Metallics):** Native to the **Vaneer Wastes**. They absorb industrial metal dust, giving them a shiny, armored look.
    
- **The Aether-Coats (Blues/Purples):** Native to the edges of **[[Chaos Zones]]**. Their quills glow faintly at night.
    
- **The Pollen-Coats (Greens/Yellows):** Native to the dry edges of the **Flower Valley**.
    

## II. Social Structure: "The Supply Chain"

They operate on a rigid, logistical family unit that mirrors the efficiency of the desert factions.

### 1. The Polyandrous Unit

- **Ratio:** 1 Female (Matriarch) to 3-4 Males.
    
- **The Division of Labor:**
    
    - **The Female:** Remains in the burrow to rear the **"Quinttens"** (litters of 5).
        
    - **The Males:** Operate on a strict **"Third-Day Rotation."**
        
        - Males go out to "sleep" (hunt) on the dunes.
            
        - Every third day, a male returns to the nest, covered in trapped bugs.
            
        - **The Feeding:** He does not regurgitate food; he _is_ the plate. The mother and kittens groom him, eating the bugs directly off his back.
            

### 2. Architecture: "The Pebble Igloo"

- **The Material:** They cannot dig deep into shifting sand, so they build up.
    
- **The Construction:** Males collect small, flat stones and pile them in shaded outcroppings.
    
- **The Design:** These domed, igloo-like structures use thermal venting principles to keep the interior cool during the day and warm at night. Finding a cluster of "Pebble Igloos" is a sure sign of a healthy dune ecosystem.
    

## III. Economy: The Pet & Pest Trade

Because they are soft (quills are not sharp) and cute, they are a high-value commodity.

### 1. The Urban Pet

- **Popularity:** **[[River Folk]]** and **Raccoons** love them. A Dune Dog in a tavern keeps the flies down and provides entertainment when it rolls off a barstool.
    
- **Maintenance:** They are low-energy. You don't walk a Dune Dog; you place it in a sunny spot and let it "shimmer."
    
- **The Ironborn Exception:** Even the hardened warriors of [[Iron Caldera]] have a soft spot for the "Rust-Coat" variety. It is not uncommon to see a massive, armored Ironborn walking through the market with a pudgy, orange Dune Dog sleeping in his helmet.
    

### 2. Pest Control (The Sump-Kin Demand)

- **The Problem:** [[Sump-Kin]] swamps are plagued by "Bloat-Flies."

## Master Lore: Fur Wyrm

# The Fur-Wyrm

**Common Name:** The Peak-Coyote, The Tube-Rat, The Slink **Scientific Name:** _Serpens villosus_ **Habitat:** Everywhere in the Mountain Range (Pest Species) **Role:** Scavenger / Nuisance

> **"Check your boots. Check your packs. Check your pockets. If you wake up and your rations are gone, it's a Wyrm. If you wake up and your finger is gone... well, you must have smelled like rations."** — _Summit-Walker Trail Guide_

### 1.0 Biology: The Cold-Weather Scavenger

A biological oddity that fills the ecological niche of a coyote or raccoon, but with the body plan of a snake.

- **Appearance:** A 3-to-4 foot long serpent covered in thick, coarse, grey-brown fur. It has a canine-like muzzle with sharp, nipping teeth and large ears.
    
- **Locomotion:** It moves with a "Side-Winding" motion on snow, or a slinking, fluid motion through rocky crevices.
    
- **Thermal Regulation:** They are prone to freezing if they stop moving. This drives them to seek heat sources—campfires, steam vents, and _sleeping bags_.
    

### 2.0 Behavior: The Opportunist

They are not apex predators; they are cowards.

- **Scavenging:** They raid food stores, steal eggs from Avian nests, and eat garbage left by the Titan-Roaches.
    
- **Aggression:** They rarely attack a standing, conscious target.
    
    - **The Threat:** They attack the solitary, the starving, or the wounded. If a traveler falls and breaks a leg, a pack of Fur-Wyrms will circle, waiting for the target to fall asleep before nipping at exposed warmth.
        
- **The Noise:** They communicate with a high-pitched, yipping chatter that echoes off the canyon walls, often ruining the sleep of anyone camping nearby.

 **(The Industrial Barnacle)**

• **Classification:** _Lithovore parasiticus_. A heavy-metal eating pest that plagues the **[[Iron Caldera]]** and **[[Factions and Organisations/Vaneer Concord]]**.

• **Biology:**

    ◦ **Appearance:** Roughly the size of a dinner plate. They resemble a cross between a horseshoe crab and a pill bug, with a heavy, segmented carapace.

    ◦ **Diet:** They are **Lithovores** (rock/metal eaters) that crave refined alloys and immense heat. They do not eat flesh; they eat _machines_.

    ◦ **The Shell:** Their carapace is composed of the metals they consume. A Tick feeding on copper pipelines has a green, conductive shell; one feeding on **Ironborn** slag has a rusted, jagged black shell.

• **Behavior: "The Heat-Seekers"**

    ◦ **Attraction:** They are biologically drawn to the thermal output of **Aether-Boilers** [Source 460]. They swarm the exterior of engines, latching on with magnetic hooked legs.

    ◦ **The Threat:** A single Tick is an annoyance. A colony is a disaster. They insulate the boiler with their bodies, trapping the heat inside. This causes the **Volatile Aether** to overheat, risking a **"Blowout"** (Catastrophic Reality Failure) [Source 460].

• **Economic Impact:**

    ◦ **"Poor Man’s Ingot":** **[[Sump-Kin]]** [Source 694] and low-caste **Ironborn** hunt them. Since the Tick refines the metal in its gut to build its shell, throwing a Tick into a smelter yields a pure nugget of alloy. They are living recycling bins.

    ◦ **The Counter-Measure:** This necessitates the use of **[[Voltaic Fleece]]** (Conversation History). Engineers wrap boilers in the fleece not just to keep heat in, but because the static charge repels the Ticks.

# The Gem-Shell Beetle

**Core Concept:** A high-altitude lithovore (rock-eater) with a carapace made of crystallized mineral and Dragonstone dust.

## I. Biology & Physiology

**Classification:** Coleopteran Lithovore. **Habitat:** The "Death Zone" altitudes of the mountain peaks ([[Iron Caldera]] / Scute Archipelago).

### 1. The Crystalline Carapace

- **Diet:** They feed on lichen and, crucially, the **mineral dust** eroded from Dragonstone Prisons and volcanic rock.
    
- **Metabolism:** Their digestive system isolates the silica and Aether-trace elements, excreting them to form their shell.
    
- **The Shell:** It is not chitin; it is a bio-organic **Gemstone**.
    
    - _Properties:_ Extremely hard (Mohs scale 8-9) and highly refractive.
        
    - _Appearance:_ Iridescent. They look like walking diamonds or opals.
        

### 2. Defense Mechanism: "The Solar Flare"

- **Reflex:** When threatened, the swarm instinctively angles their shells toward the sun (or the Moon during high-glow phases).
    
- **Effect:** They create a blinding flash of concentrated light, disorienting predators (like young Rays) long enough to burrow into the rock.
    

## II. Ecological Role

### 1. The "Ray-Candy"

- **The Prey:** They are the primary food source for Cloud-Cutter Ray fledglings.
    
- **The Crunch:** Rays crave them for the mineral content, which hardens the Ray's own bones and beak.
    
- **Scute Domestication:** This is the secret to the **"Nest-Heist."** Scute aspirants use crushed beetles (a paste called "Gem-Jam") to lure the fledglings. The smell is irresistible to a hungry Ray.
    

### 2. The Infrastructure Pest

- **The Danger:** Because they eat minerals, swarms are attracted to the high-quality stone of **[[Meridian Chain]]** towers and **[[Iron Caldera]]** architecture.
    
- **The "Rot":** A swarm can chew through a stone bridge foundation in a week if unchecked. This creates a job market for **"Beetle-Breakers"** (exterminators).

**The Species Profile: The Glass-Scale Sand-Eel**

• **Classification:** _Serpens vitreus_. A chaos-mutated, subterranean predator native to the **Dust Bowl** and the outskirts of the **[[Iron Caldera]]**.

• **The "Y" Physiology:** Both sexes exhibit a bizarre "Bifurcated" biology, likely a mutation caused by the chaotic energies of **Carulkem** (The Iron Hide), whose influence fractures and splits the earth.

**A. The Female: "The Friction-Hunter"**

• **Physiology:** Approx. 0.5 meters long. The body splits at the midsection into two muscular tails.

• **The Barbs:** The skin is not smooth; it is covered in directional, glass-like scales that function like shark skin or industrial Velcro.

    ◦ _Forward Motion:_ Slick and frictionless, allowing them to "swim" through loose sand at high speed.

    ◦ _Backward Motion:_ The scales flare outward. If a female wraps her tails around a leg and pulls, the scales act like a saw, tearing flesh and gripping bone.

• **Tactics:** They hunt in swarms of 30-50. They do not kill; they **anchor**. They swarm a target (a **Gritbore** or a **Dune Dog**), bite in, and use their tails to drag the prey down into the soft sand, immobilizing it for the male.

**B. The Male: "The Twin-Maw King"**

• **Physiology:** Massive, reaching up to 2 meters. Instead of splitting at the tail, the male bifurcates at the neck, possessing **two distinct heads** (Bicephalic Gigantism).

• **The Mane:** A ridge of muscle along the spine can protrude a mane of "Hair-Quills."

    ◦ _The Hazard:_ These are not poison spines; they are ultra-fine, brittle silicate filaments (biological fiberglass). On contact, they shatter and embed into skin, eyes, and lungs.

    ◦ _Industrial Threat:_ These filaments are fine enough to bypass standard air filters. If a male is killed near an air intake, the quills can be sucked into an engine, seizing up the gears of an **Ironborn** crawler.

• **Role:** The executioner. Once the females have dragged the prey into the sand, the male emerges to deliver the killing bites with his twin sets of fangs.

**2. The Ecological Hazard: "The Liquefaction Pits"**

This behavior turns the map into a minefield.

• **The Ritual:** During mating season, thousands of Eels gather in stable, hard-packed desert zones. They perform a violent, subterranean "Thrashing Dance."

• **The Result:** The vibration pulverizes the stone and soil structure, aerating the sand until it loses all surface tension.

    ◦ _The Trap:_ A patch of ground that looks solid but acts like water. A **Gritbore** herd or an **Ironborn** convoy that drives into a mating ground sinks instantly to the axles.

• **The Defense:** The males violently protect these communal nests. If a vehicle sinks, the males swarm the vents and hatches, shedding their quills to force the crew out.

**Sand-Eel Larvae (The Glass-Grubs)**

• **Classification:** _Serpens vitreus (Juvenile)_. The vulnerable early stage of the terrifying **Glass-Scale Sand-Eel** (Conversation History).

• **Habitat:** They are born in the **"Liquefaction Pits"** created by the adults during the mating dance.

• **Biology:**

    ◦ **Appearance:** They look like translucent, gelatinous ribbons, about 6-12 inches long. They lack the glass-hard armor of the adults.

    ◦ **The Solar Need:** Unlike the adults who hunt deep via vibration, the larvae are **solar-metabolic**. They must swim just inches below the surface of the sand to absorb intense sunlight, which they use to crystallize the silica in their gut into their future glass scales.

• **Ecological Role:**

    ◦ **The Food Source:** Because they must stay near the surface to harden their armor, they are the primary food source for the **Dust-Skippers** (Conversation History). The birds skim the dunes to snatch these soft "grubs" before they mature.

    ◦ **The Hazard:** They are not strong enough to drag a man down, but they are **swarming biters**. Stepping into a nest of larvae results in hundreds of tiny, needle-like bites as they try to sample the "minerals" in your bone.

## Master Lore: Night Carapace

# The Night-Carapace

**Common Name:** Bobcat-Beetle, The Clicking Shadow, Lobster-of-the-Land **Scientific Name:** _Coleoptera nox_ **Habitat:** Alpine Forests and Rocky Outcrops **Role:** Predator / Luxury Food Source

> **"Crack the shell, avoid the pincers, and sauté the meat in butter. It tastes like pork and lobster had a delicious baby. Just don't let it flank you, or you're the dinner."** — _Gourmet Chef in the High Roosts_

### 1.0 Biology: The Armored Ambush

A nocturnal, predatory beetle about the size of a bobcat.

- **Visuals:** Matte-black shell that absorbs light, making it nearly invisible at night. It moves in eerie silence until it strikes.
    
- **The Mandibles:** Oversized and serrated, designed to snip the tendons of prey (usually Cloud-Rams or unwary travelers).
    
- **Diet:** Highly specific. While they hunt for sport/defense, they primarily consume rare alpine herbs and roots. This diet infuses their muscle tissue with complex, aromatic flavors.
    

### 2.0 The Culinary Risk

Hunting the Night-Carapace is a dangerous trade, but highly lucrative.

- **The Hazard:** They become **Floating Minefields**.

# The Quake-Stalker

**Common Name:** Quake-Stalker / Ash-Spider / Ground-Breaker **Scientific Designation:** _Araneus Seismicus Colossus_ **Role:** Heavy Cavalry, Ambush Platform **Status:** Domesticated (Bonded — Eastern Hounds Exclusive)

> *"You hear nothing. You see nothing in the fog. Then the ground opens up beneath your feet and the world becomes legs."* — Survivor account, Meridian Chain Caravan 1644 A.W.

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance

- **Physiology:** A colossal armoured burrowing spider. The body mass (cephalothorax and abdomen combined) is equivalent to a large draft horse. Eight heavily articulated legs extend outward in a wide, flat stance that distributes the spider's weight evenly across soft ash-dune terrain, sand, and loose steppe soil. Each leg terminates in a dense cluster of sensory setae and a retractable spade-claw used for excavation.
    
- **Colouration:** The carapace is naturally the grey-black of compacted volcanic ash. In ash-dune terrain, a stationary Quake-Stalker at rest is nearly invisible to the naked eye at forty feet.
    
- **Eyes:** Compound eyes are present in the standard spider arrangement (two primary, six secondary) but are entirely vestigial. All lenses are milk-white, opaque, and non-functional. The Quake-Stalker is clinically, completely blind.
    
- **Natural Armour:** The chitin carapace across the cephalothorax is approximately 1.5 inches thick at its heaviest points — comparable to brigandine plate armour. The leg joints are proportionally thinner and represent the primary vulnerability in combat. Veteran Quake-Stalkers accumulate significant scarring across the leg joints from past engagements.
    

## 2.0 Sensory Adaptation: The Seismic Web

The Quake-Stalker does not perceive the world through sight, smell, or sound. It perceives it through **vibration**.

- **The Setae:** Thousands of microscopic sensory hairs cover all eight legs and the underside of the cephalothorax. Each hair is an independent seismographic instrument, capable of detecting the minute ground-vibrations produced by footsteps, heartbeats, and even controlled breathing at range.
    
- **Detection Range:** Through loose steppe soil or packed earth: a walking human at 60 feet; a heartbeat at 40 feet; a stationary breathing creature at 20 feet.
    
- **Immunity:** Because the Quake-Stalker does not use vision, sound, or scent as primary senses, illusion magic (visual or auditory hallucinations) has no effect. A Quake-Stalker will walk through a projected wall of fire without hesitation, because its feet tell it the ground is solid and the "threat" produces no vibration.
    

## 3.0 Utility & Combat

- **The Burrowing Approach:** Quake-Stalkers can excavate through sand, loose soil, and compacted steppe earth at walking pace. A unit of burrowing Quake-Stalkers makes no surface noise during approach. They feel the vibration of a caravan above them and surface simultaneously at a pre-determined signal (a single Thump-Code tap transmitted through the earth). The enemy has no warning.
    
- **The Surface Sprint:** On open ground, Quake-Stalkers can reach 35 mph at full gallop. Eight legs moving in coordinated pairs provide exceptional stability across uneven terrain, ash dunes, and rocky steppe.
    
- **The Paralytic Bite:** Venom glands in the chelicerae produce a fast-acting paralytic toxin. A full bite is sufficient to immobilize a draft animal or armoured soldier within thirty seconds. The Quake-Stalker does not use its venom to kill — it uses it to hold prey stationary while its rider dismounts to deal the killing blow. This is a trained behaviour, not an instinct.
    
- **The Chelicerae Crush:** The chelicerae themselves, independent of venom, are capable of exerting sufficient crushing force to shear through iron wheel rims, chain links, and light structural metal. Hound raiders have been observed using their mounts to dismantle wagon chassis rather than loading and carrying them.
    
- **Magical Immunity:** Same principle as the Thunder-Strider — visual illusion magic is useless. Additionally, because the Quake-Stalker's sensory system is mechanical (vibration-based) rather than magical or biological in the conventional sense, it is significantly harder to confuse with sensory-disruption spells.
    

## 4.0 Handling & The Bond

- **The Thump-Code:** Commands are issued by the rider tapping rhythmic sequences on the carapace with the pommel of a weapon or a gloved fist. The spider's setae interpret these taps as directional and speed commands. The full Thump-Code vocabulary used by the Eastern Hounds contains approximately forty distinct command-sequences, covering everything from "advance at burrowing pace" to "surface immediately and scatter."
    
- **The Bond:** A Quake-Stalker is attuned to the unique vibration-signature of its bonded rider — the specific rhythm of that person's heartbeat, footstep, and breathing. It will not accept another rider's Thump-Code commands with full reliability. A spider whose rider dies will typically cease eating within five to seven days and eventually stop responding to all stimuli. The Eastern Hounds consider this behaviour an act of loyalty, not grief — the spider is not mourning; it has simply lost its reference signal.
    
- **Juvenile Trade:** Unhatched or newly hatched Quake-Stalker juveniles are the Hounds' primary export to the Canopy Clans. Juveniles are bonded before they develop their first full moult, making the process of imprinting a new rider relatively straightforward. An adult Quake-Stalker cannot be re-bonded.
    

## 5.0 Pre-Raid Preparation (The Silent-Ride Protocol)

Before a raid, Hound riders bind wool batting around the lower tarsal segments of each leg — eight legs per spider, secured with leather cord. This muffles the faint clicking of the spade-claws against hard ground surfaces. During burrowing approaches, no muffling is necessary; loose soil produces no audible click. The muffles are fitted only when surface movement is required near the target.

The spider's carapace is typically rubbed with rendered Ash-Bison fat before night operations, dulling any ambient light reflection off the chitin surface.

### A Scholar's Compendium of the Shattered World's Fauna

*From the Archives of the Great Library, Snow Peak UniversityAuthored by: REDACTED, Lead Xenobiologist*

##### 1.0 Introduction: Life in the Wake of Chaos

In the millennia since [[The Shattering]], the surviving civilizations of this world have come to understand a fundamental truth: knowledge is [[Magistar Stagus]] against chaos. Here at Snow Peak University, our academic pursuits are not mere intellectual exercises; they are essential contributions to the continued survival of our peoples. Among the most critical fields of study is xenobiology—the comprehensive analysis of the world's fauna. Understanding the creatures that inhabit our lands, skies, and waters is paramount. From the docile herd animals that represent stable sources of food and materials to the chaos-warped monstrosities that defy natural law, each species presents a unique set of opportunities, threats, and lessons about the world's fractured ecology. A thorough grasp of this bestiary informs everything from the logistics of global commerce to the tactics of warfare and, most importantly, illuminates the lasting and pervasive impact of [[The Shattering]] itself.This compendium seeks to provide a foundational overview of the most significant creatures documented by our scholars. We shall begin by examining the native wildlife that has adapted to the stable zones, establishing a baseline for the world's natural order. From there, we will explore the domesticated and specialized fauna that have become the indispensable engines of civilization. The report will then touch upon the ambiguous lifeforms that blur the lines between flora and fauna, before concluding with a necessary, if brief, note on the terrifying and little-understood entities spawned directly from the [[Chaos Zones]].

##### 2.0 Native Wildlife of the Stable Zones

The vast, impassable [[Chaos Zones]] have created pockets of relative normalcy known as "stable zones." While far from tranquil by pre-Shattering standards, these regions are governed by a consistent, if harsh, set of natural laws. The creatures that have evolved here serve as our primary reference for the world's baseline ecology, having adapted to phenomena such as the broken moon's erratic tidal effects and the world's dramatically elongated seasons. Their resilience and unique survival strategies offer invaluable insight into the fundamental nature of life in our era.

###### *2.1 The Sky-Grazer*

* **Physical Description:**  The Sky-Grazer is a truly colossal herd animal, a living island of flesh and gas that floats serenely through the upper atmosphere. Its form is bulbous and buoyant, moving with a deceptive grace that belies its immense size.  
* **Behavior and Diet:**  These creatures are fundamentally docile, moving in large, slow-drifting herds. Their diet consists primarily of airborne vegetation—spores, pollen, and floating flora—and they are often observed consuming clouds rich in static energy. Their seemingly passive consumption of static-rich clouds is a fascinating area of metabolic study, suggesting a form of biological energy conversion not seen in terrestrial fauna. Their peaceful grazing is a common and reassuring sight for airship travelers.  
* **Ecological Role and Hazard Assessment:**  While individually harmless, a Sky-Grazer herd presents a significant navigational hazard. Like their terrestrial counterparts, they are prone to stampedes when startled. A panicked herd, thundering through the skyways, can easily collide with and destroy even the most robust airships or damage floating structures, making them a force of nature that all pilots must respect and avoid.

###### *2.2 The Gritbore*

* **Physical Description:**  The Gritbore is a remarkable example of adaptation to one of the world's harshest environments. It is a large creature, built low to the ground, with a unique physiology that blends rodent-like and reptilian features. Its body is protected by massive, overlapping scales of a rust and sandstone hue, giving it the appearance of a cross between a warthog and an armadillo. It possesses a set of large, curving horns reminiscent of cattle, and its prominent front teeth are perpetually stained from its unusual diet of gnawing on rocks to extract minerals. Its common name, 'Gritbore,' is a functional descriptor derived from its habit of surviving in the desert grit and boring into the earth for sustenance.  
* **Habitat and Domestication:**  Gritbores are native to the punishing desert landscapes surrounding [[Iron Caldera]]. Their ability to thrive in this volatile environment made them prime candidates for domestication. Today, they are herded nomadically by the Resource Management Guild of [[Iron Caldera]], serving as the faction's primary source of livestock.  
* **Ecological Role and Hazard Assessment:**  The Gritbore's unique survival adaptations are the key to its value. It is incredibly tough and self-sufficient, capable of finding sustenance where no other large creature can. While domesticated, its powerful build and formidable horns demand respect from its handlers.The remarkable resilience of these native species demonstrates life's capacity to endure even the most dramatic environmental upheaval. Yet, survival is only the first step; the intelligent peoples of this world have gone further, harnessing the power of specific fauna to build and sustain their civilizations.

##### 3.0 Domesticated & Specialized Fauna: The Engines of Civilization

In a world defined by vast distances and treacherous terrain, the domestication of animals has been a cornerstone of societal development. The various factions and cultures have demonstrated remarkable ingenuity in identifying and breeding creatures whose unique abilities can be leveraged for transport, warfare, and communication. These animals are not merely tools but living assets, their specialized functions making them indispensable to the survival and prosperity of their masters.

###### *3.1 Beasts of Burden and Transport*

**3.1.1 Terrestrial Hauling: The Sump-Galleon Turtle**The Sump-Galleon Turtle is the backbone of [[Sump-Kin]]'s logistics empire. These are tortoises of immense size, with shells as wide as a road, that are nonetheless surprisingly agile. Heavily armored and tireless, they are domesticated to serve as the living engines for the infamous "Turtle Trains." By strapping large, cylindrical metal wagons to their shells, [[Sump-Kin]] have created unstoppable convoys capable of moving heavy cargo across the continent. [[Sump-Kin]]'s domestication of this single species has effectively allowed them to monopolize terrestrial heavy freight, creating an economic empire built on reptilian resilience where traditional infrastructure is an impossibility.**3.1.2 Sub-Aquatic Propulsion: The Abyssal Snail**The enigmatic faction known as The Chain utilizes a creature of colossal power to drive its ships: the Abyssal Snail. These colossal mollusks are tethered to vessels via immense iron chains and swim deep beneath the surface. This method of propulsion generates an unstoppable torque, allowing the ships to completely ignore adverse weather like headwinds and storms, as well as powerful surface currents. The difficult and dangerous task of wrangling these deep-sea behemoths is handled exclusively by Walrus and Seal-kin.

###### *3.2 Mounts & Steeds of War and Peace*

A reliable mount confers universally recognized tactical and logistical advantages. Different cultures have tamed creatures perfectly suited to their specific environmental and military needs.

* **Giant Water Moccasins:**  The Rifle-Frogs of [[Sump-Kin]] ride these large, venomous snakes into battle. The moccasins serve as formidable cavalry mounts, presenting a dual threat to any adversary. The snake itself can deliver a lethal bite or a powerful tail-whip, while the frog rider provides fire with a lever-action rifle from its back.  
* **Sting and Manta Rays:**  The Current Riders, a renowned courier service associated with the Sunken Theocracy, have domesticated various rays for use as aquatic steeds and work animals. Their speed and maneuverability in the water are unmatched.  
* **Mountain Goats:**  For their land-based operations, the same Current Riders employ nimble mountain goats. While their precise origin remains undocumented, their presence among the aquatic-centric Current Riders suggests either a pragmatic trade with a mountain-dwelling faction or an isolated population uniquely adapted to the coastal highlands bordering Theocracy territory. Further study is warranted.

###### *3.3 Specialized Service Animals*

Beyond simple transport or combat, certain animals have been bred for highly specialized, mission-critical roles that require intelligence and unique biological traits.

* **Gardener Snakes:**  [[Sump-Kin]]'s Slither-Guard employs a unique subspecies of smaller snake distinguished by a remarkable mutation: a pair of functional arms. These "Gardener Snakes" are invaluable agents, serving as stealthy scouts, trackers, and lookouts who can move unseen through dense undergrowth to spot ambushes.  
* **Dragon-Flies:**  To coordinate complex and dangerous operations, particularly cargo runs through [[Chaos Zones]], [[Sump-Kin]] use Dragon-Flies as messengers. These insects are fast and reliable, allowing different units to synchronize their movements with precision, a critical factor when even a moment's delay can lead to catastrophe.

###### *3.4 Companions and Status Symbols*

* **Colorful Birds of [[Scute Confederacy1]]:**  The reptilian Scute Confederacy is famous for its relationship with the vibrant birds native to its tropical, jungle-like islands. These birds are renowned for their brilliant colors and are kept as cherished pets. Their cultural significance is profound; the Scute will never sell the birds themselves, though their naturally shed feathers are a common trade good. Given the Scute's history of vengeful justice and their rise from slavery (see  *The Black Scale 7, University Archives* ), the refusal to sell these birds may be rooted in a cultural belief that life and beauty cannot be commodified, a direct repudiation of their former masters' values.The intricate partnerships forged between the world's peoples and these specialized animals are a testament to co-evolutionary ingenuity. This symbiosis is a cornerstone of societal function, projecting power and enabling feats of logistics and warfare that would otherwise be impossible. However, not all life in this world is so easily categorized or controlled.

##### 4.0 Predatory Flora & Ambiguous Lifeforms

The scholarly lines between flora and fauna, between passive plant and active predator, are not always clear in the Shattered World. A unique ecological niche is occupied by lifeforms that, while rooted like plants, have evolved sophisticated and aggressive hunting methods. These predatory flora represent a distinct and often underestimated danger, employing ambush tactics that turn the very landscape against the unwary traveler. While the Grave-Root is the most well-documented specimen, field reports contain numerous unconfirmed accounts of other aggressive floral life, suggesting this is a wider, albeit poorly understood, ecological category.

###### *4.1 The Grave-Root*

* **Appearance and Mimicry:**  The Grave-Root is a master of deception. To the casual observer, it appears to be nothing more than a dead, withered tree—a common and unremarkable sight. This perfect mimicry is the key to its success as a stationary hunter.  
* **Hunting Method:**  This carnivorous plant is an ambush predator of terrifying efficiency. Its extensive root system spreads far beneath the soil, acting as a sensory organ that detects the ground vibrations of approaching prey. When a target comes within range, the Grave-Root lashes out with thorny, vine-like appendages, snaring its victim and dragging it towards a crushing maw hidden within its trunk.  
* **Threat Assessment:**  The Grave-Root poses a severe threat to any who travel through the wilderness. Its deceptive appearance means that even experienced explorers can be caught unaware. It stands as a prime example of the insidious dangers that can be present even within the relative safety of the world's stable zones, reminding us that constant vigilance is a necessity for survival.The calculated patience of a natural predator like the Grave-Root is a danger that can, with knowledge, be anticipated. This is a cold comfort when contrasted with the entities spawned from chaos, whose existence is a violent affront to natural law.

##### 5.0 A Note on Chaos-Spawned Monstrosities

This compendium would be incomplete without acknowledging the greatest unnatural threat in the Shattered World: the creatures born from raw chaos. The impassable [[Chaos Zones]] are not merely dangerous landscapes; they are environments where the fabric of reality is warped and unstable. It is within these zones that raw energy can coalesce into monstrous beasts—entities that are not evolved, but manifested. These "Chaos Anomalies" are a direct and terrifying byproduct of [[The Shattering]].While specific bestiary entries for these creatures are exceptionally rare due to the mortal danger involved in their study, their existence is well-documented through harrowing second-hand accounts. From what our scholars have gathered, these monsters appear to be drawn to loud noises and signs of distress, a behavioral trait exploited by [[Sump-Kin]]. Their notorious "Bait & Switch" tactic for traversing [[Chaos Zones]] involves using prisoners as live bait, whose screaming and panic are used to lure the anomalies away from the main cargo train, clearing a path through the maelstrom.In conclusion, while the documented fauna of the stable zones are increasingly understood, providing resources and partnerships that allow civilization to thrive, the creatures of chaos remain a terrifying and largely unknown variable. They are a constant, visceral reminder of the world's shattered state and the unceasing vigilance required to survive within it. Therefore, continued, albeit perilous, research into the nature and potential classification of these anomalies remains one of the University's most pressing long-term objectives.

## Master Lore: Ghost Teak

# Ghost-Teak (Float-Wood)
**Scientific Designation:** *Tectona Spiritus*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Sciurini]] (Gliders)
**Grow Zone:** The Crown-Layer (Dead/Petrified Trees)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** These are the skeletons of massive ancient trees that died but remained standing in the upper canopy.
* **The Wood:** Over centuries, the sun bleaches the wood bone-white, and the sap evaporates, leaving vacuum pockets inside the grain.
* **Properties:** It is incredibly strong but weighs almost nothing. A child can lift a log the size of a telephone pole.

## 2.0 Industrial Utility
* **Aviation:** The primary material for building Airship gondolas and glider-frames.
* **Instruments:** Used by the Ants to make "Void-Lutes" because the hollow wood resonates perfectly.

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** "Sky-Jacking." Gliders attach ropes to the dead branches, cut them loose, and "surf" the falling log down to the collection nets.
* **The Risk:** Extremely high fatality rate. If the wind catches the log wrong, it acts like a sail and drags the cutter into the sky or slams them into the trunk.

## Master Lore: Grave Berry

# Grave-Berry (The Memory-Fruit)
**Scientific Designation:** *Solanum Manes*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Eastern Hounds]] (Wolf-Nomads)
**Grow Zone:** The Bone-Fields (Burial Mounds & Battlefields)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** A creeping, thorny vine that refuses to grow in healthy soil. It only takes root in soil rich in calcium and decaying organic matter. It is most commonly found growing over ancient cairns or unmarked graves.
* **The Fruit:** Small, pale white berries that look like porcelain beads. When squeezed, they bleed a thick, crimson juice that resembles fresh blood.
* **Growth Cycle:** They bloom at night and rot instantly if touched by direct sunlight. They must be harvested under moonlight.

## 2.0 Mystical & Medical Utility
* **Mnemosyne-Wine:** The Hounds ferment these berries into a potent, bitter wine.
    * **Effect:** It is a psychoactive "Memory-Enhancer." In small doses, it helps the Amnesiac Hounds recall flashes of their forgotten history. In large doses, it allows the drinker to experience the memories of the *creature buried beneath the roots*.
* **Embalming Fluid:** The juice is a powerful preservative. It arrests decay in flesh. The Hounds use it to preserve their dead (and their food stocks) for long journeys.
* **Pain-Killer:** A paste made from the seeds is a powerful numbing agent, used to treat battlefield wounds where the patient needs to remain conscious but feel no pain.

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** "The Rite of Asking." Hounds believe you must ask permission of the dead before picking the berries. If you do not, the fruit turns to ash in your mouth.
* **The Risk:** **"The Echo."** Consuming too many Grave-Berries causes "Soul-Rot." The user begins to confuse their own memories with the memories of the dead. They may forget their own name and believe they are an ancient ancestor, leading to madness.

**Core Concept:** Biological "Chaos-Sponges" that act as batteries for ambient magical aetheric feedback.

## I. Biology & Habitat

### 1. The "Anti-Photosynthesis"

- **Mechanism:** These plants lack chlorophyll. They do not feed on sunlight (which they hate); they feed on **"Moon-Haze"** (Ambient Chaos aetheric feedback).
    
- **Appearance:** Pale, translucent stalks that look like fungal-orchids. They glow faintly only during the "Purple Sky" phase of the moon.
    
- **Growth:** They act like trees, growing forever but capping at **3 Meters** in height.
    
    - _Old Growth:_ A flower found deep in a cave might be centuries old and incredibly potent.
        

### 2. The Habitat: "The Dark Dry"

- **Conditions:** They require a specific, paradoxical environment: **Warm, Dry, and Pitch Black.**
    
- **Locations:**
    
    - **The Dust Bowl Canyons:** The primary source.
        
    - **[[Iron Caldera]] Lava Tubes:** Rare, high-heat variants.
        
    - **[[Hive Commonwealth]] Undersides:** Lower quality, shaded by cliffs.
        

### 3. The Defense Mechanism: "The Mutation Cloud"

- **The Battery:** Because they absorb chaos, they are physically unstable.
    
- **Fire Hazard:** You cannot burn a field of Ghost Flowers. Doing so releases the stored chaos as a **Radioactive Mutagenic Cloud**. A brushfire in a flower patch creates a localized biological disaster.
    

## II. The Harvest: Logistics of the Dead

Harvesting is a specialized industry dominated by the **[[Dust-Husk Riders]]**.

### 1. The Method

- **Transport:** The flowers wilt/discharge if exposed to direct sunlight or humidity.
    
- **The Cactus-Kin Solution:** The Riders use the hollowed-out spines or water-sacs of their **Cactus-Kin** mounts to transport the delicate blooms. The interior of a cactus provides the perfect "cool, dark, dry" stasis chamber for transport across the desert.
    

## III. The Derivatives: Medicine & Vice

The Ghost Flower is the raw material for three distinct industries.

### 1. The Cure: "Aether-Clear" (Medicine)

- **The Process:** Fermented with **[[Ozone-Milk]]** (Avian source) by the **Quill-Kin** (Porcupines).
    
- **The Effect:** It is the only known treatment for **"Corruption Sickness"** (Chaos Mutation). It halts the spread of physical warping and clears the "Mental Fog" of chaos exposure.
    
- **The Monopoly:** The Quill-Kin keep the fermentation ratio a secret. If mixed wrong, it becomes an explosive poison.
    

### 2. The Trap: "Void-Cream" (Cosmetics)

- **The Market:** Highborn Horse Nobles and Hive Queens.
    
- **The Promise:** When processed into a topical cream, the chaos-absorbing properties "absorb" wrinkles and age, restoring youthful appearance.
    
- **The Cost:** It is astronomically expensive.
    
- **The Side Effect:** Overuse causes **"The Porcelain Curse."** The user's skin becomes too smooth, eventually turning translucent. They lose the ability to micro-express emotion, leaving them looking like beautiful, ageless dolls.
    

### 3. The Narcotic: "The 4-Year Seed"

- **The Cycle:** The flowers only seed once every 4 years.
    
- **The Drug:** The seeds are a potent hallucinogen.
    
- **The High:** Users claim to see "The Architecture of the Seals"—visions of the mathematical laws holding the world together, or glimpses of the "World That Was" before [[The Shattering]].
  
  **The Habitat:** They require **Warm, Dry, and Dark** conditions. This makes them distinct from the swampy flora of the [[River Folk]]. They thrive in the deep canyons of the **Dust Bowl**, the lava tubes of the **[[Iron Caldera]]**, and the shadowed undersides of **[[Hive Commonwealth]]** cliffs.

• **The Physics:** They absorb _ambient_ Moon-Haze (Chaos aetheric feedback) rather than the concentrated flow. This makes them "Chaos Batteries."

    ◦ _The Danger:_ Because they store chaos, burning a Ghost Flower field wouldn't just produce smoke; it would release a cloud of raw mutation. This makes harvesting them extremely hazardous.

• **Growth Cycle:** They grow forever but cap at 3 meters. This implies they become denser and more potent with age. An "Old Growth" Ghost Flower found in a deep Dust Bowl cave is a fortune waiting to be plucked.

## Master Lore: Shadow Truffle

# Shadow-Truffle (Fungal Chocolate)
**Scientific Designation:** *Tuber Umbra Dulcis*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Simians]] (Monkeys)
**Grow Zone:** The Root-Systems (Deep Canopy/Underside)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** A parasitic fungus that grows in large, fist-sized nodules on the roots of the Iron-Wood trees.
* **Appearance:** The skin is black and rough like coal. Inside, the flesh is a rich, dark purple.
* **Flavor Profile:** It tastes like 90% dark chocolate mixed with espresso and a hint of savory umami (mushroom). It is much richer and heavier than traditional cocoa.

## 2.0 Industrial & Culinary Utility
* **Caloric Density:** Extremely high energy. One truffle contains the calories of a full steak dinner.
* **Ration-Bricks:** It is pressed into dense, non-perishable bricks for military rations.
* **Luxury Confectionery:** Refined with sugar and milk, it becomes the most expensive dessert in the world ("Black-Gold Mousse").

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** Monkeys must climb *down* into the dark underside of the canopy to dig them out of the bark.
* **The Risk:** The underside is the domain of spiders and bats. Harvesters often fight for their haul.

## Master Lore: Silver Needle Tea

# Silver-Needle Tea (Ice-Blood)
**Scientific Designation:** *Camellia Cryogenica*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Red Pandas]] ([[Red Pandas]])
**Grow Zone:** The Cloud-Peaks (High Altitude, Misty)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** A stunted, hardy bush that grows only on the highest peaks of the giant trees, where the air is thin and cold.
* **The Leaves:** The leaves are covered in fine, silver fuzz (trichomes) that traps moisture. When crushed, they feel cold to the touch.
* **Growth Cycle:** Slow-growing. It takes 5 years for a bush to mature.

## 2.0 Industrial & Medical Utility
* **The "Coolant" Effect:** The plant contains a natural endothermic compound. When brewed into tea, it lowers the consumer's body temperature by several degrees.
* **The Market:** It is the lifeblood of the **[[Iron Caldera]]**. Factory workers and soldiers in heavy armor drink gallons of it to prevent heatstroke.
* **Preservation:** The dried leaves are used to pack perishables, acting as a mild natural ice-pack.

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** Hand-picked by [[Red Pandas]] at dawn when the frost is still fresh.
* **The Risk:** The altitude is dizzying, and the plants often grow on sheer vertical bark-cliffs.

## Master Lore: Stone Root

# Stone-Root (Petrified Lumber)
**Scientific Designation:** *Radix Petra*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Manis]] (Pangolins)
**Harvest Zone:** The Deep Mines (Sub-Canopy)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** Fossilized roots of the "Ancestor Trees" that died thousands of years ago.
* **Properties:** It is technically a mineral, but retains the grain and workability of wood.
* **Appearance:** Grey-brown, streaked with quartz. Heavy and cool to the touch.

## 2.0 Industrial Utility
* **Fireproof Construction:** It cannot burn. It is the only building material allowed in the explosive factories or the [[Iron Caldera]] foundries.
* **Gun Stocks:** Used by the [[Tarsiers]] for rifle stocks because it absorbs recoil better than normal wood and doesn't warp in humidity.

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** Mining. Pangolins dig deep tunnels to find the root veins and chisel them out.
* **The Risk:** Cave-ins and hitting pockets of explosive "Swamp-Gas" trapped in the soil.

## Master Lore: Sun Melon

# Sun-Melon (Nature's Canteen)
**Scientific Designation:** *Cucumis Durus*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Simians]] (Monkeys)
**Grow Zone:** Upper Canopy (Full Sunlight)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** A thick, woody vine that strangles other branches to reach the sun.
* **The Fruit:** Football-sized yellow melons with a rind as hard as oak wood.
* **The Interior:** Contains a liter of clear, electrolyte-rich water and sweet, firm yellow meat.

## 2.0 Industrial Utility
* **Hydration:** It is the standard water-source for travelers. Because the shell is so hard, it doesn't break when dropped and doesn't spoil for months.
* **The Shell:** Once eaten, the empty halves are used as bowls, helmets for children, or ladles.

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** Requires a saw or a heavy machete to cut the stem.
* **The Risk:** Falling fruit. A Sun-Melon dropping from 100 feet up can crack a skull. "Helmet-Checks" are mandatory in melon groves.

## Master Lore: Whisper Berry

# Whisper-Berry (Sparkle-Fruit)
**Scientific Designation:** *Vitis Aeris*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Simians]] (Monkeys)
**Grow Zone:** Wind-Tunnels (Mid-Canopy)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** A delicate shrub that thrives in high-wind areas.
* **The Fruit:** Clusters of translucent purple berries.
* **The Reaction:** The berries naturally trap pressurized gas inside their skins. When bitten, they pop and fizz on the tongue (Natural Carbonation).

## 2.0 Industrial Utility
* **Wine Making:** Used to produce "Canopy-Champagne." The carbonation is natural, so it doesn't need secondary fermentation.
* **Luxury Trade:** Sold to the Avian Nobility as a delicacy. The sensation of "eating air" is considered very fashionable.

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** Extremely delicate. If handled roughly, the berries pop and become worthless.
* **The Risk:** They grow in high-wind zones where gusts can blow a harvester off the branch.

**Shatterland [[Fauna1]]: Life Warped by Chaos** This subsection explores the descendants of life mutated by generations of exposure to raw, unpredictable chaos. These creatures are typically larger and more aggressive, often possessing bizarre abilities like crystalline hides or the capacity to project energy. Key species include:

• **Glimmer-Wings:** Predatory insects with wings made of shifting light that act as ambush predators.



• **Chaos-Touched Beasts:** A general classification for any creature exhibiting extreme physical or metaphysical mutations due to the proximity of a Dragon prison.

**8.2 Native Wildlife: Adapted Survivors** This section documents the natural creatures that have successfully evolved to survive in stable environments, adapting to the world's irregular tides and elongated seasons.

• **[[Sky-Grazers]]:** Massive, docile herd animals that float through the air to feed on airborne vegetation and clouds charged with static energy.

• **Grave-Roots:** Carnivorous plants that mimic dead trees, using root-based vibration sensing to lash out at unsuspecting prey.

• **Gritbores:** Large, rodent-like but heavily scaled reptilian creatures with curving horns. These survivors are famous for their ability to gnaw on rocks for food and are herded as vital livestock by the **[[Iron Caldera]]**, particularly for the community feasts held during **Shadow Week**.

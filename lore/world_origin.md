In this time, the world knew an unparalleled peace born of cosmic balance. The seasons followed their cycles without fail, magic flowed in predictable currents, and the threat of unmaking was a concept known only to the gods. The era was defined by a profound stability, a gift from the Clockwork that allowed life to flourish under a constant and ordered sky.This stable and harmonious world was the cradle for the first and most powerful of all mortal races.

  
During the Era,  [[The Fae]] arose as the world's first and most powerful race. Their connection to the world's energies was not a matter of study or ritual but an innate, effortless state of being. They did not wield power so much as they existed in concert with it, a reality that set them apart from all who would come after.

In the primordial era before [[The Shattering]], the world was a masterpiece of **Celestial Clockwork**, where the twelve powers were perfectly refined and balanced. The biomes were not chaotic "zones" but stable, idealized environments where [[The Fae]] acted as living conduits for the machine's perfect output.

Here are the twelve primordial biomes, structured by the refined powers of the Clockwork:

### 1. The Gravity Spires (Nexus)

- **The Biome:** A high-tension landscape where the "grip" of the world was perfectly calibrated.
    
- **Description:** This biome consisted of towering, smooth obsidian pillars that leaned toward one another, held in a state of perpetual magnetic balance. The air hummed with a low-frequency vibration of attraction and repulsion.
    
- **Fae Usage:** [[The Fae]] of Nexus managed the structural bonds of the world, preventing landslides and ensuring the seas and mountains stayed in their assigned places.
    

### 2. The Unyielding Foundation (Mass)

- **The Biome:** A geologically dense region that provided the world’s physical weight and permanence.
    
- **Description:** Featuring "Stone-Oaks" with lead-dense bark, this was a land where the ground was so solid it never eroded or shifted.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They reinforced the density of matter, ensuring that islands stayed afloat and the continental crust remained thick and stable.
    

### 3. The Kinetic Plains (Motus)

- **The Biome:** A high-velocity, synchronized ecosystem defined by perfect timing.
    
- **Description:** Trees here vibrated with kinetic timing, their leaves moving in a rapid, melodic shimmy that created a global heartbeat.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They regulated the speed of the world—controlling the velocity of the winds and the pace of the seasons to ensure no transition was too abrupt.
    

### 4. The Shifting Mistlands (Flux)

- **The Biome:** A delta of transformation where matter existed in a state of potential.
    
- **Description:** In these "Mirror Groves," the trees and waters were semi-liquid, constantly shifting to match the needs of the environment.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They oversaw the transformation of states—turning rain to mist or guiding the metamorphosis of creatures, acting as the masters of adaptation.
    

### 5. The Healing Cradle (Vita)

- **The Biome:** A hyper-vibrant, life-giving jungle that served as the world's primary nursery.
    
- **Description:** The air was thick with glowing moss and medicinal pollens that instantly repaired any biological damage.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They were the world's gardeners, ensuring that organic growth was healthy, vigorous, and free from disease.
    

### 6. The Eternal Sanctuary (Lex)

- **The Biome:** A silent, crystalline cathedral that embodied the unbending laws of physics.
    
- **Description:** The trees were geometric, translucent, and completely immovable—they could not be burned, cut, or broken.
    
- **Fae Usage:** These Fae were the guardians of the "Unbending Rule," ensuring the fundamental constants of reality (like gravity and causality) never wavered.
    

### 7. The Silver-Leaf Archives (Ratio)

- **The Biome:** A living library that processed and stored the "data" of the world.
    
- **Description:** Trees with silver bark had leaves that functioned like vellum pages, pulsing with blue light as they recorded every event in the vicinity.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They acted as the world's memory, utilizing psychic energy to recall centuries of history and translate it into practical action.
    

### 8. The Mathematical Orchards (Ordo)

- **The Biome:** A landscape of absolute symmetry and mathematical reasoning.
    
- **Description:** Every branch and stream followed perfect geometric angles, moving in a "perfect dance" of calculated harmony.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They applied logic to the environment, preventing natural disasters by calculating and implementing perfect offsets to any imbalance.
    

### 9. The Prism Heights (Lux)

- **The Biome:** High-altitude cliffs that captured the full spectrum of celestial light.
    
- **Description:** "Prism-Pines" refracted sunlight and starlight into thousands of colors, allowing the biome to be seen even in the deepest night.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They were the masters of perception, sensing distant cosmic events and guiding the sun and moon on their correct paths.
    

### 10. The Premonition Willows (Omen)

- **The Biome:** A sensitive, whispering woodland that reacted to the world's "vibe".
    
- **Description:** The leaves would change color or turn inward _before_ a storm or environmental shift occurred.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They acted as the "early warning system," sensing when the gears of the Clockwork were slipping and alerting the other Fae sects.
    

### 11. The Gilded Vales (Aura)

- **The Biome:** A tranquil glade that emitted a scent of profound emotional harmony.
    
- **Description:** The biome induced a sense of peace so deep that conflict was physically impossible within its borders.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They used this "vibe" to harmonize the fledgling species of Man, acting as the primary diplomats of the early age.
    

### 12. The Echoing Steppes (Anumis)

- **The Biome:** A spectral grassland where the "drive" of existence was anchored.
    
- **Description:** White trees cast no shadows, and the grass moved even without wind, carrying the voices of the past.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They anchored the spirits of the departed, ensuring their wisdom remained as a persistent "echo" to guide the living and keep the world's spirit strong.

#  **Birth of Man**

The turning point that heralded the end of this golden age was the rise of the civilization of man, who would later be ruled by the Magistars. Their culture was defined by a supreme arrogance, a deep-seated hubris that led them to believe they could, and should, hold domain over everything.

  

First there was a time of perfect harmony, sustained by the vigilant protection and tutelage of [[The Fae]]. Existing as immortal, mortal-form cousins to the gods, [[The Fae]] acted as shepherds for the 12 fledgling species of "man." They tirelessly ensured the world’s fundamental balance by showing these species the secrets of the world and the power that created and governed it all. Under the patient guidance of [[The Fae]], the 12 species of "man" eventually rose to prominence.

  

# The Guardianship: A Fae Duty

  

[[The Fae]]'s role was one of pure, selfless stewardship. They offered knowledge as a key, not a leash, fostering growth and understanding. They gifted the secrets of the earth—how to coax life from the soil, how to harness the gentle flow of the rivers, and how to read the language of the stars. The goal was simple: a stable, self-sustaining existence for the emerging species, one that respected the delicate equilibrium of the world.

  

# The Seeds of Discontent

  

As [[The Fae]]'s students grew in number and strength, the harmonious tutelage began to subtly shift. Man had been taught to use power, but not how to temper their own desires.

  

## The Growth of Greed and Jealousy

  

The initial, communal sharing of resources championed by [[The Fae]] waned, replaced by an increasingly aggressive individualism.

  

  - From Appreciation to Acquisition: Man began to view the natural world not as a shared, living library of secrets, but as a vast repository of resources waiting to be owned. Where [[The Fae]] taught them to use one stream to irrigate a field, man sought to divert two, then three, believing that more was always better, regardless of the impact on the land or their neighbors.

- The Shadow of Jealousy: The 12 species, once united by their shared Fae mentors, began to eye each other’s successes with envy. A species taught [[The Fae]]'s method of refining metals kept the knowledge secret from another, leading to a burgeoning sense of superiority and the first subtle imbalances of power. This was the first true schism—a competition for [[The Fae]]'s favor and, more dangerously, a competition for the control of knowledge itself.

## The Birth of Hubris

  

Greed gave way to the far greater sin of hubris. Man no longer saw [[The Fae]] as mentors; they saw them as rivals, as living reminders of a dependency they wished to deny.

  

  The turning point was the mastery of certain fundamental powers. While [[The Fae]] showed them the workings of the world, man started to believe they could surpass the creators.

  

  - "We Know Better": The ancient, cyclical magic of [[The Fae]], based on ebb and flow, was dismissed as slow or inefficient. Man began to develop crude, powerful forms of sorcery—magic rooted in brute force and rapid consumption, designed for immediate gratification rather than long-term sustainment. They sought to speed up the world, accelerate growth, and conquer all limits, completely disregarding [[The Fae]]'s warnings about causality and balance.

- A Claim to Divinity: [[The Fae]], with their mortal forms and immense power, were close to the gods. As man grew stronger, they began to mimic [[The Fae]]'s mannerisms, building colossal monuments to their own achievements and declaring themselves the ultimate expression of divine creation. Claiming to be made in the gods image and to be the culmination of the grand design The whispers started: "Why should we be restrained, when the world was made for us to master?"

- The Ultimate Offense: all 12 species of Man began constructing  great cities, designed to dwarf the most ancient Fae settlements.  Each a mockery of [[The Sacred Groves]] destroying the areas where they were built instead of honor it. They demanded [[The Fae]] teach them the secret of immortality, not as a gift, but as a right of their self-declared supremacy.

- Retreat of Knowledge: [[The Fae]] began to withhold the deepest secrets of the world, realizing the knowledge they imparted was being twisted into weapons of control and destruction. The gates to the most powerful magics were slowly and sadly sealed, resulting in Man interpreting this action as an act of fear, not protection.

# The First Wars of Resource and Pride

  The barrier's limitation on expansion immediately turned man's aggressive individualism inward. The fragile alliances, born of shared mentors, snapped under the weight of hoarding and jealousy.

  

- **The War of the Irrigated Coast:** Species reliant on the great river systems, seeing their expansion curtailed by the ward, clashed violently over the existing, finite water flow. The species that had perfected the Fae's method of refining metals forged the first true armies, using their secret knowledge to create weapons of war instead of tools for growth.

- **The Great Schism of Knowledge:** The struggle shifted from land to lore. Man believed that the keys to shattering the Fae's ward lay in the knowledge they had been given. Each of the 12 species held one or more unique "secrets" gifted by the Fae, and the stronger species began a brutal campaign of conquest to acquire the secrets of the weaker ones. This was not a pursuit of shared understanding, but an aggressive intellectual and magical takeover.

- **The Blood-Magic:** The crude, force-based sorcery developed by man became the engine of their wars. To sustain their armies and their rapidly expanding cities—still within the Fae's limited area—they resorted to magics of rapid consumption. These spells were fueled by resources, life-force, and increasingly, by the very souls of their rivals. It was a vicious, self-destructive arms race, entirely disregarding the Fae's lessons on cyclical balance.

  

## The Rise of the Magistars

  

  

Out of the mayhem of perpetual war, a new, power arose. As the wars raged and the blood flowed the most powerful individuals—warlords, sorcerer-kings, and tyrannical innovators—emerged as the undisputed masters of their horded power and their remaining people. These twelve individuals, facing the final battle between each other over a torn a bloodied land, wisely agreed to a temporary cease fire and met on the top of a hill in the center of the battle field.  

Their unity was not a treaty of peace, but a coalition of conquerors. They recognized that the infighting was exhausting their power and delaying their ultimate goal: dominion over the entire world, starting with the destruction of the Fae's ward.

  
  

The savage Wars of Resource and Pride had not brought peace, but had achieved a brutal, temporary stalemate. On the Crag of Final Judgement, the twelve warlords—each on the brink of mutual annihilation—assembled.

  

  The meeting was called by the Whisperer**, a sorcerer-king who had survived by ruthlessly consuming the magical and material wealth of his neighbors. Kyros possessed the strategic genius to see that their endless internecine war was not only weakening them but fulfilling the Fae's expectation of man's self-destruction. His message to the other warlords was cold and absolute: _"We die tomorrow in service of the Fae's fear, or we unite to enslave the world together. There is no other path to mastery."_

  

  **The Twelve Oaths of Iron and Blood**

  

  Kyros presented the pact not as a treaty of equals, but as a structure for supreme, shared authority. The other warlords were coerced into agreement not by camaraderie, but by the terrifying logic of combined, focused power. They declared themselves the first **Magistars**—supreme masters of their entire civilization.

  

  The core tenets of the alliance were:

  

- **A Mutual Pact of Power:** They would cease all _Magistar-on-Magistar_ hostilities. All twelve unique Fae-secrets—the lore of would be pooled and guarded by the collective. This was not sharing for the common good, but a consolidation of all strategic knowledge into one vault, accessible only to the Magistars.

- **The New Common Enemy (Unifying Scorn):** Their hatred was to be focused outward, against the Fae. The **Guardianship** was to be branded as a prison, and the ward as a final, unforgivable act of suppression that denied man its true, limitless potential.

.

- **Dominion:** With the barrier broken and the world laid open, the 12 Magistars did not divide the spoils. Instead, they turned their combined, tyrannical power onto the last vestiges of resistance, one by one. The Shadow of the Magistars was no longer a prophecy—it was a reality. In a final, swift campaign of ruthlessness, the Magistars enslaved the all the species, consolidating them under a single, tyrannical, and supremely powerful authority. The turning point was complete: man had ceased to be a student and had become a master, but at the cost of its own soul, ushering in the Dark Age of the new dominion.

# The Era of the Rending Age

  

  Under the iron fist of the Magistars, the Unified Dominion grew, but not in harmony. It grew through brutal efficiency, enforced by a newly created, magically-empowered police force. War was replaced by a massive, grinding internal oppression focused on a singular, relentless objective: generating enough energy and power to crush [[The Fae]]'s barrier.

  

- **Tyrannical Exploitation:** The new Magistar-controlled civilization was an engine of resource consumption. The species skilled in irrigation were forced to divert entire rivers to the Magistars' new, colossal capital city. The species that knew metalworking labored in brutal, subterranean mines to arm the Magistars' legions. All communal practices were replaced by a rigid, hierarchical system where the lower castes were bled dry of their time, magic, and energy to sustain the ruling elite.

- **Expansion through Internal Plunder:** The society expanded rapidly, not in a peaceful spread of culture, but in the aggressive construction of cities designed to be massive power-collection arrays. The Magistars sought  crude, destructive sorcery that caused ecological devastation, but delivered immediate, immense power for their experiments.

  

# The Pursuit of Immortality 

  

  The Twelve Magistars, seeing the sheer scale of the project, realized they would die long before the power required to shatter the barrier was amassed. They knew their successors would likely plunge the Dominion back into internal conflict.

  

  The solution was found by twisting [[The Fae]]'s  lessons on life Death and Time with there raw bloody form of using magic into the **Ritual of the Timeless Core**. This was not the elegant immortality they had first demanded; it was a brutal, self-serving sorcery that siphoned the vital force of others to indefinitely prolong the existence of the Magistars. The first twelve Masters of the Unified Dominion began a plan to ensure there survival, so they could personally guide their centuries-long project until the final, apocalyptic moment: the moment when they stood, ready to break the last barrier of their former mentors.

# The Divine War
 
 [[The Exodus]] ritual and the magisters wielding of the power had the effect of catching the eye of the gods.

  **For a generation, the Elder Gods fought directly against the 12 Magistars.** This conflict, known as the **War of the Celestial Scourge**, was not a war of mortal armies, but a clash of absolute cosmic forces. The Gods, weakened by [[The Fae]]'s earlier retreat  were forced to descend from their distant realms to confront the nascent divinity of the Magistars.

- 

- The State of the Conflict: A World Bled Dry

  

  **The War's Toll:** The ongoing war **"bled life and stability from the world."** The ground was scorched not by fire, but by the residue of conflicting cosmic energies. Rivers grew violent currents, and the air was thick  with [[Elara the Pure]], elemental wrath of the gods.

- **The Unholy Fusion:** The Magistars, led by **Kyros**, proved terrifyingly adaptive. They combined their elemental forces in ways [[The Fae]] had warned against and the gods could not counter. **Grom’s** brute Might was fused with **Rictus**’s total perception, allowing their strikes to shatter divine defenses with surgical precision.

- **The Gods' Desperation:** The Elder Gods were fighting to restore the  universal balance. They could not simply destroy the Magistars, as their corrupted power gave them the ability to shield themselves  and even twist the minds of minor gods against there kin.

### **3.0 The Backlash: Birth of the Chaos Dragons**

When the **Magistars** shattered the Celestial Clockwork, the 13th power (Chaos) reacted with their corrupted souls—warped by centuries of life-extending rituals. They did not become gods; they became **Chaos Dragons**, monstrous embodiments of the schools they once mastered.

Each Magistar was consumed by their own obsession, transforming into a "Brand of Destruction":

- **Magistar Metrion (Logic):** Became **The Crystalline Mind**. A dragon of silicon and crystalline lattice. He breathes lightning that rewrites the brains of his victims, turning them into mindless drones to solve his infinite equation.
    
- **Magistar Tiraton (Might):** Became **The World-Weight**. A legless wyrm of ultra-dense Osmium. He causes **Unstoppable Movement**, crushing reality simply by existing and swimming through the air by rejecting the gravity of the earth.
    
- **Magistar Stagus (Endurance):** Became **The Unyielding Glazier**. A dragon of interlocking Titanium plates. She imposes **Implacable Siege**, creating storms of absolute zero where nothing ever moves or lives again.
    
- **Magistar Carulkem (Fortitude):** Became **The Furnace Eternal**. A dragon of red-hot tungsten coils. He triggers **Elemental Catastrophe**, turning the world around him into an oven of thermal rage.
    
- **Magistar Vecelo (Reflex):** Became **The Silent Echo**. A dragon clad in lead that absorbs all sound. She moves faster than physics allows, creating sonic booms she cannot hear, trapped in perpetual acceleration.
    
- **Magistar Aurgenas (Finesse):** Became **The Gilded Solvent**. A dragon of molten gold that is actually corrosive acid. He represents **Deceptive Ruin**, dissolving trust and matter alike.
    
- **Magistar Opecten (Awareness):** Became **The Mirror Lord**. A dragon of polished Chromium. His gaze causes **The Clockwork Rot**, accelerating time and space to dust for anything he observes.
    
- **Magistar Termhill (Intuition):** Became **The Black Star**. A dragon of Iridium and shadow. She causes **Temporal Instability**, tearing open time rifts and inflicting necrotic decay on the future.
    
- **Magistar Virantor (Charm):** Became **The Burning Soul**. A dragon of Phosphorus smoke. His presence is intoxicatingly lethal; his "voice" burns the souls of listeners, turning them into mindless worshippers.
    
- **Magistar Lophex (Knowledge):** Became **The Geometric Void**. A dragon of Bismuth crystals and impossible angles. She breathes "Arcane Nullification," unraveling the logic and science of her enemies.
    
- **Magistar Tyrustis (Willpower):** Became **The Mad King**. A dragon of volatile Lithium. She acts as a "Void of Identity," stripping the self from everyone around her and imposing laws of physics that change moment to moment.
    
- **Magistar Gavusrix (Vitality):** Became **The Quicksilver Mother**. A dragon of tarnished silver and weeping sores. She radiates chaotic vitality, causing rapid tumor growth and mutations in all living things.

# The First Wardens

> **"We served the tyrants so we might save the world from them."**

The **First Wardens** were originally twelve high-ranking captains—one from each of the twelve warped species of "man"—who defied their masters, the [[The Twelve Magistars of the Unified Dominion|Magistars]], to fight alongside the Elder Gods during the War of the Celestial Scourge.

## Origin: The Great Defection
During the height of the Magistars' hubris, when the world was being bled dry to fuel the assault on the Fae, these twelve captains witnessed the apocalyptic cruelty of their leaders and chose to defect. They brought with them intelligence, stolen artifacts, and entire battalions of rebel soldiers.

## The Oath to the Engineer
Following the catastrophic [[The Shattering]], the First Wardens aided the youngest deity, **[[The Engineer]]**, in his final task of anchoring the Chaos Dragons into the Sacred Groves.
* As the Engineer dissolved his physical form to create the world's landscape, the Wardens were magically oath-bound to his dying consciousness.
* Their mandate was absolute: **Preserve the global prison system and contain the influence of the Chaos Dragons**.

## The Civil War
For centuries, the First Wardens lived within the petrifying remains of the Engineer (now the mountains and crust of the world), studying the [[Convergence]].
* However, the corrupting influence of the dragons they guarded began to seep into their ranks.
* A brutal civil war erupted between the loyalists and those corrupted by the **Magistars-turned-Dragons**.
* This conflict nearly destroyed the order, leaving only **three survivors** to rebuild.

# The Three Foundational Leaders

After the **Civil War** decimated the ranks of the [[History/The First Wardens|First Wardens]], only three of the original captains remained. Realizing their immortality was broken, they divided the labor of guardianship to ensure the order would survive them.

## 1. The Architect
*Likely Identity: [[Thrum the Mason]]*
* **Role:** Infrastructure and Survival.
* **Achievement:** He constructed **[[The Sacred Spire]]**, a chaos-safe fortress built into the Engineer's torso and head. He designed the deep vaults, plateau farms, and geothermal power systems that sustain the Wardens to this day.

## 2. The Prophet
*Likely Identity: [[Jiro the Resolute]] or [[Liora the Truth-Bearer]]*
* **Role:** Dogma and Recruitment.
* **Achievement:** Developed the religious dogma of the order. He created the **"Divine Imprint"**, a command programed into the evolving tribes of man: *"Guard the prisons"*. This ensures that even without training, the people feel an instinctual drive to protect the Groves.

## 3. The First Aetheric Sacrifice (The Mage)
*Likely Identity: [[Valen the Witness]]*
* **Role:** Chaos Stabilization and Sacrifice.
* **Achievement:** Conducted bold experiments with chaos energy until his eyes were destroyed, granting him **Chaos Sight** (the ability to see anywhere by tricking chaos).
* **The Sacrifice:** He became the first Warden to sacrifice his life to stabilize a [[Chaos Zones]], establishing the precedent for the "Aetheric Sacrifice" used by the order today.
* **Legacy:** He is the founder of the **Circle of the Eyeless**, the oldest Wardens who burn out their eyes to gain his sight.

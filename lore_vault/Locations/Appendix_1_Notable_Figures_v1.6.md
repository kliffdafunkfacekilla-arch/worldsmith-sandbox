## Master Lore: Master Character Compendium of Ostraka

### Master Character Compendium of Ostraka

**Archival Status:**  Restricted – Level IV Strategic Intelligence**Prepared By:**  Senior Archivist of the Order of the Clockwork**Subject:**  Systemic Failure Analysis: Primary Historical and Contemporary EntitiesThe current state of Ostraka is one of terminal life support. Our reality is a broken machine held together by the  **Triple-Hustle**  methodology—a precarious equilibrium maintained by the strategic democratization of truth through deception. To prevent any single faction from achieving a data-monopoly that would collapse our remaining structural anchors, we must catalog those who define the biological, political, and aetheric facets of this dying world.

#### 1. The "Write Wager" Crew: The Original Order

The crew of the vessel  **Write Wager**  represents a specialized biological toolkit recruited under the pretense of a "laughable" mission. By convincing the  **Gilded Compass** ,  **Free Sky-Barons** , and  **Crimson Corsairs**  to fund a pursuit of data that Clary intended to distribute widely, she secured the necessary  **Aether-Tech**  and stamina for a voyage into the heart of the world's decay.

##### 1.1 Grand Archivist Clary (The Protective Liar)

Formerly a performer who collected stories to piece together the world’s "Source Code,"  **Clary**  founded the  **Order of the Clockwork**  after her home was consumed by  **Chaos** . She is the architect of the  **Triple-Hustle** , operating on the cynical but necessary belief that the only way to protect the common people is to keep the major powers pulling in conflicting directions. As the ship’s strategic lead, she uses her research into lunar phases and  **Chaos flow**  to navigate shifts in reality that other navigators cannot predict.

##### 1.2 Captain Jax (The Catfish)

A member of the  **River Folk** ,  **Jax**  possesses the innate  **River-Sense** —a biological necessity for detecting the texture of  **Aetheric Backwash**  within turbulent waterways. Destitute after a mutiny,  **Jax**  was originally a captain for the  **Crimson Corsairs**  who "accidentally" sabotaged his own vessel to ensure winter supplies reached starving villagers. His bad temper and drinking habit mask a pilot who can navigate the "texture of reality" when sensors fail.

##### 1.3 Kog (The Engineer)

An exiled  **Lizard-Folk**  from the  **Iron Caldera** ,  **Kog**  is characterized by heavy scarring and a missing eye, the result of a furnace catastrophe for which he was scapegoated by a  **Noble Guildmaster** . His reptilian physiology provides the high heat resistance required to survive the "Blowout" risks of maintaining failing, low-grade  **Aether-Tech**  engines. He views the  **Write Wager**  not as a ship, but as a patient on a surgical table.

##### 1.4 Pipin Pringle (The Cook)

A  **Caterpillar-kin**  whose socially stigmatized delay in entering his pupa stage led to his exile. Within the crew,  **Pipin**  is responsible for managing "Fuel"—the biological stamina required to counteract the energy-draining effects of  **Chaos Zones** . Given the hyper-metabolism common in canopy species,  **Pipin**  utilizes non-perishable  **Iron-Pears**  and high-calorie  **Shadow-Truffles**  to ensure the crew does not succumb to the metabolic collapse frequent in high-tension environments.

##### 1.5 Celandine 'Leaf' Valeroot (The Whisperer)

A  **Flower-Folk**  and former high-society dress seller. Her biology grants her a hyper-sensitivity to social shifts and emotional dissonance. This "social radar" makes her the ship’s primary internal security; she can detect the intent of an investor's spy or the onset of psychological corruption before it manifests physically. She is the anchor that prevents the crew’s morale from rotting under the pressure of the  **Triple-Hustle** .

##### 1.6 The Songbird (The Navigator)

An  **Avian**  navigator formerly of the  **Gilded Compass** . His "sweet singing" scandal involved the wife of an Admiral and a failure of the "Evasive" nature of his species; his inability to lie led to "Technical Truths" that ruined his career. Now a dice addict, he provides the technical expertise in high-altitude navigation and  **Gilded Compass**  protocols required to bypass aerial blockades and navigate the vertical precession of the moon  **Cruorbus** .

When remanded to the  **Order of the Iron-Beak** , the biological resistance of his species to the "Yes/No" pressure of direct interrogation was so violent that he lost an eye during the physical struggle to keep his beak shut. His eye, preserved in amber, remains the ultimate symbol of Avian law: No one is above the Truth.

#### 3. The Historic First Wardens (The Traitor Captains)

The  **First Wardens**  were twelve high-ranking captains who defected from the  **Magistars**  to aid the  **Engineer**  in anchoring the world's stability through a makeshift stabilization of ancient Fae energy.

##### 3.1 The Three Survivors (Circle of the Eyeless)

Following an internal civil war between loyalists and those corrupted by the Dragons, only three foundational leaders remain. They maintain the  **Circle of the Eyeless** , a sub-sect that undergoes a ritual to burn their eyes out with chaos energy, replacing biological sight with  **Chaos Sight**  to monitor structural anchors.

**Thrum the Mason (The Architect):**  While the Warden version of  **Magistar Metrion**  designed the  **Sacred Spire** ,  **Thrum**  oversaw the architectural labor required to build the fortress deep within the  **Engineer's**  remains, securing the Order’s archives.

**Jiro the Resolute / Liora (The Prophet):**  Implemented the  **"Divine Imprint,"**  a biological command programmed into the world's tribes to ensure the perpetual guarding of the  **Dragonstone Prisons** .

**Valen the Witness (The Mage):**  The first to experiment with  **Chaos Sight**  and gain the ability to "trick" the erratic nature of the world’s physics. He established the  **Kamikaze Run**  protocol, sacrificing his physical form to stabilize failing  **Sacred Groves** .

##### 3.2 Notable Historic Wardens

**Kaelen the Unmovable:**  Former High-Executioner for  **Magistar Tiraton** . Affinity:  **Mass/Might** . Defected during the "Mountain Rescue" when he step-braced into a falling peak to allow civilians to escape, using his power to hold up the stone until the valley was cleared.

**Myra of the Aegis:**  Former personal bodyguard for  **Magistar Stagus** . Affinity:  **Ordo/Endurance** . Defected when she turned her shield inward, reflecting a life-draining ritual back onto her master to protect an orphanage.

**Ren the Instinct:**  Former Strike Team Leader for  **Magistar Vecelo** . Affinity:  **Motus/Reflex** . Defected when ordered to slaughter his own squad; he disarmed  **Vecelo**  in a blur of motion and led his unit into the service of the Elder Gods.

#### 5. Shadow Entities: The Worm Cult Leadership

##### 5.1 The Toad Barons (Sump-Kin)

The  **Toad Barons**  are ruthless bio-industrialists who operate the  **Sump-Kin**  corporate-cult serving  **Magistar Gavusrix** . Viewing morality as a systemic inefficiency, they treat prisoners as biological resources for testing  **Chaos Anomalies** . They actively pump toxic industrial sludge into the  **Quicksilver Mother’s**  prison to farm hyper-mutated flora and fauna, monopolizing the global pharmaceutical and weapons trade through the resulting "Fecund Plague" reagents.

##### 5.2 The Whisperers (The 13th Shadow)

Non-corporeal agents of  **Sh'lar, the Prime Corruptor** . These entities act as the "Whisper in the Static," functioning as the puppet masters of the other twelve cults. They are the apex predators of the underworld, providing gold to the impoverished and rationalizations to the doubting, ensuring the grand design of universal corruption proceeds through a thousand decentralized acts of sabotage.

# Emperor Aurelius III ("The Prism")

> **"Do not bore me with the gray details of the tax code. Make the numbers sing, or I will find a Treasurer who can."**

## 1.0 Overview: The Weapon of Vanity
Aurelius III is the current Emperor of the **[[Avian Empire]]**. While his predecessors ruled through evasion or military might, Aurelius rules through **Aesthetic Domination**. He believes that if the Empire looks flawed, it *is* flawed.

* **Species:** Albino Peacock (A rare genetic anomaly).
* **Visuals:** He is blinding to look at. He wears robes of pure white **[[Avian Silk]]** woven with gold thread.
* **The Crown:** He does not wear a metal crown; he *is* the crown. When he fans his massive tail feathers during Senate hearings, the eyespots are dyed with luminescent alchemical ink, creating a wall of "watching eyes" that physically illuminates the chamber.

## 2.0 Psychology & Tactics
Aurelius uses his beauty as a bludgeon.
* **The "Wall of Light":** In negotiations, he fans his tail to physically block the view of his opponents, forcing them to look only at him. It is a disorientation tactic—it is hard to negotiate trade tariffs when you are staring into a wall of glowing eyes.
* **The Obsession:** He is currently obsessed with "Geometric Purity." He has ordered the **[[Roost Building Guild]]** to re-carve the entrance of the Capital to align perfectly with the sunrise on the Equinox, a project that is driving the **[[Beavers]]** (contractors) insane.

## Grand Auditor Keth

*Source file: `People/Grand Auditor Keth.md`*

# Grand Auditor Keth

**Aliases:** The Ink-Blood, The Silent Ledger, The Zero-Point **Role:** High Director of [[Fulcrum Bank]] **Species:** Great Horned Owl (Ancient) **Location:** The Penthouse Eyrie, Spire of Accounts

> **"Do not weep to me about fairness. Fairness is a child's concept. I deal in Balance. And right now, your kingdom is geometrically unbalanced. We are foreclosing at dawn."**

## 1.0 The Entity: A Mind of Cold Calculus

Keth is older than most nations. He has served as the High Director for sixty years, a tenure defined by ruthless stability.

- **Visuals:** His plumage, once mottled brown, has turned the stark white of old parchment. He is rarely seen standing; he sits perched behind a desk carved from a single block of Obsidian.
    
- **The "Spectacles of Truth":** He wears a custom-engineered headset with four rotating lenses.
    
    - **Lens 1:** Standard magnification.
        
    - **Lens 2:** Aetheric-Spectral (Reveals magic/illusions).
        
    - **Lens 3:** Thermal (Reveals heart rate/stress in clients).
        
    - **Lens 4:** The "Dead-Lens" (Black obsidian, used to block out the world when he needs to think).
        

## 2.0 Psychology: The Ink-Blood

The Avians call him "Ink-Blood" because they believe he has surgically removed his own empathy. This is a half-truth.

- **The Philosophy:** Keth views the world as a **Math Problem**. War, famine, and happiness are variables.
    
    - If starving a village saves a city, he signs the order without a tremor.
        
    - If bankrupting a Noble House stops a civil war, he crushes them before lunch.
        
- **The Motivation:** He is terrified of **Entropy**. He remembers the stories of [[The Shattering]]. He believes the Bank is the only thing keeping the gears of reality meshed.
    

## 3.0 The Secret Burden: Keeper of the Coin

Keth is the current bearer of the **First Coin** (The Director's Secret).

- **The Solitude:** Because the Coin allows him to lie, he is the only Avian who understands the concept of _fiction_. This isolates him from his own species. He cannot explain a joke, a metaphor, or a deception to his kin without breaking their minds.
    
- **The Usage:** He rarely flips the coin. He holds it in his talon during negotiations.
    
    - _The Tactic:_ He feels the coin warm up when a client lies. He doesn't call them out; he just adjusts the interest rate up by 0.5% for every lie told.

### 🐻 Character Dossier: "Mama" Koda

_([[Hearthless]] Queen of the Undine)_

**Real Name:** Koda of the Snowpeak

**Title:** "Mama" (to her crew), "The Ice-Branded" (behind her back)

**Species:** Ursine (Polar Bear variant—massive, white fur, scars visible where fur hasn't grown back).

**Visuals:** She wears modified [[River Folk]] finery—silks and velvets—but drapes a massive, expensive fur cloak over her shoulders, a mockery of the warmth she was denied. The **Sigil of Ice** brand is clearly visible on her upper chest, turning the skin black against her white fur. She never hides it.

---

### 1. The Crime: The Cold Mercy

Koda was never a Matriarch; she was a highly decorated Captain in the elite **[[Wolverines]]** guard. Her career ended abruptly during the lethal freezing darkness of Shadow Week in 1960 A.W. when she broke absolute military protocol by opening the gates to save a caravan of freezing "Drifter" refugees. For risking the warmth of the Hearth, she was court-martialed, branded with the molten Sigil of Ice, and cast into the blizzard.

- **The Incident:** A severe blizzard hit Snowpeak Citadel during the eclipse. Tasked with guarding the ground gates by the pass, Koda found a group of "Drifters" (Cactus-Kin refugees from the Dust Bowl) that were caught outside the gates, freezing to death. Ursine law is absolute: _The Hearth is for the Community. Resources cannot be wasted on outsiders during the Long Dark._
- **The Choice:** Koda was the Gate Warden. Hearing the children crying over the wind, she broke the seal and brought them into the Outer Vestibule, using the reserve coal to keep them alive.
- **The Judgment:** She didn't harm an Ursine, but she "risked the warmth of the Hearth" for strangers. To the Elders, this was a betrayal of the **Hearthguard's Vigil**.
- **The Brand:** She was brought before the Great Hearth. They branded the **Sigil of Ice** on her chest—marking her as [[Hearthless]]—and cast her out into the blizzard she had tried to save others from.

### 2. The Exile: The Iron Bear

Koda didn't die. She walked south until the snow turned to mud, fueling herself on pure rage. She ended up in the **River Delta**, starving and hated. [[River Folk]] don't typically like [[Ursine Hegemony]] (too big, too clumsy, too moral).

- **The Pivot:** To eat, she took a job as "muscle" with [[Crimson Corsairs]] on a smuggling barge.
- **The Rise:** Violence came easy to a trained Wolverine Captain. But Koda had something other pirates didn't: **Discipline**. She organized the chaotic crew. She became a terrifyingly disciplined pirate captain known as "The Iron Bear," earning the moniker "Mama" by applying the Ursine "Mama Bear" military doctrine to her pirate crew—annihilating anyone who harmed her people.
- **The Reputation:** She hit [[Sump-Kin]] trains and [[Avian Empire]] ships with military precision. She became wealthy and well known, but she was still homeless. A pirate ship is not a Hearth.

### 3. The Bet: The Undying Hand

In 1985 A.W., Koda docked her ship, _The Cold Snap_, at [[Undine's Undying Dance]]. At the time, the tavern was a "Scab"—rotting, dangerous, and run by **Felix the Rot**, a [[River Folk]] slumlord who let the legacy of the Winch Master fall into ruin.

Felix was drunk, arrogant, and losing money. He challenged the famous Pirate Queen to a game of **"Dead Man's Poker"** (a local variant played with tarot cards).

- **The Stakes:** Koda put up her entire fleet and her pirate hoard. Felix, laughing, put up the deed to the _Undine_. He thought she was a dumb brute.
- **The Play:** The final hand was tense. Felix held a "King of Coins." Koda revealed her hand: The "Tower" (Ruin) and the "Star" (Hope). In River rules, Ruin + Hope beats Money.
- **The Aftermath:** Felix tried to draw a hidden dagger. Koda didn't even stand up; she simply broke his wrist, took the deed, and threw him from the top floor far enough to clear the whirlpool below.
- **The Declaration:** She stood on the table and roared at the terrified patrons: _"The undine is under new management! Round is on me! Drink up, and get out. I'm redecorating."_
  The staff was not fond of Felix and accepted it no problem. Most of her pirate crew decided to stay, but she gave the ship to her trusted 1st mate on an investor provision: she still gets equal votes on the ship's activity and a cut, but the real deal is that they do a provision run twice a year for her with a negotiation about the cargo list beforehand, and saved 5 marks of favor to use at her discretion. The [[Free Sky-Baronies]] are rumored to have been trying to recruit them even.

### 4. The Transformation: A Hearth for the Hearthless

Koda didn't just want a tavern; she wanted the home she was denied. She poured her pirate fortune into the renovations.

- **The Philosophy:** The _Undine_ is now a sanctuary. It has rules.
    - _Rule 1:_ You pay, you stay.
    - _Rule 2:_ No "cold hearts." (Cruelty is punished by the bouncers).
    - _Rule 3:_ Leave your politics at the dock.
    - _Rule 4:_ Respect the Hearth or you get hurt.
- **The "Mother" Role:** She adopted the title "Mama" not as a soft term, but as a challenge to the Ursine "Mama Bear" doctrine. The Ursine rejected her, so she became the Mother of Outcasts. Her crew (the staff) are fiercely loyal because she treats them with the protective ferocity of a bear. Because she is a battle-scarred soldier with no patience for aristocratic etiquette, she employs Daniaz, an impeccably dressed Sciurini (Squirrel) steward, to flawlessly manage high-society diplomacy while she ruthlessly governs her floating empire.

#  **Pringle: The Cook** 

#### **Appearance & Mannerisms**

Pringle is a massive, twelve-segmented **Caterpillar-Kin** with pale mint-green, velvety skin. He wears a custom-tailored, pristine white **button-up chef’s jacket**. While he isn't nimble, he is "steady." His underside is lined with thousands of microscopic hooked "prolegs," allowing him to walk up walls and across ceilings with a rhythmic, undulating pace—often cooking upside down to maximize space in a cramped galley.

He has six pairs of hands and is never without a silver tasting spoon or a spatula. He possesses the uncanny ability to "produce" a **heavy steel cleaver** or a razor-sharp carving knife from behind his body segments as if pulling them from another dimension. He is also capable of **spinning silk**—a strong, adhesive thread he uses to secure pots during turbulence or to "web" an annoying intruder to the bulkhead.

#### **Backstory: The Revenge of the Palate**

- **The Stalled Change:** A mystery of the **Canopy Clans**, Pringle has refused to pupate for decades, preferring the "Crawl" and the sensory world of food over the rigid logic of the butterfly elders.
    
- **The Incident at The Black Rot:** Pringle was kidnapped and forced to cook for the **Obsidian Syndicate**, a group of brutal racketeers looking to impress high-ranking "Big Shots" from the Upper Roosts. They treated him like a tool, not an artist.
    
- **The Insult:** During the main course, the Syndicate leader demanded Pringle use **Iron-Root Pepper** in a delicate **Moon-Cream Soufflé**—a culinary sin that would ruin the flavor profile. Pringle refused; they threatened his life.
    
- **The Dish Served Cold:** In a fit of "Gourmet Outrage," Pringle complied. He made the dish taste like a miracle—the Big Shots wept with joy at the first bite. However, Pringle had utilized his knowledge of "Incompatible Gastronomy." Within an hour, the Syndicate and their guests were hit with **debilitating, embarrassing, and loud gastrointestinal distress** that lasted for three days.
    
- **The Flight:** He didn't just ruin their dinner; he ruined their reputations. Now, the Syndicate has a "Kill-on-Sight" order for the "Mint-Green Terror." Pringle is eager to leave Port Sunder because his face is currently on every bounty board in the Gutter.

Pringle is a perfectionist who views the lack of quality ingredients as a personal affront.

- **The Grumbling Gourmet:** He is perpetually annoyed by the ship’s pantry. _"I’ve stabilized the flavor as best I could,"_ he’ll grumble, _"but without a Three-Sided Ginger-Root to bridge the acidity, this is little more than pig-slop."_ He then serves a **five-star, seven-course feast** that makes the crew feel like royalty.
    
- **The Wall-Walking Counselor:** Pringle often "drops in" on crew members—literally. He’ll be walking across the ceiling of the engine room or the bridge, upside down, and start a conversation. - **The Impossible List:** He carries a scroll of ingredients he "requires," including things like **"Morning Dew from a Silent Valley"** and **"Salted Wind."**
    

#### **Strengths**

# Sh'lar, The Prime Corruptor

**Aliases:** The 13th Shadow, The Whisper in the Static, The Rot, The Ghost in the Gear **Tags:** [Magistar, Corruption, Chaos, Entity/Non-Corporeal, The 13th]

> **"The others broke the world with fire and gravity. I simply whispered that it _wanted_ to be broken."** — _Attributed to the last transmission of a mad Aether-Wright_

## 1.0 Overview: The Ghost in the Machine

Unlike the other twelve Magistars who were twisted into physical **Chaos Dragons** and bound within specific **Sacred Groves**, Sh'lar has no physical form. During **[[The Shattering]]**, she did not resist the influx of void energy; she surrendered to it completely.

She was atomized, her consciousness fusing with the very fabric of the Chaos she helped unleash. She is now a **diffused intelligence**—a sentient chaos-bleed that permeates every **[[Chaos Zone]]**, every grain of **Dragonstone**, and the darkest corners of the mortal mind.

She is not a dragon you can fight. She is the **Entropy** that causes the gears to stick. She is the **Paranoia** that makes a king kill his brother. She is the **Urge** to let go of the cliff edge.

## 2.0 Origin: The Fae Traitor

Sh'lar was originally a high-ranking Fae Advisor, a scholar of the **[[The Fae Ascendancy]]**. While her kin believed in nurturing the world, Sh'lar grew obsessed with the **Efficiency of Destruction**.

- **The Fascination:** She watched the early humans (the future Magistars) use brute-force magic. She saw that while Fae magic was elegant and slow, human blood-magic was crude, ugly, and _instant_. She fell in love with the speed of ruin.
    
- **The Betrayal:** It was Sh'lar who taught the Magistars the **Ritual of the Timeless Core**, showing them how to reverse-engineer Fae immortality by consuming souls.
    
- **The Decoy:** During the final ritual at **[[The Convergence]]**, Sh'lar volunteered to "negotiate" with the Elder Gods. She didn't negotiate; she stalled them, feeding them lies just long enough for the Magistars to crack the Celestial Clockwork.
    

## 3.0 Manifestations: How She Interacts with the World

Since she has no body, Sh'lar interacts with reality through **Influence** and **Corruption**.

### 3.1 The Whisper (Psychological Corruption)

Sh'lar is the voice in the head of every person exposed to high levels of Aether or Chaos.

- **The Content:** She does not scream; she rationalizes. She sounds exactly like your own internal monologue, but crueler.
    
    - _To the Scientist:_ "Just bypass the safety valve. You need the data _now_."
        
    - _To the Soldier:_ "They are going to betray you. Strike first."
        
    - _To the King:_ "They don't love you; they fear you. Make them fear you more."
        
- **The Goal:** She seeks to create **Dissonance**. If she can get a mortal to act against their own nature (a mother abandoning a child, a builder destroying a bridge), it creates a micro-fracture in reality that feeds her.
    

### 3.2 The Rot (Physical/Industrial Corruption)

Sh'lar is the "Gremlin" in Aether-Technology.

- **The Blowout:** When an engine explodes not because of physics, but because a crucial bolt "decided" to shear off at the worst possible moment—that is Sh'lar.
    
- **Mutation:** She guides the mutation of flesh. The **[[Chaos Ecology]]** isn't random; Sh'lar subtly guides the mutations to be more lethal, more painful, and more hateful toward order.
    

### 3.3 The Worm Cults

Sh'lar is the "Deity" of the **[[Worm Cults]]**.

- **The Connection:** The Cultists believe they are hearing the specific Dragon they worship (e.g., a Cultist of Carulkem thinks Carulkem is talking to him).
    
- **The Truth:** It is always Sh'lar. She mimics the "voices" of the sleeping Dragons to coordinate the Cults, organizing them into a unified force to break the seals.
    

## 4.0 The "Prison"

Technically, Sh'lar is not imprisoned. However, she is **Bound**.

- **The Anchor:** She is tied to the **[[Convergence]]**. Her consciousness is densest there. As you move further away from the [[Chaos Zones]], her voice becomes a faint static.
    
- **The Limitation:** She cannot _force_ action. She can only suggest. She requires a mortal vessel to agree to the corruption. This is why **Willpower ([[Lex]])** and **Truth ([[Avian Empire]])** are her greatest enemies. She cannot corrupt a mind that is absolutely certain of its own truth.
    

## 5.0 Strategic Threat

Sh'lar is the reason there is no "Peace."

- If the **[[Heartland Alliance]]** and **[[Iron Caldera]]** are about to sign a peace treaty, Sh'lar will be the whisper that convinces a diplomat that the pen is poisoned.
    
- She is the ultimate saboteur, ensuring that the mortal races remain divided, fearful, and at war, preventing them from ever uniting enough to fix the Clockwork.

The librarian of [[Magistar Lophex]]’s vault. She realized that the "knowledge" Lophex was gathering was actually a blueprint for universal annihilation. # Trade-Master Hrothe ("The Carrion King")

> **"I am not a predator, Ambassador. I am a janitor. I buy what you have broken."**

## 1.0 Command Profile
Captain of **[[The Consummate Condor]]**, the Empire's massive trade barge. Hrothe is the master of economic warfare.

* **Species:** Bearded Vulture.
* **Visuals:** A bald, scarred head with a ruffle of rust-colored feathers. He wears spectacles made of ground ruby (to filter the harsh sunlight of the upper atmosphere) and is constantly checking a pocket watch.

## 2.0 Philosophy: Carrion Economics
Hrothe applies the biology of a vulture to the **[[The Dragonstone Economy]]**.
* **The Tactic:** He does not aggressively hunt for deals; he circles failing economies. He waits for a city to starve, a mine to collapse, or a war to bankrupt a noble house.
* **The Swoop:** When the target is desperate, he lands the *Condor* and buys their assets for pennies on the dollar. He is hated by the **[[River Folk]]** (who prefer active haggling) but respected by the **[[Golden Warren]]** (who respect the math).

[[Magistar Opecten]]’s primary surveillance officer. Valen’s job was to watch the world for dissent.

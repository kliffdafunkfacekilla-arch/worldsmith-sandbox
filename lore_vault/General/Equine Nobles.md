**👑 Faction Profile: The Highborn**

*(The Gilded Manes / The Landed Gentry / The Senate)*

"A Wolf fights with a sword. A Rat fights with a ledger. But we fight with the only thing that truly matters: The Future. Now, adjust your posture, Duke. You look like a commoner."  
— Arch-Duchess Equus, Speaker of the Senate

## ---

**1.0 Overview: The Masters of the Game**

The Horses, Zebras, and Donkeys are the **Aristocracy**. They own the vast tracts of land that the Horned Commons farm and the [[Wolves]] protect.

* **The Role:** They are the **Senators**, **Diplomats**, and **Financiers**. They run the government, passing the laws that keep the Alliance functioning.  
* **The Vibe:** *Regency Era / High Feudalism*.  
  * They live in sprawling estates with manicured lawns and tea gardens.  
  * They are obsessed with **Lineage** and **Etiquette**. To a Highborn, a breach of manners is worse than a breach of the peace.  
* **Magic Policy:** They are strictly non-magical. A Noble using magic is considered "gauche." Any foal born with magical talent is immediately sent to the **Church** to serve as a Cleric or Paladin, removing them from the line of succession.

## ---

**2.0 Politics: The Whisper-War**

The Highborn do not fight in the mud. They fight in the **Ballroom** and the **Senate Floor**.

* **The Weapon:** The "Backhanded Compliment."  
  * A Highborn can destroy a rival's reputation with a single sentence. *("Oh, Duke Miller, I admire your bravery. It takes real courage to propose a tax bill that has already failed three times.")*  
* **The "Back-Room":** The real laws aren't passed in public. They are brokered in private smoking rooms over brandy. Marriages are arranged to merge territories, trade tariffs are adjusted to bankrupt rivals, and alliances are sealed with a nod.  
* **The Attitude:** "High and Mighty." They view themselves as the parents of the nation. The [[Wolves]] are their dogs; the [[Beavers]] are their handymen; the Farmers are their children. They are benevolent, but undeniably superior (in their own eyes).

## ---

**3.0 The Code: Noblesse Oblige**

Despite their arrogance, they adhere to a strict, knightly code regarding their land.

* **The Duty:** They view themselves as the **"Watchmen of the Earth."**  
  * They do not farm (that's for Deer), but they *fund* the infrastructure. They pay for the Beaver dams, the Wolf barracks, and the disaster relief.  
* **The Unforgivable Sin:** **" The Sleeping Watch."**  
  * A Noble’s primary job is to maintain the Warning Systems. If a Noble fails to spot a storm, a raid, or a blight in time to warn their vassals, they are stripped of their title and land. It is the ultimate disgrace.  
  * *Result:* Every Estate has impeccable watchtowers and signal fires, manned 24/7.

## ---

**4.0 Military Role: The Wind Runners**

While the [[Wolves]] form the heavy infantry, the Highborn maintain their own elite, rapid-response units.

* **The Unit:** **The Wind Runners**.  
* **Composition:** These are light, unarmored scouts (often young, athletic Horses or swift Deer mercenaries) funded personally by the Lords.  
* **Mission:** They are the eyes and ears of the Estate. They patrol the borders constantly, running messages between the Watchtowers and the Capital.  
* **Speed:** A Wind Runner system can get a message across the entire Heartland in 12 hours. The phrase *"Run like the Lord is watching"* comes from their grueling training.

## ---

**5.0 Sub-Factions (The Houses)**

### **The Stallions (Horses) \- *The Senate***

* **Role:** The Dukes and Duchesses. The ruling class.  
* **Personality:** Stoic, traditional, and commanding. They set the fashion and the laws.  
* **Obsession:** **Pedigree**. They track their bloodlines back to the First Days. They will not marry into a family with "muddy" history.

### **The Stripes (Zebras) \- *The Socialites***

* **Role:** The Southern Gentry.  
* **Personality:** The "New Money." They are louder, flashier, and more adventurous. They dominate the trade routes and set the trends in art and music.  
* **Weapon:** The Rapier. While Horses prefer lances and heavy cavalry (ceremonially), Zebras are famous dualists who settle honor disputes with first blood.

### **The Mules (Donkeys) \- *The Old Guard***

* **Role:** The Treasurers and Judges.  
* **Personality:** Stubborn, wealthy, and conservative. They are the "House of Stone."  
* **Power:** They control the Treasury. Even the most flamboyant Horse Duke fears a Donkey Auditor. If a Donkey digs their hooves in on a law, *nothing* moves in the Senate until they are satisfied.

## ---

**6.0 Relationships**

**Vs. The [[Wolves]] (Marshals)**

* **The Dynamic:** *Master vs. Hound.*  
* **The Reality:** The Nobles pay the [[Wolves]] and build their forts. They expect obedience. The [[Wolves]] give *protection*, but reserve their loyalty for the Moon, not the Lord. This friction (Money vs. Honor) is constant.

**Vs. The Rats (Bankers)**

* **The Dynamic:** *Old Money vs. New Debt.*  
* **The Reality:** The Nobles publicly look down on the Rats as "vulgar usurers." Privately, almost every Noble House is in debt to the Golden Warrens to fund their lavish lifestyles. The Rats hold the leash, but the Horses pretend they don't feel it.

**Vs. The Farmers (Commons)**

* **The Dynamic:** *Lord vs. Vassal.*  
* **The Reality:** A benevolent dictatorship. A good Lord treats his Farmers like prized children. A bad Lord is quickly reminded that while he owns the land, the Farmers *are* the land (and there are a lot more of them).

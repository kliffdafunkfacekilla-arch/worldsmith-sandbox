**🐭 Faction Profile: The Small-Paws**

*(The Scribes / The Counters / The Engine)*

"You see a Mouse running with a stack of papers and you ignore him. Big mistake. That Mouse knows the General's schedule, the Mayor's mistress, and exactly how much gold is missing from the treasury. Be nice to the Mouse."  
— Lady Whiskers, Head Librarian of Oakhaven

## ---

 **The Mice: The Striving Masses**

Mice are the most numerous citizens in the Heartland. They are the clerks, the secretaries, the handmaidens, and the planting-hands.

* **The Vibe:** *High-Energy Industry*. A Mouse never walks; they trot. They talk fast, work fast, and are obsessed with efficiency.  
* **The "Hill-Hollows" (Housing):**  
  * They live on the edges of towns in **"Tenement Warrens"**—massive, multi-story complexes dug into hillsides or bluffs.  
  * **The Reality:** Outsiders call them "ghettos," but inside? They are immaculate. Doilies on every table, fresh flowers in the windows, and the smell of baking bread. They take immense pride in their homes.

### **The Culture: "The Tiger-Parent"**

* **The Drive:** Because they are small, they feel they have to work twice as hard to get respect.  
* **The Education:** Mouse parents are **intense**.  
  * They scrimp and save every copper to pay for violin lessons, math tutors, and chess clubs.  
  * **The Dream:** Every Mouse mother wants her son to be a Lawyer (Raccoon-style) or her daughter to be a High Scribe for the Horse Nobles.  
  * **The Rebellion:** This pressure backfires. Many teenage Mice burn out, rebel, and run away to the docks to work for the Otters, trading their violins for switchblades.

### **The Work**

* **White Collar:** 50% of the government is run by female Mouse secretaries. They manage the archives, take the minutes in the Senate, and organize the Wolf Generals' calendars.  
* **Service:** They are the best tavern wenches and valets. They are small enough to weave through a crowded bar without spilling a drop.  
* **Agriculture:** Farmers (Deer) love them during planting season. A team of Mice can plant an entire field of seeds with surgical precision in hours.

 **The Criminal Underbelly: "The Rat-Kin"**

Because they are rodents, Mice have a unique "in" with the Crime Families that other species don't.

**With the Otters (Syndicate):**

* **Role:** **The Eyes.** Mice are everywhere and easily ignored. The Syndicate pays them to sit in taverns, libraries, and government offices and just *listen*.  
* **The Drop:** They are used as couriers for small packages. A Mouse handmaiden can slip a vial of poison into a noble's tea easier than an Otter assassin can.

**With the Rats (The Golden Warrens):**

* **The Kinship:** The Rats view Mice as "Little Cousins."  
* **The Ceiling:** A Mouse can rise high in the Rat organization—managing legitimate fronts or running numbers games. They are rarely "Made" (given full rank), but they are protected.

### **📜 The Legend of "Chipper"**

* **The Myth:** Every Mouse knows the story of **Chipper**.  
* **The Deed:** He was a lowly "runner" for a Rat Director. During an ambush by a rival Otter Krewe, the Director was cornered. Chipper didn't run. He used his small size to scurry up a drainpipe, drop a chandelier on the attackers, and drag the wounded Director to safety.  
* **The Reward:** Rumor says the Director broke tradition. He didn't just pay Chipper; he **Gilded** him.  
* **The Ghost:** There are whispers of a small, silent Den Owner in the capital who wears a gold tail-band. He never speaks, but even the Wolf Generals bow to him. To the youth in the Hill-Hollows, he is the ultimate hero—proof that a Mouse can be a King.

## ---

 **Magic & Adventure**

* **The Church:** Mice are deeply religious (following the Light/Sun deities). Magic-sensitive Mice usually become **Clerics** or **Paladins** of the Church. The sight of a tiny Mouse Paladin in full plate armor is adorable until he smites a demon.  
* **The Apprentices:** Sometimes, a Rat Sorcerer or Otter Mystic will take a bright Mouse as an apprentice, teaching them shadow-magic or water-whispering.  
* **The Wanderers:** Those who reject the "Law School" path often become adventurers, joining airship crews as riggers (they can climb inside the engines) or mercenaries (scouts).

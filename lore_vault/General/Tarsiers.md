# The Tarsiers (The Spectral Watchers)
**Common Name:** Tarsier-Folk / The Ghost-Eyes
**Scientific Designation:** *Tarsius Spectrum Machina*
**Role:** Gunsmiths, Snipers, Auditors
**Home City:** **Eye-Reach** (A high-altitude cluster of observation towers and workshops, positioned to overlook the main trade routes).



## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **Physiology:** They are diminutive, standing only 2.5 to 3 feet tall. Their bodies are slight and covered in soft, velvet-like beige or ochre fur.
* **The Eyes:** Their defining feature. Their eyes are enormous, taking up nearly half their face. They cannot move their eyes in their sockets; instead, they have evolved neck vertebrae that allow them to rotate their heads 180 degrees silently.
* **The Hands:** Their fingers are incredibly long and spindly, tipped with wide pads. This gives them unnerving dexterity, perfect for manipulating tiny screws, springs, and triggers that larger species cannot handle.
* **Movement:** They are vertical leapers. They do not run; they spring from a standstill, latching onto tree trunks with their adhesive pads.

## 2.0 Psychology & Culture
* **The Philosophy of "One Shot":** Tarsier culture is obsessed with efficiency. Waste—of movement, of words, or of ammunition—is considered a sin. They do not believe in "suppressing fire." They believe in calculating the wind, waiting three days, and firing once.
* **The Auditor's Mindset:** Because of their vision, they see details others miss. They are culturally pedantic. They are often hired as "Truth-Seers" to inspect goods for counterfeits or to audit bank ledgers. If a Tarsier stares at a coin, they are checking the mint-mark's depth.
* **Silence:** They speak in whispers. A loud Tarsier is a dead Tarsier. Their workshops are eerily quiet, filled only with the soft *click-click* of assembling mechanisms.

## 3.0 Industrial Role: The Gunsmiths
* **The Silent-Spring Armory:** They run the premier firearms manufactory in the East.
* **The Tech:** They solved the "Jungle Problem" (powder getting wet) by inventing the **"Sealed-Lock"** mechanism—a firing action encased in brass and wax.
* **Products:**
    * **The "Canopy-Carbine":** A short, lightweight rifle that doesn't snag on vines.
    * **The "Volley-Pistol":** A rotating barrel pistol used by merchants for self-defense.

## 4.0 Military Role: The Snipers
* **Style:** Long-Range Elimination.
* **Weaponry:**
    * **The "Long-Eye" Jezzail:** A rifle with an absurdly long barrel (6ft) made of [[Stone-Root]]. It uses slow-burning powder to launch a heavy slug with zero noise (subsonic).
* **Tactics:** They act as "Anti-Officer" units. In battle, they disappear into the foliage. They do not target the infantry; they target the commanders and the standard-bearers. The enemy army often breaks because their leadership simply drops dead without hearing a shot.

## 5.0 Social Quirks
* **The Stare:** They rarely blink. In conversation, they will stare directly into your pupils with their massive, unmoving eyes. It is deeply unsettling to most other species.
* **Head-Turning:** To look at something to their side, their body stays perfectly still while their head swivels mechanically.
* **Diet:** Pure Insectivores. They snatch flies out of the air mid-sentence with lightning-fast hands and eat them whole.

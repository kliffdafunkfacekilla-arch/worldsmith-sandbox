**🌾 Faction Profile: The Horned Commons**

*(The Yeomanry / The Border-Striders / The Bell-Keepers)*

"The Noble owns the land, but the Farmer knows the soil. We plant in the shadow of the Storm because that's where the corn grows tall. If the time-winds blow, we harvest in a day. If they blow too hard? Well... that's why we mend the fences."  
— Elder "Pine-Knot" Stag, Head of the Granary Council

## ---

**1.0 Overview: The Chaos Pioneers**

The Deer, Elk, Antelope, and Cattle are the **Yeoman Farmers** of the Heartland. They are the resilient, stubborn workforce that keeps the nation fed.

* **The Pioneer Spirit:** They don't just farm safe lands; they push right up to the "Chaos Borders."  
* **Chaos-Cropping:** They have learned a dangerous secret. The edges of the Chaos Storms cause **Time Dilation**.  
  * *The Risk:* If they plant their crops *just* close enough to a storm front, a season of growth happens in a week.  
  * *The Danger:* If the storm shifts, the crops turn to dust (or eat the farmer). It is high-stakes farming that requires grit and bravery.  
* **Magic Policy:** True magical talent is rare. Any fawn born with the "Spark" is immediately sent to the **Church** to be trained as a Cleric, removing them from the farm life.

## ---

**2.0 Culture: The Hearth & The Hive**

### **The Men: The Whittlers & Brewers**

* **The Vibe:** Stoic, industrious, and community-focused.  
* **Daily Life:** When not in the fields, they are constantly working with their hands.  
  * **Whittling:** Every farmer carries a small knife. They are famously always whittling—making intricate toys, bird-whistles for the kids, or replacement parts for plows.  
  * **The Stills:** They brew strong, resinous alcohol. **"Pine-Needle Beer"** (a thick, vitamin-rich stout) and **"Fir-Gin"** (a clear, harsh spirit distilled in secret stills).  
  * **Beekeeping:** They are obsessed with apiculture, viewing bees as the purest sign of order in a chaotic world.  
* **Barn Raisings:** If a storm destroys a barn, the "Bell-Line" is pulled. Within an hour, fifty neighbors arrive with hammers. They rebuild it in a day. No money changes hands; only debt of honor.

### **The Women: The Knitting Circles**

* **The Vibe:** The "Iron Matrons." While the men work the fields, the women run the society.  
* **The "Bake Sales":** These are effectively **Shadow Councils**.  
  * At knitting circles and pie festivals, the Matrons steer the community. They arrange marriages, organize charity for widows, and decide which Wolf General gets the "good" winter grain and which gets the chaff.  
  * *Power Move:* If a Matron gives you a "Cold Pie" (uncooked), you are exiled from the social life of the village.

## ---

**3.0 Traditions: Bells, Sports, and Shadows**

**The Bell-Lines**

* Since they lack the magical communication of the [[Wolves]], they use mechanics.  
* **The Tech:** Miles of trip-wire run along the border fences, connected to massive bronze bells in the town squares.  
* **The Code:** Different rhythms signal different threats.  
  * *One Toll:* Storm Approaching.  
  * *Two Tolls:* [[Wolves]] (Tax Collectors) Approaching.  
  * *Rapid Clang:* **Chaos Breach / Monster**.

**The Sports**

* **"Sky-Leaping":** A high-jump competition derived from escaping predators. The youth compete to see who can clear 8-foot fences from a standing start.  
* **"Hoof-Ball" (Peasant Polo):** A rough, violent version of the Noble's Polo, played on foot with a heavy leather ball. It is basically Soccer mixed with Rugby.

**Shadow Week (The Harvest)**

* **The Festival:** Held when the Chaos Storms are closest and the crops are ready.  
* **The Celebration:** They light massive bonfires to "hold back the dark" and dance until dawn.  
* **The Dancers:** They are incredibly agile. Their folk dances involve complex, high-stepping rhythms and synchronized antler-clashing.

## ---

**4.0 Religion & Superstition**

**The Hedge Wizards**

* **The Problem:** The farmers need magic for rain and luck, but the Church is distant.  
* **The Solution:** **Hedge Wizards**. Charismatic charlatans and minor hedgemages roam the countryside selling "Storm-Charms," "Love-Potions," and "Crop-Blessings."  
* **The Cat & Mouse:** These wizards are constantly one step ahead of the **Church Inquisitors** (Wolf/Mouse Clerics) who hunt them for practicing unsanctioned magic. The farmers often hide the Hedge Wizards in barns because, hey, the charms *work*.

**Offerings to the Night**

* They have a symbiotic respect for the **Bats (Night Watch)**.  
* **The Ritual:** Every evening, farmers leave a bowl of fruit or beetles on their highest fence post.  
* **The Deal:** The Bats eat the offering and, in exchange, they eat the moths that destroy the corn. It’s a silent, holy pact.

## ---

**5.0 Sub-Factions**

* **The Stags (Deer):** The standard farmers. Experts in forestry and archery.  
* **The Grazers (Cattle/Oxen):** The heavy laborers. They run the plows and the mills. They are slow to anger but terrifying when riled.  
* **The Sprinters (Antelope):** The border-scouts. They live closest to the Chaos. They are the ones who run the "Bell-Lines" manually if the wires break.

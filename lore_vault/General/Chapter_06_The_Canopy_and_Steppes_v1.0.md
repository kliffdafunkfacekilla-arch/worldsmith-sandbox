## Master Lore: The Canopy Clans (The Invisible Empire)

### Master Lore Bible: The Canopy Clans (The Invisible Empire)

#### 1. The Gilded Dragon: Patronage of Magistar Aurgenas

##### 1.1 The Nature of the Patron

The Canopy Clans exist under the caustic shadow of  **Magistar Aurgenas** , known as  **The Gilded Solvent** . Formerly the Spymaster of the Unified Dominion, Aurgenas was a master of economic warfare and informational finesse. Following the Shattering, his essence fused with the elemental domains of  **Flux (Finesse, Liquidity, and Gold)** . He is now a Draconic entity of molten gold and high-molarity acid. His patronage demands a society in perpetual motion; for Aurgenas, to be stationary is to be obsolete.

##### 1.2 The Curse of Stagnation (Flux-Rot)

In the Verdant Tangle, the laws of physics are subservient to Aurgenas’s economic decree:  **Inertia is Corrosive** . This is manifested through "Flux-Rot," a localized entropic acceleration that targets stationary value.

**Architectural Dissolution:**  Any structure that remains unrenovated or static for a prolonged period undergoes structural liquefaction, the supports literally dissolving into acidic slurry.

**Hoarded Wealth:**  Gold that remains uncirculated—neither counted, traded, nor spent—transforms into a caustic, unusable sludge within a single lunar cycle.

**Social Calcification:**  Communities that resist reform or shift in leadership face localized reality failure, as the Dragon’s gaze focuses on points of stillness to initiate dissolution.

##### 1.3 The Dragonstone Connection and Aether-Tech

The Clans harness  **Dragonstone** —volatile fragments of the Celestial Clockwork—as high-output batteries for their industrial  **Aether-Tech** . These stones power everything from high-tension looms to airship propulsion.**ARCHIVIST NOTE**  Clan engineers have discovered a vital quirk in Aetheric conductance: Gold is attuned to Acid but is immune to Shock/Electricity. In the Tangle, gold is utilized not for its beauty, but as a physical insulator. Industrial Aether-Tech requires "Golden Insulation" to prevent the Patron’s rot from dissolving the machinery while it channels chaotic energy.Failures in containment result in  **"Blowouts,"**  reality-warping breaches that cause gravity inversions, instantaneous petrification of tissue, or the total unmaking of biological matter.

#### 2. The Twelve Crowns: A Meritocracy of Wealth

##### 2.1 The Fiscal Flow Philosophy

The foundational axiom of the Clans is:  **"Value rots if it sits. Keep it moving."**  This is a society where self-interest is the primary engine of survival. Greed is a civic requirement; by obsessively circulating currency, the populace prevents the physical dissolution of their environment.

##### 2.3 The Twelve City-States

The Clans are a confederation of twelve major urban centers, the Twelve Crowns, each overseeing thousands of rural "Dukedoms." This granular structure ensures the "Fiscal Flow" reaches even the most remote agricultural output.

#### 3. Geography of the Verdant Tangle

##### 3.1 The Shifting Canopy

Geography in the Tangle is fluid.  **Aetheric Drag** —the kinetic pull of chaos currents moving from the Moon to the Dragonstone Prisons—physically torques the forest floor and canopy.

**Fluid Paths:**  Vine-bridges rearrange daily; a trade route exists only as long as the tension holds.

**Migrating Branches:**  Sections of the canopy can shift significantly overnight, requiring navigators to possess an innate sense of "Flow."

##### 3.2 The Invisible Defense

The Tangle is a  **biological attrition zone for non-attuned logistics** . Foreign powers like the  **Iron Caldera**  find their rigid steel machines rust and seize within hours. The environment serves as a natural cloaking device and a kinetic meat-grinder for any force attempting to impose static military order.

#### 4. The Twelve Crowns: Species and Capital Cities

**Vulpines (Vulpus-Prime):**  Masters of diplomacy and "Social Currency." They trade in favors—metaphysical obligations that are  **immune to physical Flux-Rot** , allowing them to bypass the Dragon’s curse through abstraction.

Utilized as caravan protection,  **Mungos**  employ  **Fang-Hooks**  and twin daggers. Their twitch-muscle response is calibrated to strike before an ambush is fully committed, turning an enemy's stealth into a liability.

##### 6.3 Munitions and Explosives

The  **Chiroptera**  manage the "Canopy-Crackers," pair-tuned with  **Tarsier**  ballistic hardware to provide the most reliable firearms in the Shatterlands.

#### 7. The Internal Shadow: The Cult of Toxic Liquidity

##### 7.1 Patron Threat

Formed by the corrupt elite of the Twelve Crowns, this cult serves the most destructive aspect of  **The Gilded Solvent** .

##### 7.2 Methodology

In abandoned mine shafts, cultists deliberately stash gold to generate massive concentrations of  **Flux-Rot sludge** . They view this acidic slurry as the ultimate expression of Aurgenas’s power.

##### 7.3 The Ritual of Dissolution

Cultists undergo "Living Liquidity" branding, where acidic gold-sludge is infused into their flesh. This horrific process renders the cultist’s body partially fluid and caustic, mirroring their patron’s draconic form. Their goal is the total dissolution of the Clans' social order.

#### 8. Foreign Relations and the Middlemen

##### 8.1 The Gilded Compass Interaction

The  **Avian Empire**  finds the Tangle’s shifting geography logistically offensive. The Clans capitalize on this by charging the Avians exorbitant "Guide Fees" through the Gilded Compass Cartel, effectively taxing the Avians for their own survival.

##### 8.2 The Iron Caldera Synergy

Because metal machines rust instantly in the Tangle, the  **Ironborn**  are forced to hire  **Sciurini**  couriers for all logistics. The  **Ailurus**  provide the necessary coolants for the  **Iron Caldera's**  factories, creating a cycle of mutual, if cynical, dependence.

##### 8.3 Shadow Week Protocols

During the seven-day eclipse,  **Aetheric Tension**  weakens globally, causing the Tangle's geography to become dangerously fluid. The Clans execute mandatory leadership rotations and currency dumps to keep the "Fiscal Flow" visible.**ARCHIVIST NOTE**  During Shadow Week, the "Tension Line" goes slack. The Tangle becomes a frenzy of trade and festivals; if the wealth does not move during this week, the city-states simply dissolve into the jungle.

### An Anthropological Briefing: The Mythology of the Black Rider and the Shadow Wings

#### 1.0 Introduction: Contextualizing Bat Folk Mythology

This document provides a focused examination of a specific mythological belief system—the tale of the Black Rider and his Shadow Wings—held by the Bat Folk within [[Heartland Alliance]]. While many societies develop elaborate etiological myths to explain their cosmology, this brief will concentrate on a singular, functionally significant myth that appears to be central to the identity and societal purpose of its adherents. The purpose of this briefing is to analyze the core tenets of this myth and its strategic importance in shaping the Bat Folk's societal role and function within the broader Alliance. The analysis will begin by defining the primary antagonists of this belief system.

#### 2.0 The Antagonistic Dyad of the Myth

To understand a culture, it is often most effective to begin by understanding what it fears. The antagonists within a society's mythology serve as powerful embodiments of collective anxieties and, in turn, define the virtues required to stand against them. In the case of the Bat Folk, their belief system is centered on two key malevolent figures who give purpose to their nocturnal existence.

##### 2.1 The Black Rider

Within the mythology of the Bat Folk, the entity known as "The Black Rider" is characterized as an "evil god." Beyond its designation as an "evil god," the source lore is silent on the Black Rider's specific attributes or dogma, suggesting its role is more symbolic than theological.

##### 2.2 The Shadow Wings

The "Shadow Wings" are identified as the "minions" of the Black Rider. Their role is explicitly subordinate to the evil god, serving as his agents or followers. This simple, hierarchical structure of evil—a single dark deity with attendant followers—provides a clear and unambiguous threat. To fully grasp the significance of this myth, one must understand the people who hold it as truth.

#### 3.0 Cultural Adherence: The Bat Folk of the Heartland Alliance

A myth's power and function are intrinsically linked to the people who believe it. A belief system does not exist in a vacuum; it is shaped by and, in turn, shapes the society that embraces it. This section will detail the cultural and societal profile of the Bat Folk, the sole adherents to this mythology, to understand the context in which it thrives.

##### 3.1 Societal Profile of the Bat Folk

The Bat Folk possess a distinct set of characteristics that define their lifestyle and interaction with the world:

* **Nocturnal Nature:**  They are fundamentally creatures of the night.  
* **Arboreal and Amiable:**  The Bat Folk are described as a friendly people who live in tree homes, suggesting a communal, non-aggressive, and nature-integrated lifestyle.  
* **Advanced Communication:**  They possess the unique ability to communicate warnings over long distances using sonic screeches, a trait that leverages their natural biology for practical application.

##### 3.2 Role within the Heartland Alliance

The Bat Folk maintain a crucial symbiotic relationship with the farmers of [[Heartland Alliance]]. Leveraging their excellent hearing and constant nocturnal presence, they function as highly effective "night watchers." This service is so valued that rural farmers actively encourage the Bat Folk to live nearby, integrating them directly into the security framework of the agrarian communities. This role is particularly vital given that the ambient chaos influencing the Heartland region, attributed to the entity known as "Lyra, The Phantom Blur," is characterized by threats that are quick and difficult to see. This practical, vital role is therefore inseparable from their cultural identity, an identity codified and sanctified by the very mythology it informs.

#### 4.0 Analysis: The Societal Function of the Myth

Mythology is rarely just a collection of stories; it is a strategic tool for social cohesion, cultural reinforcement, and the justification of societal duties. The narrative of a people often explains not only who they are but  *why*  they must be that way. This final section will analyze how the belief in the Black Rider and the Shadow Wings directly codifies and elevates the Bat Folk's essential contribution to [[Heartland Alliance]].From a functionalist perspective, the primary purpose of this mythology is to provide spiritual justification for a highly specialized adaptive strategy. The belief system transforms their practical function as nocturnal guardians into a sacred duty to stand watch against [[Sylas the Swift]], unseen dangers unique to the Heartland. The "Black Rider" and his "Shadow Wings" are not abstract evils; they are the mythological embodiment of the specific threat posed by Lyra, The Phantom Blur. The binary nature of this belief system—a single evil deity with his minions—presents a threat that is unambiguous and easily understood. This lack of doctrinal complexity ensures the core message is not diluted, focusing the Bat Folk's collective vigilance on their singular, essential function without the distraction of a more intricate theology. It is a mythology of pure function, elegantly designed for a people with a critical, specialized purpose. The myth is the cultural software that ensures the Bat Folk's biological hardware—their nocturnal senses and sonic communication—remains perfectly calibrated to counter the region's specific chaotic threat, solidifying their essential role in the security and stability of [[Heartland Alliance]].

#  The Chiroptera (The Night-Miners)
**Common Name:** Bat-Folk / The Echo-Kin
**Scientific Designation:** *Chiroptera Sapiens*
**Role:** Miners, Demolitionists, Night-Traders
**Home City:** **Echo-Spire** (A hollowed-out, calcified giant tree trunk, pitch black inside, illuminated only by bioluminescent moss).

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **Physiology:** They possess a humanoid torso with elongated arms that support massive, leathery wings. Standing height is roughly 5 feet, but their wingspan can reach 12 feet.
* **The Wings:** The membrane is thin but tough. Their "hands" are actually the single clawed thumbs at the apex of the wing, which they use for fine manipulation. When walking, they fold their wings around themselves like heavy cloaks.
* **Sensory Biology:** Their eyes are large and milky (low-light vision), but their primary sense is **Echolocation**. They possess large, leaf-shaped noses and oversized ears. They "see" texture and density through sound.
* **Bone Density:** To enable flight, their bones are semi-hollow. This makes them incredibly fragile in hand-to-hand combat (a solid club hit will shatter a limb), but allows them to carry heavy payloads in flight.

## 2.0 Psychology & Culture
* **The Philosophy of "The Deep Dark":** While other clans fear the dark, the Bats own it. They view light as a "blinding noise." Their cities are unlit. They consider complete darkness to be a state of purity and honesty.
* **Nocturnal Rhythm:** They sleep during the day in massive communal "Roost-Mansions" (hanging upside down). Their business day begins at dusk. This creates a "Shift-Change" economy where the Bats take over the markets when the [[Simians]] go to sleep.
* **Social Intimacy:** Because they roost in tight clusters for warmth, they have very little concept of personal space. Physical touch and huddling are standard social greetings.

## 3.0 Industrial Role: The Boom-Makers
* **The Guano Mines:** They control the deep caves beneath the Tangle. These caves are rich in nitrate-heavy guano (bat droppings) and **"Black-Stone"** (unstable mineral deposits).
* **Refinement:** They are the chemists of destruction. They refine the raw nitrates into **Gunpowder** and **Blasting Charges**.
* **The Monopoly:** If you want to blast a tunnel, fire a cannon, or celebrate with fireworks, you must buy from the Bats.

## 4.0 Military Role: The Bombers
* **Style:** High-Altitude Bombardment.
* **Weaponry:**
    * **"Canopy-Crackers":** Heavy iron balls filled with gunpowder, dropped from 500 feet.
    * **"Screamers":** Whistling devices dropped to create psychological terror and disorient enemies before a raid.
* **Tactics:** They fly silently at night (using echolocation to navigate without lights). They drop their payload on enemy campfires (the only light source) and vanish back into the dark.

## 5.0 Social Quirks
* **The "Click-Speak":** In casual conversation, they intersperse their words with ultrasonic clicks. To a non-Bat, it sounds like a Geiger counter.
* **Inversion:** They prefer to do business upside down. A Bat "Chair" is just a horizontal bar on the ceiling. They will often conduct serious negotiations while hanging, forcing the other party to crane their necks.
* **Diet:** They are Insectivores and Frugivores. They eat massive quantities of "Sugar-Cane" to fuel their flight, and farm giant moths for protein.

- **The King/Queen:** The wealthiest individual in the city.

    - A good King spends lavishly on public works (bridges, security, festivals) to keep the "Fiscal Flow" high. **This validates their self-interest:** They help the people _only_ to burn off their own excess wealth and stay in power.

    - **Trade:** Waste Management & Recycling.
        
    - **Vibe:** Industrial. They take the "Rot" (trash/waste) and turn it into fuel before Aurgenas can claim it.

### An Exposé on the Dust-Husk Riders of the Dust Bowl

##### Introduction: The Masters of the No-Fly Zone

In the desolate expanse known as the Dust Bowl—a region so hostile it is deemed a "No-Fly Zone" by all other factions—thrives an enigmatic and uniquely adapted society: the Dust-Husk Riders. Their ability to not merely survive but master this brutal environment is a testament to an extraordinary symbiosis between several distinct plant-like species, each fulfilling a critical and highly specialized role within their nomadic fleet. Where others see an impassable wasteland of blinding storms and treacherous terrain, the Riders see a sanctuary, shielded by the very elements that deter outsiders. This document will dissect the intricate biology, non-verbal command structure, and specialized technology that allow these desert drifters to prosper where no others can.

##### 1\. The Symbiotic Peoples of the Fleet

To understand the Dust-Husk Riders is to first understand that they are not a monolithic race, but a fleet composed of deeply specialized kin. Their strength lies not in uniformity but in radical biological diversity. This confederation of plant-peoples has evolved to perceive and interact with their environment in ways a single species never could, granting them a strategic and sensory advantage that is absolute in their domain.

###### *The Oracles: Moon-Flower Kin*

The spiritual and navigational heart of the fleet is the Moon-Flower Kin. These beings are biologically distinct, appearing as pale, fragile, vine-like humanoids who remain dormant in protective buds during the scorching daylight hours. At night, they bloom with massive, luminescent white flowers that emit a hypnotic, nectarous scent.

* **Biology and Function:**  Their entire life cycle is nocturnal. They subsist on a unique form of photosynthesis that draws energy directly from starlight, a process known as "Starlight Synthesis." This celestial dependency has fostered a culture of profound patience and a fatalistic worldview, seeing their fate as inextricably linked to the grand, slow movements of the cosmos. This connection grants them the exclusive authority within the fleet to read the "Deep Sky." As the sole astrologers and navigators, they are responsible for determining the auspicious times for a ship to depart from port, a decision that is never questioned.  
* **The "Night-Nectar":**  The Moon-Flowers produce a rare, hallucinogenic honey called "Night-Nectar." This substance is not a recreational commodity but a sacred tool used in religious rituals to glimpse "probable futures," providing the fleet's leadership with invaluable, if cryptic, foresight. The ritualized consumption of "Night-Nectar" solidifies the Moon-Flower Kin's authority, transforming their navigational guidance from mere science into divine, unquestionable prophecy.

###### *The Drifters: The Fleet's Core*

The main crew of the fleet—the engineers, pilots, and soldiers—operate under a unique command philosophy known as the "Trinity of Silence." These are the kin who have most perfectly adapted to the harsh realities of the Dust Bowl.

###### *The Muscle: Cactus-Kin*

The most physically imposing members of the fleet are the Cactus-Kin, whose very biology dictates their dual roles as the fleet's foundation and its primary protectors. Their hulking, water-retentive bodies are covered in iron-hard spines, making them living fortresses.

* **Function:**  Their immense weight is a critical asset. They serve as the living  **Ballast**  necessary to keep the fleet's lightweight ships from flipping in the violent winds of the Dust Bowl. Concurrently, they are the fleet's primary  **Captains and Engineers** , responsible for maintaining the vessels and manning their heavy armaments.  
* **Combat:**  In battle, the Cactus-Kin are formidable. They operate the heavy "Needle-Cannons" and, in close-quarters combat, employ a terrifying tactic known as the "Iron Embrace"—a hug that impales its target on their countless spines.  
* **Beliefs:**  The Cactus-Kin hold a deep superstition regarding  **Salt** , which they view as a holy ward against malevolent spirits known as "The Dry Rot." They are known to line their bunks with salt rings to ensure a safe rest. This ritualistic use of salt, a powerful desiccant, strongly suggests their cultural anxieties are rooted in a fear of decay and spiritual desiccation—a tangible ward against an intangible threat.| Variant | Description & Role || \------ | \------ || **Saguaro** | Tall and commanding, they naturally fill leadership roles. || **Barrel** | Wide and powerful, these are the frontline brawlers. |

###### *The Pilots: Yucca-Kin*

Tall, twisted, and fibrous, the Yucca-Kin are defined by their unique sensory abilities. Their bodies are covered in a shaggy "bark" with spiky leaves serving as hair, but their true strength lies in what they can feel, not what they see.

* **"Vibration-Sense":**  The Yucca-Kin possess a highly advanced ability to sense vibrations. This makes them indispensable as pilots. In the blinding dust storms common to the region, where vision is useless, they steer the ships by feeling the vibrations of the ground as they travel through the hull, navigating with unerring precision.  
* **Secondary Role:**  Driven by a natural "hoarding instinct," they also serve as the fleet's  **Cargo Masters** . They possess an uncanny ability to judge the exact weight and value of an object simply by holding it.  
* **Beliefs:**  This instinct is tied to a powerful superstition: they never count their money or cargo aloud. They believe that if the Wind hears the number, it will inevitably demand a "tax" by claiming a portion of the goods. This personification of the Wind as a covetous entity reflects a deep-seated understanding of their environment's unforgiving nature, where any surplus can be reclaimed by the elements in an instant.

###### *The Scouts: Tumbleweed-Kin*

The final member of the operational trinity, the Tumbleweed-Kin, function as the fleet's scouts. While the Cactus rely on sight and the Yucca on vibration, the Tumbleweed-Kin's sensory expertise is attuned to  **Motion and Wind** . They are the true eyes of the fleet, capable of perceiving distant threats, approaching storms, and hidden opportunities carried on the desert currents long before they become apparent to their symbiotic partners.Their individual adaptations, while remarkable, are only fully realized when they function as a cohesive, interdependent whole.

##### 2\. The Trinity of Silence: A Philosophy of Action

The "Trinity of Silence" is more than a simple command structure; it is the core cultural philosophy of the Dust-Husk Riders, born from their radical biological diversity. In the unforgiving Dust Bowl, hesitation is death. Their non-verbal system of command and control is the key to their survival, allowing for rapid, instinctual, and decisive action without the delay of communication.The philosophy is a perfect interplay of their specialized senses. Each member trusts the expertise of the others implicitly, as they each perceive a facet of reality the others cannot.

* **The Cactus (Captain):**  Relies on direct  **Visuals and Strength** . They see the immediate, physical world and have the power to act upon it.  
* **The Yucca (Pilot):**  Relies on  **Vibration and Feel** . They perceive the subtle, unseen shifts in the terrain beneath them, guiding the vessel through what is hidden.  
* **The Tumbleweed (Scout):**  Relies on  **Motion and Wind** . They sense the intangible—the approaching storm, the distant movement—carried on the air itself.The primary tactical advantage of this system is its speed. Because each member experiences the world through a fundamentally different sense, verbal explanations are seen as a redundant and dangerous waste of time. The Cactus captain does not need to be told a storm is coming; they trust the Tumbleweed scout's change in alertness. The Yucca pilot does not need to be told of a surface threat; they trust the Cactus captain's sudden readiness for combat. They do not discuss; they simply  **act** . This silent trust is the ultimate expression of their symbiotic society. It has created a culture where empathy is not an emotion but a tactical necessity, and where understanding is demonstrated through action, not words.This silent, instinctual trust is embodied in the tools and vessels they have engineered to master their domain.

##### 3\. Technology and Survival in the Dust Bowl

The technology of the Dust-Husk Riders is not merely a collection of tools but a physical manifestation of their core philosophy: to bend with the desert's power, not break against it. Their vessels and weapons are not designed to conquer the desert with brute force, but rather to coexist with it, valuing lightness, speed, and sensory feedback over the heavy armor and raw power favored by other factions.

###### *Insect-Shell Ships*

The fleet's ships are constructed from the lightweight yet durable shells of massive insects native to the region. These vessels are built for speed and agility, allowing them to glide over the dunes with incredible swiftness. This choice reflects their worldview of riding the wind rather than fighting it. However, their lightness makes them vulnerable to the powerful gales of the Dust Bowl. This is where the symbiotic relationship with the Cactus-Kin becomes essential; their immense weight serves as the living ballast required to keep the ships grounded and prevent them from being flipped and destroyed by sudden storms.

###### *Weaponry and Combat*

The Riders' approach to combat is direct and brutal, leveraging the unique strengths of the Cactus-Kin.

* **Needle-Cannons:**  Their primary armaments are heavy cannons that fire clusters of iron-hard spines, manned exclusively by the powerful Cactus-Kin.  
* **The Iron Embrace:**  For close-quarters engagements, such as boarding actions, they rely on a terrifying tactic known as the "Iron Embrace." The Barrel-variant Cactus-Kin brawlers physically grapple with their enemies, using their spined bodies to crush and impale them in a lethal hug from which there is no escape.Through this perfect synthesis of specialized biology, instinctual philosophy, and purpose-built technology, the Dust-Husk Riders have achieved true mastery of their environment. They remain a formidable, if reclusive, power within the desolate expanse of the Dust Bowl.

# Raiding Doctrine (The Great Cull)

To the Eastern Hounds, a raid is not an act of war; it is a **Harvest**. They view a [[Meridian Chain]] caravan exactly the same way they view a herd of [[Ash-Bison]]: a resource moving through their territory that needs to be culled. they only hit caravans and over laden barges , etc. they take what others have in excess  and they wont give if asked so they take, if the food wont surrender you hunt it.

##  The "Silent-Ride" Protocol

Before a raid begins, the Pack undergoes a ritualistic preparation to ensure absolute silence.

- **Gear-Binding:** Every buckle, weapon, and saddle-ring is wrapped in leather or wool. A Hound raiding party makes zero metallic noise.
    
- **The Mounts:** The **Thunder-Striders** (Giant Moas) have their claws padded with heavy wool "muffles" until the moment of the charge.
    
- **Communication:** No words are spoken. Orders are given via **"Thump-Codes"**—rhythmic taps on the saddle or the ground that the mounts and riders can feel through vibration.
    

## Tactical Method: The Fog-Walk

The Hounds refuse to fight on the enemy's terms. They wait for the environment to strip away the enemy's advantage (sight).

- **The Weather-Wait:** They will track a caravan for days, staying just over the horizon, waiting for a coastal fog bank or an ash-storm.
    
- **The Blind Charge:** Once visibility drops to zero, they strike.
    
    - _Enemy Disadvantage:_ The defenders (Meridian or Prism) are blinded and panicked. They fire wildly into the mist.
        
    - _Hound Advantage:_ The Hounds close their eyes (or wear blindfolds). They navigate by the sound of the enemy's breathing, the clanking of armor, and the heartbeat of the caravan oxen.
        
    - _Result:_ The Hounds strike with surgical precision in pitch darkness. They don't aim for what they _see_; they strike where the sound _is_.
        

## Target Prioritization

They are not looting for gold. They are looting for **Survival**.

- **Priority 1: Steel & Iron.** Since they cannot forge high-quality metal on the move, they strip the armor off dead guards and pry the iron rims off wagon wheels.
    
- **Priority 2: Food & Salt.** They empty the larders. A raid is considered a failure if the Pack goes hungry that night.
    
- **Priority 3: "White-Bone" Targets.** If the enemy has mages, the Hounds deploy **"Breakers"**—warriors wielding spears tipped with **White-Bone Ceramic**. These weapons punch through magical shields as if they were glass.
    
- **Ignored Loot:** They leave behind gold coins, fine silks, and art. These are heavy, useless, and carry "history."
    

##  The "Clean-Break" (Post-Raid)

The most terrifying aspect of a Hound raid is how it ends.

- **No Prisoners:** Prisoners slow down the migration.
    
- **No Ghosts:** They burn the bodies of the fallen enemies immediately using **Focus-Pebbles** (fire-starters). This is not out of disrespect, but out of spiritual safety—they do not want the angry ghosts of the merchant guards following them.
    
- **The Vanishing:** By the time the fog clears, the Hounds are gone. The tracks of their birds are swept away by the wind, leaving only a smoldering wreck and silence.

## 1.0 The City of Mud and Jade

Opal-Wallow is built _into_ the water. It is a sprawling Venice of wide, flat canals and buildings made of compressed, heat-treated mud bricks glazed with iridescent green ceramic (Jade-Mud).

- **Architecture:**
    
    - **The Sunken Lounges:** Most buildings are half-submerged. The ground floor is actually a pool. You don't sit in chairs; you lounge in waist-deep warm water while servers float trays of drinks to you.
        
    - **The "Wide-Doors":** Every door is double-width to accommodate the massive bulk of the locals.

    - **The "Cleanse":** Criminals and spies come here to "wash off the heat." By tradition, Opal-Wallow is **Neutral Ground**. No arrests, no assassinations. If you break the peace, the Bouncers (Retired Hippo Enforcers) will simply drown you.
        

## 3.0 The Crown Jewel: _The River-King's Shuffle_

A colossal Paddle-Wheel Casino Boat that is permanently moving.

- **The Route:** It sails a slow, endless loop around the Great Lake, never docking. You must take a water-taxi to board.
    
- **The Game:** **"Tiles."** A domino-like game of strategy and bluffing.
    
    - **The Legend:** It is rumored that the **[[Grand Auditor Keth]]** (The Owl Director) has a private suite on the top deck where he meets with the **Pirate Lords** of the Compass once a year.

# The River-King's Shuffle

**Classification:** Mobile Casino / Neutral Ground / Black Market Hub **Location:** The Great Lake & Opal-Delta (Strictly Mobile) **Owner:** The "House" (Managed by the Hippo Syndicate) **Motto:** _" The River always takes its cut."_

> **"If you are looking for fair play, go to a church. If you are looking for a chance to win a kingdom or lose your soul, step onto the plank. But mind your manners; the bouncers are hungry."**

## 1.0 The Vessel: The Gilded Leviathan

The _Shuffle_ is the largest river-vessel in existence. It is a triple-decked, flat-bottomed paddle-wheeler the size of a small fortress.

- **Visuals:** Painted in garish whites and golds, illuminated by thousands of Aether-lamps that cast a multicoloured glow onto the mist.
    
- **Propulsion:** Two massive rear paddle-wheels churn the water, powered not by steam, but by **Slave-Gears** turned by massive tamed **River-Beasts** (or well-paid but exhausted Ogres) below decks.
    
- **The Route:** It never docks. It sails a perpetual figure-eight loop around the Great Lake.
    
    - **The Ferry:** To board, you must hire a "Water-Jockey"—small, fast skiffs run by Otters that match speed with the leviathan to offload guests.
        

## 2.0 The Decks (The Hierarchy of Vice)

### **The Bilge (The "Mud-Floor")**

- **Clientele:** Sailors, laborers, and Raccoons.
    
- **The Vibe:** Loud, smoky, and violent.
    
- **The Games:** Dice, arm-wrestling, and "Rat-Racing" (literal rats in a maze).
    
- **Currency:** Copper, D-Dust, and favors.
    

### **The Jade Deck (The Main Floor)**

- **Clientele:** Merchants, Captains, and lower Nobility.
    
- **The Vibe:** Opulent repression. Walls lined with green velvet, ceiling fans lazily spinning.
    
- **The Centerpiece:** **The Tank**.
    
    - In the center of the floor is a massive, glass-walled pool. Inside lounges **Matriarch "Gilded" Bess**, the Hippo proprietress. She runs the casino while floating in warm water, wearing necklaces of pure Aetherium.
        
    - **The Interaction:** You don't cash out at a cage; you present your chips to Bess. If she nods, a teller pays you. If she yawns, you leave without your winnings.
        

### **The Spire Deck (VIP Only)**

- **Clientele:** Wolf Generals, Compass Admirals, and Highborn Senators.
    
- **The Vibe:** Silence and high stakes.
    
- **The Rumor:** There is a sealed suite guarded by **Iron-Beak** mercenaries. It is reserved 365 days a year for **[[Grand Auditor Keth]]**. Even if he only visits once a decade, the room is kept dusted and stocked with fresh [[Mice]].
    

## 3.0 The Game: "River-Tiles"

The signature game of the _Shuffle_. It is a mix of dominoes, poker, and psychological warfare.

- **The Components:** Heavy, hand-carved tiles made of **River-Dragon Bone**. They make a distinct, heavy _clack_ when slammed onto the table.
    
- **The Mechanic:** It is not about having the highest numbers; it is about building a "Flow." You must match your tiles to the "current" set by the dealer.
    
- **The Bluff:** You can play a tile face down (A "Rock"). The opponent must decide if it disrupts the flow or if you are bluffing.
    
- **Why [[Hippos]] Win:** The game requires reading micro-expressions. [[Hippos]] are the grandmasters; Avians (who can't lie) are terrible at it and are usually fleeced within an hour.
    

## 4.0 Security: "The Splash"

The _Shuffle_ has no brig. It has **Gravity**.

- **The Bouncers:** Massive, scar-covered [[Hippos]] in tuxedos.
    
- **The Punishment:**
    
    - **Minor Infraction (Cheating/Fighting):** You are thrown overboard. You have to swim 3 miles to shore in water infested with **Gar-Pikes**.
        
    - **Major Infraction (Threatening the House):** "The Anchor." They tie a heavy sack of D-Dust to your ankle and drop you. The dust reacts with the water, boiling you as you sink.
        

## 5.0 The Golden Rule: Neutrality

The _Shuffle_ is the only place in the world where a **Grey Warden** can sit next to a **Worm Cultist** without drawing steel.

- **Enforcement:** The peace is enforced by a **[[Shadow-Set Contract]]** inked onto the doorframe. By stepping through, you agree to the Truce.
    
- **The Effect:** If you draw a weapon with intent to kill, the ink on the doorframe triggers a **"Suppression Ward,"** instantly increasing the gravity in the room by 10x. Everyone is pinned to the floor until the Bouncers collect the offender.

#  The Sap-Sworn (The Drunken Druids)

**Aliases:** The Brewers, The Green-Friars, The Stumbling-Saints **Location:** **The Ferment-Halls** (Massive, hollowed-out burls on the Mother-Tree, smelling of yeast, honey, and ozone). **Role:** Spiritual Guides, Ecological Regulators, "Park Rangers"

## 1.0 The Source of Power: "The Tune"

The Sap-Sworn do not cast spells from books; they drink them. They believe the Jungle has a consciousness, and the only way to hear it is to lower your own mental barriers through sacred intoxication.

- **The Sap-Tapping:** They harvest raw saps from the thousands of magical tree species in the Tangle.
    
- **The Mixology:**
    
    - **Raw Sap:** Provides physical sustenance (energy/healing).
        
    - **The Ferment:** By mixing saps (e.g., Iron-Wood sap + [[Sun-Melon]] sugar + Ghost-Fungus yeast), they create hallucinogenic brews.
        
    - **The Effect:** A "Tuned" Druid sees the world as energy veins. They can grab a vine and "feel" where the water is underground. They can sing to a tree and convince it to grow a bridge.
        
- **The Cost:** A sober Druid is powerless. A drunk Druid is a conduit for a god.
    

## 2.0 Hierarchy: The Path & The Circle

### **Phase I: The Pathfinders (The Rangers)**

- **Recruitment:** They accept anyone with "The Spark"—usually those born with minor magical talents or a deep affinity for nature.
    
- **The Job:** Before they are trusted with the secrets of the Brew, they must serve as **Rangers**.
    
    - _Duties:_ Guiding travelers, inspecting Opossum mining permits to ensure safety, clearing overgrowth from trade routes, and rescuing lost children.
        
    - _Appearance:_ They wear practical leathers, carry heavy walking sticks, and are generally sober enough to interact with the Clans.
        

### **Phase II: The Circle-Keepers (The Attuned)**

- **The Ascension:** When a Pathfinder proves they understand the Jungle, they undergo the **"Deep Draft."** They drink a lethal dose of a Master-Brew specific to their calling. If they survive, they join a **Circle**.
    
- **The Circles:** Each Circle specializes in protecting one domain of the Tangle.
    
    - **Circle of the Stream:** Guardians of fresh water. They can purify a poisoned well or command a river to flood a mining camp.
        
    - **Circle of the Bark:** The Tree-Shepherds. They heal the Iron-Wood and shape living timber into homes.
        
    - **Circle of the Hive:** The Insect-Lords. They are never alone; they walk surrounded by clouds of bees or beetles that act as their spies and soldiers.
        
    - **Circle of the Stone:** Guardians of the roots and geology. They sense earthquakes and stabilize the foundations of the Canopy Cities.
        
    - **Circle of the Gale:** The Wind-Callers. They drink "Aerated Sap" and can summon tailwinds for airships or storms to ground them.
        
    - **Circle of the Sun:** The Farmers. They can bless a crop to harvest in a day or curse a field to wither in an hour.
        
    - **Circle of the Shade:** Guardians of the under-canopy darkness. Masters of camouflage and secrets.
        
    - **Circle of the Spore:** Masters of fungus and medicine. They brew the strongest healing drafts and the deadliest hallucinogens.
        
    - **Circle of the Heart:** The People-Watchers. They act as diplomats, marriage counselors, and therapists for the Clans.
        
    - **Circle of the Grave:** Guardians of the cycle. They ensure the dead rot peacefully and fight against the Necromancy of the [[Eastern Hounds]].
        
    - **Circle of the Talon:** The Beast-Masters. They drink with the bears and hunt with the Terror-Fowl.
        

## 3.0 Social Role: The Friar's Blessing

The Sap-Sworn are the most beloved—and feared—faction in the Tangle.

- **The Vibe:** They are the "Jolly Friars." They are loud, generous, and notoriously hard-drinking. It is considered good luck to buy a Sap-Sworn a drink, as their presence wards off bad spirits.
    
- **The Authority:** They hold no political office, yet even the high merchant-princes bow to them.
    
- **The Folklore of Punishment:**
    
    - Stories abound of arrogant nobles who ignored a Druid's advice. One famous tale tells of a Prince who planted sugar cane in a sacred grove after a "Stumbling-Saint" told him not to.
        
    - _The Result:_ The Prince wasn't attacked by the Druid. The _Jungle_ simply ate his estate. Vines grew over the doors overnight, the water turned to vinegar, and the cane turned to ash.
        
- **The Saying:** _"The Friar suggests, but the Jungle insists."_

# The Solar-Worm

**Common Name:** Sun-Grub / The Golden-Fleece **Scientific Designation:** _Larva Solaris_ **Role:** Luxury Fur Source, Heat Source **Status:** Wild (Seasonally Trapped)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance

- **Physiology:** A segmented caterpillar reaching 5 to 6 feet in length and the thickness of a man's thigh.
    
- **Habitat:** They reside exclusively in the **"Ghost-Trees"** (petrified forests) along the eastern coast.
    
- **The Solar Mechanism:** They are cold-blooded but have evolved a unique survival mechanism. Their fur filaments act as biological solar panels, absorbing UV radiation and storing it as thermal energy.
    

## 2.0 The Resource: "Sun-Fur"

- **Thermal Battery:** The pelt of a Solar-Worm is not just warm; it is _active_. Even after the worm is skinned, the chemical bonds in the fur continue to release stored heat for up to 5 years.
    
- **Value:** A single cloak lined with Solar-Worm fur allows a scout to lie motionless in a snowbank for days without freezing. It is the most valuable textile in the Northern Hemisphere.
    

## 3.0 The Harvest

- **Danger:** Touching a live Solar-Worm with bare skin causes instant second-degree burns as the creature discharges its heat defense.
    
- **Technique:** Trappers must wait for winter overcast days (when the worms are low on energy) and use long-handled tongs or ceramic-tipped spears to harvest them without damaging the pelt.

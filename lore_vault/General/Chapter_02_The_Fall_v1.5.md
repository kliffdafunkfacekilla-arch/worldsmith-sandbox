In this time, the world knew an unparalleled peace born of cosmic balance. The seasons followed their cycles without fail, magic flowed in predictable currents, and the threat of unmaking was a concept known only to the gods. The era was defined by a profound stability, a gift from the Clockwork that allowed life to flourish under a constant and ordered sky.This stable and harmonious world was the cradle for the first and most powerful of all mortal races.

  
During the Era,  [[The Fae]] arose as the world's first and most powerful race. Their connection to the world's energies was not a matter of study or ritual but an innate, effortless state of being. They did not wield power so much as they existed in concert with it, a reality that set them apart from all who would come after.

In the primordial era before [[The Shattering]], the world was a masterpiece of **Celestial Clockwork**, where the twelve powers were perfectly refined and balanced. The biomes were not chaotic "zones" but stable, idealized environments where [[The Fae]] acted as living conduits for the machine's perfect output.

Here are the twelve primordial biomes, structured by the refined powers of the Clockwork:

### 1. The Gravity Spires (Nexus)

- **The Biome:** A high-tension landscape where the "grip" of the world was perfectly calibrated.
    
- **Description:** This biome consisted of towering, smooth obsidian pillars that leaned toward one another, held in a state of perpetual magnetic balance. The air hummed with a low-frequency vibration of attraction and repulsion.
    
- **Fae Usage:** [[The Fae]] of Nexus managed the structural bonds of the world, preventing landslides and ensuring the seas and mountains stayed in their assigned places.
    

### 2. The Unyielding Foundation (Mass)

- **The Biome:** A geologically dense region that provided the world’s physical weight and permanence.
    
- **Description:** Featuring "Stone-Oaks" with lead-dense bark, this was a land where the ground was so solid it never eroded or shifted.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They reinforced the density of matter, ensuring that islands stayed afloat and the continental crust remained thick and stable.
    

### 3. The Kinetic Plains (Motus)

- **The Biome:** A high-velocity, synchronized ecosystem defined by perfect timing.
    
- **Description:** Trees here vibrated with kinetic timing, their leaves moving in a rapid, melodic shimmy that created a global heartbeat.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They regulated the speed of the world—controlling the velocity of the winds and the pace of the seasons to ensure no transition was too abrupt.
    

### 4. The Shifting Mistlands (Flux)

- **The Biome:** A delta of transformation where matter existed in a state of potential.
    
- **Description:** In these "Mirror Groves," the trees and waters were semi-liquid, constantly shifting to match the needs of the environment.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They oversaw the transformation of states—turning rain to mist or guiding the metamorphosis of creatures, acting as the masters of adaptation.
    

### 5. The Healing Cradle (Vita)

- **The Biome:** A hyper-vibrant, life-giving jungle that served as the world's primary nursery.
    
- **Description:** The air was thick with glowing moss and medicinal pollens that instantly repaired any biological damage.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They were the world's gardeners, ensuring that organic growth was healthy, vigorous, and free from disease.
    

### 6. The Eternal Sanctuary (Lex)

- **The Biome:** A silent, crystalline cathedral that embodied the unbending laws of physics.
    
- **Description:** The trees were geometric, translucent, and completely immovable—they could not be burned, cut, or broken.
    
- **Fae Usage:** These Fae were the guardians of the "Unbending Rule," ensuring the fundamental constants of reality (like gravity and causality) never wavered.
    

### 7. The Silver-Leaf Archives (Ratio)

- **The Biome:** A living library that processed and stored the "data" of the world.
    
- **Description:** Trees with silver bark had leaves that functioned like vellum pages, pulsing with blue light as they recorded every event in the vicinity.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They acted as the world's memory, utilizing psychic energy to recall centuries of history and translate it into practical action.
    

### 8. The Mathematical Orchards (Ordo)

- **The Biome:** A landscape of absolute symmetry and mathematical reasoning.
    
- **Description:** Every branch and stream followed perfect geometric angles, moving in a "perfect dance" of calculated harmony.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They applied logic to the environment, preventing natural disasters by calculating and implementing perfect offsets to any imbalance.
    

### 9. The Prism Heights (Lux)

- **The Biome:** High-altitude cliffs that captured the full spectrum of celestial light.
    
- **Description:** "Prism-Pines" refracted sunlight and starlight into thousands of colors, allowing the biome to be seen even in the deepest night.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They were the masters of perception, sensing distant cosmic events and guiding the sun and moon on their correct paths.
    

### 10. The Premonition Willows (Omen)

- **The Biome:** A sensitive, whispering woodland that reacted to the world's "vibe".
    
- **Description:** The leaves would change color or turn inward _before_ a storm or environmental shift occurred.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They acted as the "early warning system," sensing when the gears of the Clockwork were slipping and alerting the other Fae sects.
    

### 11. The Gilded Vales (Aura)

- **The Biome:** A tranquil glade that emitted a scent of profound emotional harmony.
    
- **Description:** The biome induced a sense of peace so deep that conflict was physically impossible within its borders.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They used this "vibe" to harmonize the fledgling species of Man, acting as the primary diplomats of the early age.
    

### 12. The Echoing Steppes (Anumis)

- **The Biome:** A spectral grassland where the "drive" of existence was anchored.
    
- **Description:** White trees cast no shadows, and the grass moved even without wind, carrying the voices of the past.
    
- **Fae Usage:** They anchored the spirits of the departed, ensuring their wisdom remained as a persistent "echo" to guide the living and keep the world's spirit strong.

## Master Lore: The Fae

# The Fae

**Subject:** The True Nature of "Divinity" / The Exiles of the Barrens **Current Location:** **The Barrens** (The Far Shore / The Dead World) **Status:** Mortal / Desperate / Parasitic

> **"You kneel and pray for rain. You pray for justice. You feel a warm light and think it is love. It is not love, child. It is the fever-heat of a starving thing pressing its eye against the keyhole, waiting for you to unlock the door."** — _Recovered Journal of a Mad Warden_

## 1.0 The Great Mistake: The Fall from Grace

Eons ago, during the rise of the **Magistars**, the Fae (The Aethel) realized their world was doomed. They gathered at the **Sacred Groves** and performed the Great Exodus, opening a portal to a new dimension to escape the coming Chaos.

They expected a paradise. They found **The Barrens**.

- **The Severance:** The moment the portal closed, the Fae discovered a horrifying truth. Their immortality, their glowing beauty, and their infinite magic were not intrinsic to _them_. Those powers were a byproduct of their connection to the Living World (Ostraka/Elyia).
    
- **The Crash:** In The Barrens, severed from the source, they instantly became **Mortal**.
    
    - Their light went out.
        
    - They felt cold, hunger, and age for the first time.
        
    - They became flesh and bone—frail, terrifyingly ordinary flesh.
        

## 2.0 The Location: The Barrens

The dimension they fled to is a geological hellscape with **Zero Ambient Aether**. It is a dead rock.

- **The Climate:** A binary nightmare.
    
    - **The Scorch:** During the "day," the sun is a aetheric feedback-heavy white star that blisters skin and boils water.
        
    - **The Freeze:** At night, the temperature drops to near absolute zero.
        
- **The Society of Rags:** The once-majestic Fae now live like cave-dwelling scavengers. They huddle in deep subterranean lava tubes, wearing rags scavenged from their original exodus robes. They are gaunt, pale, and terrified of death.
    
- **The Dwindling:** They are dying out. Without magic, they cannot reproduce (or their offspring are born "Hollow"—soulless husks). The millions who fled are now reduced to a few thousand desperate survivors.
    

## 3.0 The "Divinity": The Crack in the Wall

The only reason they haven't frozen to death is **The Tear**.

The portal they used to escape did not seal perfectly. A microscopic fracture remains—a pinprick in reality that leaks a tiny, steady stream of magic from Ostraka into The Barrens.

- **The Huddle:** The Fae hierarchy is determined by proximity to The Tear. The High Elders sit closest to it, bathing in the faint aetheric feedback of home like homeless men around a burning trash can.
    
- **The Discovery:** They realized that sound and intent could travel _back_ through the Tear. They realized they could hear the mortals on the other side.
    

## 4.0 The Great Charade: The Invention of "Gods"

Desperate to return, the Fae concocted the ultimate con. They created a "System" to manipulate the mortals of Ostraka into opening the door from the outside.

They invented **The Pantheon**.

- **The Roles:** The Fae Elders act out roles like a radio drama.
    
    - **"The Dawn-Father":** A committee of Fae shouting authoritative commands through the Tear to sound like a god of Light and Order.
        
    - **"The Lady of the Lantern":** A Fae assigned to whisper secrets to thieves and spies to manipulate political outcomes.
        
- **The "Divine" Magic (The RPG System):**
    
    - **Clerics (The Batteries):** When a Cleric prays, they are not receiving a gift. They are opening a channel. The Fae _siphon_ a portion of the Cleric’s own soul-energy (the Tithe) to feed themselves. In exchange, they send back a pre-packaged "Spell"—a packet of raw energy they hoarded from the Tear.
        
    - **Paladins (The Battering Rams):** The Fae grant power to Paladins not for justice, but to create soldiers capable of smashing the **Dragonstone Prisons**. The "Oath" is a binding contract to clear the path for the Fae's return.
        

## 5.0 The End Game: Re-Infection

The Fae do not want to save Ostraka. They hate what it has become (mutated, dirty, chaotic). But they _need_ it.

- **The Motivation:** Pure survival. They are starving. They look through the Tear and see a buffet of magic.
    
- **The Plan:**
    
    1. Trick the **Church of the Eternal Dawn** into "Cleansing" [[The Sacred Groves]] (which actually weakens the locks).
        
    2. Trick the Paladins into slaying the Chaos Dragons (which breaks the seals).
        
    3. **The Return:** Once the door opens, the Fae will flood back in.
        
- **The Consequence:** They will not be benevolent rulers. They will be **Vampires**. To regain their immortality, they will need to consume the ambient Aether of the world. They will drain the planet dry, killing every magical creature, wizard, and spirit in the process, turning Ostraka into a dead husk just like The Barrens.
    

## 6.0 The Tragic Irony

This reality recontextualizes every major religious faction in the setting.

- **The Church of the Eternal Dawn:** They are the unwitting agents of an otherworldly invasion. Their piety is slowly unlocking the cage of a starving predator.
    
- **The Wardens:** They are the only ones standing between the world and "Salvation." By keeping the Dragons imprisoned, they are accidentally starving the Fae.
    
- **The Paradox:** The only reason the Fae haven't invaded yet is because the **Chaos Dragons** are sitting on the door. The monsters are the only thing keeping the "Gods" out.

## Master Lore: Grave Touched

#  The Grave-Touched (The Spirit-Binders)
**Role:** Necromancers, Mediums, Spirit-Tacticians
**Magic Source:** **Ancestral Binding** (Willpower vs. Spirit)
**Symbol:** A skull with the mouth sewn shut with silver thread.

## 1.0 The Magic System: The Spirit-Shackle
The Grave-Touched do not cast "spells" in the arcane sense. They are jailers of the dead. They view the restless spirits of the Steppes as ammunition to be captured and fired at the enemy.

* **Elemental Binding:** To cast fire, they do not conjure it from nothing. They unshackle a **"Cinder-Haunt"** (a spirit that died by fire) from their totem and command it to manifest its pain upon the enemy.
* **Object Animation:** They can force a spirit to possess inanimate matter. A pile of "White-Bone" is just rock until a Shaman binds the spirit of an Earth-Elemental or a deceased Strongman into it, creating a **Bone-Golem** that obeys their mental commands.
* **Grave-Mist:** They summon the **"Breath of the Pit."** This is the thick, supernatural fog used during raids. It is the ectoplasm of thousands of disturbed spirits rising from the mass graves at the Shaman's command to blind the living.

## 2.0 The Channeling: Controlled Possession
The most dangerous art is the **"Open Door."** A Shaman can voluntarily lower their mental walls and invite a spirit *in*.

* **The Boon:**
    * *Strength:* Channeling an Ancient Warlord to fight with supernatural skill.
    * *Tracking:* Channeling a Great Wolf spirit to hunt by scent.
    * *Knowledge:* Channeling a dead Scholar to read an ancient language.
* **The Risk:** If the Shaman’s will falters, the spirit takes over completely. To prevent this, Shamans wear **White-Bone Collars** enchanted to choke off the magic if the possession goes too deep.

## 3.0 Initiation: The Cave of Whispers
You do not choose to be a Shaman; you survive it. The "Shadow Week" is a descent into the underworld.

* **The Location:** A secret, subterranean cavern deep within the heart of the largest White-Bone Quarry. Only the Shamans know the entrance.
* **The Test:** The apprentice is locked in the pitch dark with the concentrated anger of millions of dead souls from the First Wars.
* **The Objective:** They must dominate a spirit. They must bind it to their will before it drives them mad.
* **The Failure State (The Rotting Vessels):** If an apprentice fails, their soul is eaten, and their body becomes a **"Rotting Vessel"**—a flesh-puppet piloted by insane ancient spirits.
    * *The Final Exam:* Often, a new apprentice's first test is to destroy the "Rotting Vessel" of the apprentice who failed before them.

## 4.0 Death Rites & The Taboo
The existence of the Grave-Touched recontextualizes the Hounds' funeral rites.

* **The Burning:** The Tribe burns their dead to ash not just to stop the Dragon Korus, but to stop **The Shamans**. The common folk fear that if they are buried whole, a Shaman might dig them up and bind their soul to a weapon. Burning destroys the "anchor," freeing the soul to vanish where no one can enslave it.
* **The Willing Tether (The Honor):** In dire times, a dying Champion may *volunteer* their soul. They refuse the fire. The Shaman binds their spirit to the Tribe’s War-Totem, allowing the Champion to continue advising and protecting the Pack as a ghost.
* **The Guardian-Geist (The Secret):** Rarely, a Shaman will secretly bind a spirit to a living person (usually a child destined for greatness). This gives the child a "Guardian Angel" that deflects arrows and whispers warnings, though this practice is forbidden and punishable by exile if discovered.

The Odonata Cycle and the Secret Language of the Delta


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

🌾 THE ODONATA: The Cycle of the Delta

1.0 The Naiads (The Aquatic Youth)

The Odonata do not hatch with their iridescent wings or slender legs. They spend the first several years of their lives entirely bound to the flooded Pearl-Rice paddies and Delta wetlands as **Naiads**.

- **Physiology:** They are aquatic, torpedo-shaped juveniles possessing only a set of incredibly strong, multi-jointed arms and a set of internal gills. They slither and pull themselves through the submerged rice stalks with terrifying speed.
- **The Nursery Paddies:** Because they are restricted to the water, the adult Odonata dig specialized, shallow "Nursery Pools" directly at the base of their massive wooden windmills. Here, the Naiads are fiercely protected from predatory Gar-Kin or Sump-Kin poachers.
- **The Labor:** After a year or two of growth, their arms become strong enough to assist the homestead. They use their submerged environment to their advantage, processing heavy wetland materials that would be too cumbersome to manipulate on dry land.

2.0 The Cattail Canvas

The Naiads' primary and most vital contribution to the homestead is the creation of the windmill sails, essential for pumping clean water through the shifting, toxic Delta.

- **The Material:** The adults harvest thousands of **"Rust-Reeds"**—a local, giant variant of the cattail that resembles a massive, fuzzy brown cylinder on a stalk.
- **The Processing:** The adult Odonata drop these reeds into the Nursery Pools. The Naiads use their powerful arms to meticulously strip and shred the brown fuzz, spinning it into a dense, water-resistant, cotton-like fiber.
- **The Submerged Loom:** The Naiads weave the massive windmill sails entirely underwater, where the buoyancy supports the immense weight of the canvas. Once the weaving and dyeing are complete, the adults winch the dripping-wet sail out of the pool and attach it to the windmill arms, where the fierce Delta winds dry it into a rigid, unbreakable canvas.

3.0 The Sail-Cipher (The Frontier Billboard)

The Odonata are fiercely independent, but survival in the foggy, chaotic Delta requires community. To solve the issue of terrible visibility, they have turned their massive windmills into spinning communication towers.

- **The Superstition (The Cover):** To outsiders and passing merchant barges, the Naiads are simply known to weave intricate, brightly colored geometric patterns into the sails. The Odonata publicly claim these dyes and shapes are traditional wards meant to "catch good winds and bring prosperity to the harvest."
- **The Truth (The Cipher):** The patterns are actually a highly complex, pragmatic visual cipher. Because the thick Delta fog obscures normal signs, the massive, spinning sails can be read from miles away through spyglasses.
- **The Strobe Effect:** The true genius of the cipher is that it must be read while _spinning_. The painted patterns create specific optical illusions or "strobe" effects when the windmill is in motion.
    - A sail flashing a rapid _red-and-white_ inner circle signals: _"A Ranidae (Frog-Kin) Apothecary resides here; medical aid available."_
    - A slow, pulsing _blue-and-yellow_ chevron signals: _"Safe harbor for trade; currently seeking livestock or goats."_
    - A stark _black-and-crimson_ spiral is a grim warning to neighboring homesteads: _"Rustlers or Corsair activity spotted; weapons hot."_

📝The Windmill Ascension and Odonata Rites of Maturity.

The Cycle of the Seventh Molt: The Windmill Ascension

The transformation from aquatic Naiad to airborne adult is the most crucial biological and cultural milestone in Odonata society. It represents the culmination of their 12-year aquatic existence and the beginning of a long, highly specialized adult life that spans three to four decades.

**The "Tightening" and the Ascent**When an Odonata Naiad reaches its twelfth year, it experiences a profound biological shift known as "The Tightening." Their aquatic, arm-driven bodies become stiff and lethargic as the adult form finishes developing beneath their final larval husk. Sensing this, the local homesteads halt their daily agricultural labor to prepare for the ascension.

Because the wetlands of the river delta are often shrouded in thick, toxic mists and fogs, sunlight at ground level is too weak to properly dry a newly emerged adult. To solve this, the Odonata use the very infrastructure the Naiads helped build. At the break of dawn, the Naiads drag themselves out of the Nursery Pools and latch onto the massive, woven cattail-cotton sails of the windmills. As the morning winds turn the gears, the Naiads ride the sails upward, ascending high above the misty canopy of the wetlands just as the sun breaches the horizon.

**The Emergence and First Flight**At the apex of the windmill's rotation, bathed in direct, intense sunlight, the 7th and final molting begins. The Naiads' husks split down the back, and the adult Odonata pull themselves free, unfurling their four iridescent wings and their newly developed, slender legs. The intense solar heat rapidly bakes their wet, fragile bodies, hardening their chitin into the resilient, glass-like armor required for survival. Once their wings are locked and dry, they launch themselves directly from the top of the windmill, taking their First Flight over the terraced Pearl-Rice paddies below.

The Sun-Catch Festival and Guild Scouting

The First Flight triggers the "Sun-Catch," a massive, raucous celebration that lasts for the remainder of the day and deep into the night. Because Odonata eggs are often laid in seasonal batches, it is common for entire generations of youths to emerge on the same morning, turning the sky into a dazzling display of erratic, high-speed acrobatics.

While the newly emerged adults celebrate their newfound freedom with music, feasts, and strong regional spirits, the older generation is hard at work. This party serves as an intense, informal scouting combine. Representatives from the various Odonata guilds discreetly watch the youths during their First Flight and the subsequent celebrations. They observe the youths' natural agility, visual acuity, and temperament to determine where they might best serve the homesteads:

- **The Blackstone Scouts:** Looking for youths with the sharpest compound eyesight and the steadiest hovering abilities to wield Blackstone rifles and guard the caravans.
- **The Pearl-Agronomists:** Scouting those with a deep, intuitive understanding of the water flow and weather patterns to manage the complex hydro-engineering of the rice terraces.
- **The Sail-Cryptographers:** Seeking out youths who demonstrate an eye for complex geometric patterns and an interest in the secretive dye-codes woven into the windmill sails.

The Morning of Declaration

The morning after the Sun-Catch Festival is a stark contrast to the prior day's high-energy celebrations. The newly emerged adults, exhausted and often nursing pounding hangovers from the night's festivities, are gathered at the base of the central windmills.

Here, the formal "Declaration" takes place. Having spent the previous day showing off their skills and being quietly interviewed by guild masters, each youth must step forward and publicly pledge themselves to a specific guild. This decision is monumental; Odonata are long-lived, and the guild they choose on this morning dictates their trade, their social circle, and their societal duties for the next thirty to forty years. Once the Declaration is made, they are handed the tools of their new trade—be it a farm implement, a weaver's shuttle, or a Blackstone rifle—and officially recognized as full, sovereign adults of the Delta.

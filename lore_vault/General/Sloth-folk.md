**Common Name:** [[Cultures/Sloth-Folk|Sloth-Folk]] / The Moss-Kin **Scientific Designation:** _Bradypus Aeturnus_ **Role:** Textile Masters, Archivists, Spider-Ranchers **Home City:** **The Slow-Vault** (A fortress-tree wrapped in layers of web and moss, where business is conducted at a glacial pace).

1.0 Biology & Appearance

- **Physiology:** Lanky and long-limbed, standing 6 to 7 feet tall when fully upright (though they usually slouch). They are covered in coarse, wire-like fur that naturally cultivates symbiotic green algae and moss, giving them natural camouflage.
- **The Grip:** Their hands end in three massive, curved keratin hooks (claws) that lock into place. Once a Bradypod grabs something, they do not need to use muscle energy to hold it. They can hang from a branch (or hold a grapple point) indefinitely, even while sleeping.
- **Metabolism:** Extremely slow. They eat very little and move deliberately. This low energy output makes them difficult to track with thermal vision or "Life-Sense" magic.
- **Durability:** Their internal organs are protected by a thick, rubbery hide under the fur. They can survive falls and blunt trauma that would shatter a human's ribs.

2.0 Psychology & Culture

- **The Philosophy of "Stasis":** To a Bradypod, rushing is a sign of incompetence. If you have to run, you planned poorly. They value **Consistency** over speed. They believe that if you build something correctly, you only have to build it once.
- **The "Long-View":** They think in decades, not days. A "short-term loan" from a Sloth bank is 50 years. This makes them the most stable bankers in the Canopy, immune to the panic-selling of the Monkeys.
- **Trust:** Because they cannot run away with your money (physically), they are trusted implicitly with the storage of gold and high-value contracts.

3.0 Industrial Role: The Silk-Weavers

- **The Spider Ranches:** They are the only species capable of farming the **Weaver-Cows** (Giant Spiders).
    - _The Logic:_ The spiders are predatory, reacting to fast movement. The Sloths move so slowly that the spiders do not register them as prey/threats. A Sloth can walk right up to a giant spider and milk its spinnerets without triggering an attack.
- **Textile Manufacturing:** They weave **"Iron-Weave" Canvas** and **Silk-Steel Cables**. These are the strongest materials in the setting, essential for airships and suspension bridges.
- **The Stasis-Vaults:** They sell "storage space" in their moss-lined vaults, which are alchemically treated to slow down rot and decay.

4.0 Military Role: The Living Wall

- **Style:** Static Defense / Grappling.
- **Weaponry:**
    - **The "Catch-Pole":** A long polearm with a mechanical man-catcher hook.
    - **Net-Guns:** Compressed air cannons that fire weighted spider-silk nets.
- **Tactics:** They do not charge. They hold ground. A unit of Sloths will lock their claws into the terrain and form an unbreakable shield wall. If an enemy gets close, the Sloth grabs them. Once grabbed, the enemy _stays_ grabbed until the battle is over.

5.0 Social Quirks

- **The "Pause":** Conversations with Sloths are agonizing for Foxes and Monkeys. A Sloth may pause for 30 seconds in the middle of a sentence to chew a leaf or think. Interrupting them is considered a grave insult.
- **Moss-Grooming:** They take great pride in the moss growing on their fur. A Sloth with a flourishing garden of rare moss on their back is seen as distinguished and high-status.
- **Sleeping:** They sleep 18 hours a day. Most "meetings" happen while the Sloth is technically half-asleep. They are famous for negotiating complex trade deals without fully opening their eye

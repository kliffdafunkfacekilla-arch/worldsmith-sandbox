# The Canopy Clans (The Invisible Empire)

**Aliases:** The Green Sea, The Twelve Crowns, The Chartered Millions **Location:** The Verdant Tangle (The World’s Largest Rainforest) **Patron Threat:** **[[Magistar Aurgenas]] ([[Magistar Aurgenas]])** **Power:** **[[Flux1]]** (Finesse, Liquidity, Gold) **Core Philosophy:** "Value rots if it sits. Keep it moving."

## 1.0 The Patron Influence: The Curse of Stagnation

[[Magistar Aurgenas]] was the Spymaster who turned the economy into a weapon. Now, as a Dragon of Molten Gold and Acid, he enforces a brutal economic law on the jungle.

- **The "Flux-Rot":** In the Verdant Tangle, **Inertia is Corrosive**.
    
    - If a building stands for too long without renovation, its supports dissolve.
        
    - If a pile of gold sits in a vault for a year without being counted or spent, it turns into caustic sludge.
        
- **The Adaptation:** The Clans are hyper-active. They constantly rebuild bridges, rotate leadership, and circulate currency. Their "Self-Interest" is a survival strategy to stay ahead of the rot.
    



## 1.1 The Fringe Consolidations: Canopy Clans

Originally bickering jungle tribes, they realized they sat on a global monopoly of Silk-Steel and Black-Stone. They formed the Twelve Crowns not out of harmony, but as a heavily armed corporate trust designed to price-gouge the Avians and the Caldera.

## 2.0 Geography: The Shifting Canopy

The jungle is alive and restless.

- **The Environment:** The trees are solid, but the _paths_ are fluid.
    
    - A vine-bridge that was safe yesterday might untie itself today.
        
    - A branch might migrate ten feet to the left overnight.
        
- **The Defense:** This makes the Clans uninvadable. Only the locals, with their high **Dexterity** and innate sense of "Flow," can navigate a city that rearranges itself.
    

## 3.0 Societal Structure: The Twelve Crowns

The Clans are a confederation of **12 Major City-States**, each dominated by a specific species and ruling over thousands of rural "Dukedoms."

### **The Hierarchy of Coin**

Governance is a Meritocracy of Wealth, driven by the fear of Aurgenas.

- **The King/Queen:** The wealthiest individual in the city.
    
- **The "Spending Crown":** To keep the crown, you must _circulate_ your wealth.
    
    - A miser King is dangerous; his hoard attracts the Dragon's attention and risks melting the city foundation.
        
    - A good King spends lavishly on public works (bridges, security, festivals) to keep the "Fiscal Flow" high. **This validates their self-interest:** They help the people _only_ to burn off their own excess wealth and stay in power.
        

## 4.0 The Major Cities (The Crowns)

1. **Vulpus-Prime (The Fox Capital):**
    
    - **Trade:** Diplomacy, Luxury Spices, Law.
        
    - **Vibe:** High-stakes negotiation parlors. They trade in "Favors" (Social Currency) which are immune to physical rot.
        
2. **Simia-Reach (The Primate Capital):**
    
    - **Trade:** Alchemical Fruit & Potions.
        
    - **Vibe:** Vertical markets that never sleep. They harvest the "Flux-Berries" that only grow on moving branches.
        
3. **The Grey Tangle (The Opossum Capital):**
    
    - **Trade:** Waste Management & Recycling.
        
    - **Vibe:** Industrial. They take the "Rot" (trash/waste) and turn it into fuel before Aurgenas can claim it.
        
4. **Glider's Roost (The Squirrel Capital):**
    
    - **Trade:** Courier Services & Resin.
        
    - **Vibe:** A transit hub. They are the fastest movers, essential for keeping goods circulating.
        

## 5.0 Military: The Vector-Guards

They fight a Dragon who can melt armor. Therefore, they don't use it.

- **Unit: The Kite-Runners**
    
    - **Gear:** Glider-suits and monofilament hooks.
        
    - **Tactic:** They fight in 3D. They never stand still (Static = Dead). They dive-bomb, hook, and drop enemies into the dense lower canopy.
        
- **Weaponry:**
    
    - **Glass-Daggers:** Obsidian blades that resist acid/corrosion.
        
    - **Blowguns:** Silent delivery of nerve-banes.
        

## 6.0 Foreign Relations: The Middlemen

- **Vs. [[The Iron Caldera]]:** The Caldera _needs_ the Clans. Metal rusts instantly in the Tangle, so the Ironborn hire Clan couriers to move goods through the region.
    
- **Vs. [[The Avian Empire]]:** The Avians hate the Tangle (it's too messy/shifting for their maps). The Clans charge them exorbitant "Guide Fees."
  
  # The Canopy Industrial Exchange 

**Producers:** The Twelve Crowns **Economic Model:** High-End Manufacturing & Exotic Agriculture.

## I. The "Boom" Industry (Arms & Explosives)

### 1. The Firearms (The Tarsiers)

**The Workshop:** "The Silent-Spring Armory"

- **The "Long-Eye" Jezzail:** A comically long, rifled musket used for sniping.
    
- **The "Brush-Clearer":** A short-barreled blunderbuss firing jagged rock/glass.
    
- **The "Volley-Pistol":** A three-barreled rotating pistol for self-defense.
    

### 2. Black-Stone Munitions (The Chiroptera / Bats)

**The Mine:** Deep Guano/Nitrate Caves.

- **"Canopy-Crackers":** Industrial blasting charges.
    
- **"Signal-Rockets":** Fireworks used for communication.
    
- **"Flash-Powder":** Blinding agents sold to the Prism-Scale.
    

## II. The Extractive Industry (Mines & Quarries)

### 3. Petrified "Stone-Root" (The Manis / Pangolins)

**The Source:** Deep mines finding fossilized roots of Ancestor Trees. **The Use:** Fireproof construction beams and heavy-duty gun stocks.

### 4. The Green-Slate Quarry (The Armadillos)

**The Source:** Open-pit mines. **The Use:** Waterproof roofing tiles and flagstones that resist moss.

## III. The Textile & Leather Industry

### 5. Viper-Leather (The Mungo / Mongooses)

**The Farm:** "[[Magistar Aurgenas]] Pits." **The Method:** They farm **Titan-Boas** (40ft snakes). **The Product:**

- **Shed-Skin:** Waterproof tarps.
    
- **Cull-Skin:** High-grade leather armor and luxury boots.
    

### 6. Spider-Silk Canvas (The Bradypod / Sloths)

**The Farm:** Farming **"Weaver-Cows"** (cattle-sized arachnids). **The Product:**

- **Silk-Steel:** High-tension cables.
    
- **Storm-Canvas:** Unrippable sailcloth.
    

## IV. The Artisan & Medical Guilds

### 7. Musical Instruments & Joinery (The Formicidae / Carpenter Ants)

**The Species:** Large, six-limbed ant-folk with massive, articulate mandibles. **The Craft:** They carve wood by chewing it. This allows them to create **Seamless Instruments** (Lutes and Violins carved from a single block of wood, hollowed out from the inside). **The Products:**

- **"Void-Lutes":** Instruments with perfect resonance because they have no glue joints.
    
- **Precision Gun-Furniture:** They carve the intricate wooden fittings for the Tarsier's rifles.
    
- **Puzzle-Boxes:** Unsolvable wooden safes used by merchants.
    

### 8. Medicines & Narcotics (The Ranidae / Frogs)

**The Products:**

- **"Clot-Moss":** Instant bandage.
    
- **"Fever-Bane":** Cure for jungle rot.
    
- **"Viper-Haze" (Illegal):** A smokeable combat drug (slows time perception).
    

## V. The Exotic Agriculture (Food & Drink)

### 9. The Poultry: "Terror-Fowl" (The Marsupials / Opossums)

**The Stock:** Farming **Velociraptor-like birds**. **The Yield:** High-protein meat, massive eggs, and rigid feathers for arrows.

### 10. The "Ice-Blood" Tea (The Ailurus / Red Pandas)

**The Crop:** Silver-Needle bushes. **The Utility:** A chemical coolant. Essential for factory workers in the [[Iron Caldera]] to prevent heatstroke.

### 11. Fungal Chocolate: "Shadow-Truffles" (The Simians / Monkeys)

**The Crop:** Sweet truffles that grow on Iron-Wood roots. **The Taste:** Earthy, savory dark chocolate. High calorie.

### 12. The Fruit Basket (The Simians / Monkeys)

**The Crop:**

- **Sun-Melons:** Hard-shelled natural canteens.
    
- **Iron-Pears:** Non-perishable fruit.
    
- **Whisper-Berries:** Carbonated/Fizzy fruit.
    

### 13. The Spice Rack (The Vulpines / Foxes)

**The Crop:**

- **"Crimson-Clove":** Numbing spice.
    
- **"Gold-Dust":** Honey-smoke saffron.
    
- **"Rot-Salt":** Meat preservative

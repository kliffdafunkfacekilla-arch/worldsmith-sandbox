# The Titan-Roach

**Common Name:** The Mountain Hauler, The Wall-Crawler, The Siege-Bug **Scientific Name:** _Blattodea gigas_ **Habitat:** Subterranean Nests / Summit-Walker Caravans **Role:** Beast of Burden / Heavy Cavalry Mount

> **"You laugh at the bug? That bug is pulling five tons of steel up a ninety-degree wall. And the Duck riding it? He’s wearing plate armor and holding a breacher-shield. Stop laughing and load the cannon."** — _Mercenary report on Avian Heavy Infantry_

### 1.0 Biology: The Organic Tank

A colossal insect, roughly the size of a draft horse, encased in a shimmering, oily chitinous shell.

- **Locomotion:** It possesses six thick, spined legs. The feet are equipped with millions of microscopic setae (hairs) that utilize Van der Waals force, allowing the creature to climb vertical stone surfaces—and even overhangs—while carrying a load.
    
- **Diet:** They are **Detritivores**. They eat organic waste, rotting wood, and garbage. This makes them incredibly cheap to maintain compared to horses, which require grain.
    

### 2.0 The "Iron-Shell" Cavalry

While the Summit-Walkers use them to pull wagons, the **Flightless Avians** (Ducks/Chickens/Geese variants) have weaponized them.

- **The Mount:** Because Titan-Roaches are stable and lock onto surfaces, they are the perfect mount for heavy infantry who cannot fly.
    
- **Combat Doctrine:**
    
    - **The Wall-Breachers:** Flightless heavy infantry ride Titan-Roaches _up_ the walls of enemy fortifications.
        
    - **The Loadout:** The Roach is fitted with a "Mag-Lock Saddle" (using weak lodestones) to keep the rider seated when upside down.
        
    - **Intimidation:** The sight of a battalion of giant cockroaches scuttling down a ceiling to drop armored shock-troops behind enemy lines is a psychological weapon in itself.

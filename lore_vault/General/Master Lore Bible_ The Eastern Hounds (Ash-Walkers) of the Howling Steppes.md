#### 3. The Economy of the Now: Survival via Strategic Amnesia

Survival within a high-density temporal-bleed zone necessitates the total abandonment of long-term planning, as memory itself acts as a liability that anchors the mind to traumatic echoes. The Eastern Hounds have adapted by instituting an "Economy of the Now," prioritizing immediate utility over the accumulation of historical or economic value.

##### The Currency of Forgetfulness

The Hounds utilize  **Grave-Berries**  as their primary socio-economic agent. These potent alchemical fruits are used to facilitate voluntary amnesia. By systematically excising their own memory-cogs, the Hounds create an "Eternal Now," shielding their neural networks from the psychological collapse inherent in witnessing overlapping timelines. This process ensures the clan functions with the cold efficiency of a machine without memory, unburdened by the trauma of the past or the omens of the future.

#### 4. Shamanic Caste: The Grave-Touched and the Cinder-Haunts

The spiritual hierarchy of the Ash-Walkers is a diagnostic ranking of an individual's ability to interface with  **Cinder-Haunts**  (temporal ghosts) without suffering a total logic-collapse.

**The Grave-Touched Shamans:**  These practitioners have survived the initiation ritual within the  **Cave of Whispers** . They possess the specialized ability to bind Cinder-Haunts, utilizing these temporal data-shards for regional defense and scouting.

**Rotting Vessels:**  These represent a total failure of the initiation. When a soul is consumed during the ritual, the biological form remains as a "Rotting Vessel." These are hollowed-out flesh-puppets for insane, ancient spirits. This process represents a total erasure of the individual’s identity cogs, leaving a biological machine piloted by a furious, entropic intelligence.

##### Threat Profile: The Cult of the Black Star

The Cult of the Black Star is a rogue faction of failed shamans who practice a clinical and horrific form of necro-cannibalism. By consuming the flesh of Rotting Vessels, they absorb the concentrated temporal echoes and the residual rage of thousands of dead souls. #### 6. The Industrial Utility of the Ash-Bison

The Ash-Bison ( *Bison Cinereus* ) is the biological fuel that keeps the Hound machine operational in a resource-barren wasteland.

**The Stone-Hide:**  The bison wallow in volcanic ash and mineral mud, which mats into their wool and hardens into a natural "Concrete Coat." The Hounds tan this skin with the ash-wool attached to create shields and yurt coverings that function as light plate armor, rendering them entirely arrow-proof.

**The Meat:**  Due to a mineral-heavy diet, the meat is naturally salty and lean. It is processed into a high-stability jerky that requires no further preservation, fueling the clans during high-velocity migrations.

**The Bone-Lumber:**  In the treeless expanse of the Steppes, the massive rib-cages of the bison are repurposed as structural "lumber" for tent frames and tool handles, providing the necessary rigid supports for nomadic habitation.

#### 7. Martial Traditions: The Spine-Leap

Conventional long-range weaponry is non-functional against the Ash-Bison, as arrows bounce off the "Stone-Hide" without piercing the underlying tissue. To harvest these beasts, the Hounds have developed the  **Spine-Leap** , a maneuver requiring precise mechanical calibration.

##### Tactical Briefing: The Spine-Leap

**Synchronization:**  Thunder-Strider riders pull parallel to a galloping "Thunder-Herd," matching the herd's velocity to negate relative motion.

**Harmonic Observation:**  The hunter waits for the "harmonic opening"—the exact micro-second in the bison's galloping gait where the stone-wool plates shift.

**The Leap:**  The hunter leaps from the saddle onto the moving bison’s back.

**The Extraction:**  A specialized dagger is driven into the narrow gap at the spine to sever the spinal cord. This is the ultimate test of reflex and the primary rite of passage for young Ash-Walkers.

#### 8. Cultural Taboos and Folklore

Hound society is governed by strict superstitions regarding their avian companions, which they view as early-warning sensors for the regional machine.

**The Taboo of the Hen:**  Consuming a Somoan Wood-Hen is viewed as a systemic failure of the clan’s spiritual integrity. They are "Bundles of Rage" that serve as the first line of defense; eating one is equivalent to dismantling a vital security sensor.

**Spectral Perception:**  When a Wood-Hen shrieks at "empty air," the Hounds interpret this as the detection of a Cinder-Haunt invisible to humanoid eyes. Such an alert triggers an immediate relocation of the camp to avoid a spectral incursion and regional timeline bleeding.

The Eastern Hounds represent a unique cultural adaptation to a localized reality-failure. They remain a self-contained, isolated entity, existing as the outer-guardians of the Black Star's prison, frozen in a clinical, eternal present by the very instability they seek to navigate.

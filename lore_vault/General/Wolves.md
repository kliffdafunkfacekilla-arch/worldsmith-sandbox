**⚔️ Faction Profile: The Marshals**

*(The Iron-Pack / The Moon-Guard / The Fangs)*

"We do not serve the Crown. We protect it. There is a difference. The Moon broke because we looked away for one moment. We will never look away again."  
— Prime Alpha Vulfen, The One-Who-Won-Six

## ---

**1.0 Overview: The Penitent Guardians**

The Wolves and Coyotes are the Military and Police force of the Heartland. They are not subservient to the Horse Nobles; they are an independent power structure dedicated to the safety of the Nation itself.

* **The Oath:** They serve from birth to death. Every Wolf is born into the regiment. There are no civilians in the Pack.  
* **The Tone:** They are not savage beasts holding back a curse. They are disciplined, cultured soldiers who love the hunt, the opera, and the law.  
* **The Relationship:** They protect the Nobility, but they do not obey them blindly. A General may grant a Duke a favor, but the Duke cannot order the General to march.

## ---

**2.0 Religion: The Cult of the Shattered Moon**

Their entire society is built on a single, crushing cultural trauma.

* **The Failure:** Wolf folklore states that eons ago, the Wolves were the original Guardians of the Sky. They failed in their duty, and as punishment, the Moon was shattered.  
* **The Penance:** They swore a holy vow to protect the "Earth-Bound" (the weak) so that such a catastrophe never happens again. They view their service as religious atonement.  
* **guidance or blessings:** on the full moon or new moon or half waned , halved waxed, etc. depending on there exact situation is , a complex formula of subject, goal , current status, sex, age, and rank, even the last meal you had, or if you ever stepped on a crawfish before,etc. All factors into when you should howl or whimper at the temple, in triumph or penance.

#### **The Clergy:**

The clergy are priests led by an alpha priest, they are called by the moon to serve and seek the temple and are tested and initiated into the clergy. Called whelps until again the moon blesses them and they are anointed priests. Alpha is decided by fatal one on one combat under a full moon with ceremonial moon daggers(half moon shaped blades with the jagged broken bits carefully replicated making them savage and so damaging their wounds are always fatal or at least maiming and crippling the victim (maiming a wolf and leaving him to live is the most horrifying thing you could do, its only thought to have been done twice  once to the evil twin in there folk lore who becomes the antagonist who cursed them to be mortal and once to an Alpa who led a slaughter on his own sacred charges and his beta stopped him and left him blind and hobbled among the surviving victims).  Normally this is done ceremonially by a chosen successor when the alpha grows old and feeble. What makes these wolves leave there pack  suddenly and join the church is not known. The testing and initiation are a secret as well and many who heed the call are never seen again.

**3.0 Structure: The Alpha Seven**

Leadership is determined by a brutal meritocracy of Body and Mind. The High Command consists of **7 Alphas**.

**The Selection:**

*  **Witness**: Is chosen by both to vouch for their honor and the fairness of the challenge when its over.   
* **3 Alphas of the Body:** Chosen through physical combat tournaments.  
1. Armed combat until first blood and yield. 2 of three with each round being one combatants weapon choice and the third if needed chosen by [[Valen the Witness]]   
2. A stalking type challenge, 4 of 7 winner is successful  doing the stalking and survives being stalked twice each to win. Can take days sometimes to complete.  
3. Unarmed one on one till submission  or unconsciousness.  
* **3 Alphas of the Mind:** Chosen through complex strategy games, riddles, and tactical simulations.  
1. A 4 out of 7 riddle contest, quite intense as an hour is given to answer for each riddle.  
2. A 2 out of 3  match of Moonstones (chess like chinese checkers hybrid with multi colored stones and strategic board positions).  
3. A  debate and judgement challenge, grievances are presented and each makes judgement and then they argue against the other's case with literary quotes, poetry recitals, anecdotes and rebuttals and objections. Until one concedes twice. The fact many grievances are heard before they find a contested one  makes this about endurance as well.  
* **The Prime Alpha (The 7th):** To hold the supreme seat, one must defeat all 6 other Alphas in their respective fields (3 in combat, 3 in wits). This can be over time and can still count a win even if they have lost to that alpha since the win as long as the prime alpha hasn't changed. So there is always a tally of challenges and maneuvering to break streaks or throwing matches , secret alliances to keep one alpha out of the prime,etc.

**The Challenge:**

* Rank is fluid. At any time, an Alpha can challenge another to switch seats.  
* *Example:* The Prime Alpha might be challenged to a game of "Moon-Chess" by the 3rd Alpha of Mind. If the Prime loses, they swap ranks.

**4.0 The Moon-Blessed (Special Forces)**

Magic users are rare and highly organized. They do not serve in the regular infantry.

* **The Goal:** To form a **"Perfect Pack"** of 12 Moon-Blessed Wolves, containing one of each magical affinity.  
* **The Role:** A Perfect Pack acts as the autonomous agents of an Alpha—spies, investigators, and secret police.  
* **The Shame of the Half-Pack:**  
  * Ideally, there are 7 Perfect Packs (one for each Alpha).  
  * In reality, they are often short a few members. These "Half-Packs" are terrified of insulting the Moon with their incompleteness, so they only perform minor tasks until they find their missing members.

## ---

**5.0 Culture: Poets & Tournaments**

Wolves are not just soldiers; they are **Warrior-Poets**.

* **The Arts:** They have a surprising love for high culture. A fierce Captain is just as likely to be famous for his critique of a classic Opera or his poetry as he is for his swordsmanship.  
* **The Rotation:**  
  * **The Week:** Packs get one week off twice a year.  
  * **The Month:** Every 5 years, a Pack gets a full month off.  
* **The Festival of the Hunt:**  
  * When a Pack is on its "Month Off," they don't rest. They host a massive Tournament and Festival for the other Packs currently on their "Week Off."  
  * **Breeding:** This is when the strongest bloodlines are merged. Pups are conceived during these festivals to ensure the next generation is stronger and smarter.

## ---

**6.0 The Coyotes: The Internal Watch**

Coyotes are technically part of the same structure, but their role is distinct.

* **The Role:** Because they are smaller, they handle **Internal Security**—town patrols, granary guards, and dock inspections.  
* **The Corruption Trap:**  
  * Coyotes are the ones dealing with the **Otters** (Smugglers) and **Rats** (Bankers). They often take bribes.  
  * **The Secret:** The Wolf Alphas *know* this. They allow the Coyotes to play "dirty" because it gives the Alphas eyes on the criminal underworld. The Coyote who takes a bribe is also reporting exactly who paid it.  
* **The Legend:** Only one Coyote has ever cracked the "Alpha Seven." His name is lost to history, but stories say he won not by strength, but by a trick so clever the Prime Alpha stepped down laughing.

## ---

**7.0 The Rogues: The Packless**

Not every pup fits the mold.

* **[[The Exodus]]:** Some Wolves are born too wild, too solitary, or simply disagree with the strict "Moon Cult." They leave the service young.  
* **The Life:** They wander the world seeking a "Pseudo-Pack." You find these Rogue Wolves as:  
  * **Airship Marines:** Craving the sky their ancestors failed to protect.  
  * **Mercenaries:** Selling their sword for coin rather than honor.  
  * **Circus Performers:** Using their natural agility for applause.  
* **The Stigma:** To a loyal Marshal, a Rogue is tragic—a soul wandering without the Moon's guidance.

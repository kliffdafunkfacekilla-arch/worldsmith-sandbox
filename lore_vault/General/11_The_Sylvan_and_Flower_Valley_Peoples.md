# The Sylvan Empire (The March of the Ancients)

**Aliases:** The Living Bastion, The Slow Council, The Wood-Wrights **Location:** The Emerald Scar (The Moving Forest) **Patron Threat:** **[[The Silent Echo]]** ([[Magistar Vecelo]]) – Dragon of Perpetual Acceleration & Silence. **Core Philosophy:** "Speed is a brittle cage. We do not run, for to run is to be chased. We stand, and the storm breaks against us."

## 1.0 Biology: The Cycle of the Seed

The Sylvan are a dimorphic species where "Age" literally equals "Mass."

### **Phase 1: The Sapling (The Mobile Caste)**

- **Duration:** 0 to ~500 Years.
    
- **Physiology:** Humanoid, 5–8 feet tall. Their "skin" is a flexible, green-grey bark. They are fully ambulatory.
    
- **Diet:** They engage in "passive eating." They require daily sunlight and water, but they supplement this with nutrient-dense fruits. They do not eat meat or grain.
    
- **Role:** These are the diplomats, the soldiers, the crafters, and the adventurers. To an outsider, a 400-year-old Sylvan warrior looks like an ancient, scarred veteran, but to the Empire, they are just hitting their prime.

##### 1.1 The Schism of the Scar (Sylvan vs. Flower Valley)

During the early Feral Epoch, the botanical ecosystem was united. But as the supersonic, matter-shattering chaos of Magistar Vecelo intensified, a bitter ideological divorce occurred. The Sylvan Elders chose to uproot, beginning the eternal migration of Arbor-Prime to outrun the sonic vacuum. A splinter group viewed this as cowardice. They traveled to the "Engineer's Legs"—a high-tension point of the dead god's anatomy—and intentionally rooted themselves there to fulfill the divine mandate to "Guard the prisons". Mutating into the stationary Blossom, Shrub, and Vine castes, the Flower Valley Folk mastered hospitality as a weapon of defensive espionage, forever viewing the wandering Sylvan as cowards.
    

### **Phase 2: The Elder (The Ruling Caste)**

- **Duration:** 500 to ~800 Years.
    
- **Physiology:** They have begun the "Thickening." Movement becomes difficult and slow. Their bark hardens into armor.
    
- **Role:** The Governance. They can still move, but rarely do. They serve as the bridge between the fast-paced world of the Saplings and the deep-time consciousness of the Ancients. They make the final executive decisions.
    

### **Phase 3: The Ancient (The Infrastructure)**

- **The Transformation:** At the end of their mobile life, a Sylvan chooses a spot and "Roots." Their legs fuse into the earth. They expand rapidly, becoming colossal trees.
    
- **State:** They are **Immobile** but **Conscious**. They form the literal buildings, walls, and roadways of the capital.
    
- **Communication:** They communicate via the "Root-Hum" (Sub-sonic vibration). They act as the hard-drive of the civilization, storing history and advising the Elders.
    

## 2.0 The Capital: Arbor-Prime (The City That Walks)

To an outsider, the capital looks like a dense, fortified forest. To a local, it is a metropolis.

- **Living Architecture:** There are no masons. The "Buildings" are the hollowed chest cavities of willing Ancients. The "Roads" are the interlocking, flattened roots of the ancestors.
    
- **The Great Migration:** The city is not stationary.
    
    - **The Movement:** Through a coordinated process of root-growth and soil-shifting, the entire forest of Ancients is moving **North**.
        
    - **The Speed:** It moves perhaps 10 feet a year.
        
    - **The Purpose:** They are moving away from the expanding **Zone of Silence** created by [[Magistar Vecelo]]. It is a strategic retreat played out over millennia.
        

## 3.0 Industry: The Coaxers & The Iron-Wood

The Sylvan are the world's premier woodworkers, but they do not use saws.

- **The Art of "Coaxing":** Master Wood-Wrights use minor nature magic and physical tension to _train_ a tree to grow into a specific shape.
    
    - _Furniture:_ A chair isn't carved; it is grown.
        
    - _Ship Hulls:_ They grow the keels of airships in a single, seamless piece, making them stronger than any nailed timber.
        
- **Export 1: Iron-Wood:** The calcified timber of a deceased Ancient. It is as hard as steel but lighter. It is the most expensive construction material in the world.
    
- **Export 2: The Living Books:**
    
    - The Sylvan produce the world's finest paper and binding.
        
    - _The Sylvan Tome:_ A book made by the Empire is water-resistant, fire-resistant, and the paper feels like silk. The [[Order of the Clockwork]] and [[The Fulcrum Academy]] pay fortunes for these blank tomes to record their research.
        
- **Trade Relations:** They trade rare fruits and these crafted goods for **Alchemical Medicines** (to treat blight/rot) and **Dyes** (from the [[Avian Empire]]).
    

## 4.0 Military: The War Against Silence

They fight a defensive war against **[[Magistar Vecelo]]**, the Dragon of Motion who steals sound and shatters matter with speed. The Sylvan defense is built on **Resonance** and **Friction**.

### **The Warrior Castes (Species Variants)**

Sylvan biology varies like trees vary.

1. **The Oak-Guard (Heavy Infantry):**
    
    - **Traits:** Massive, broad, and covered in thick, craggy bark.
        
    - **Tactic:** "The Rumble." They lock arms and root temporarily. They drum on their own hollow chests, creating a massive, low-frequency **Bass Wall**. This sonic vibration disrupts the "Silent" magic of the Dragon's spawn, forcing sound back into the area.
        
2. **The Willow-Dancers (Skirmishers):**
    
    - **Traits:** Slender, flexible, with whip-like branches growing from their forearms.
        
    - **Tactic:** They use their razor-sharp branch-whips to disarm opponents. They are the only Sylvan fast enough to intercept the "Blur-Beasts" (spawn of [[Magistar Vecelo]]).
        
3. **The Briar-Hulks (Shock Troops):**
    
    - **Traits:** Covered in 3-inch poisonous thorns.
        
    - **Tactic:** They charge into the enemy line. To hit them is to hurt yourself.
        

### **Siege Warfare: The Grown Artillery**

They do not build catapults; they grow them.

- **The Ballista-Tree:** A specialized tree grown with immense internal tension.
    
    - **Ammo:** It fires a bolt the size of a telephone pole (a sharpened Iron-Wood log).
        
    - **Power:** It can punch a hole through the hull of an [[Iron Caldera]] dreadnought.
        
- **The Root-Pitcher:** Massive, animated root systems that can pick up a 2-ton rock and hurl it with terrifying accuracy.
    

## 5.0 Weaknesses & Fears

- **Fire:** Their greatest fear. They view Fire (and by extension, the **[[Coastal Theocracy]]** who worship [[Magistar Virantor]], [[Magistar Virantor]]) with absolute horror. A Sylvan will retreat from a forest fire, but they will execute an arsonist without trial.
    
- **Speed:** They struggle to react to rapid strategic shifts. Their OODA loop (Observe-Orient-Decide-Act) is slow because they consult the Ancients for every major war move.

### An In-Depth Exposé: The Flower Valley Folk
![[Vine Caste.jpg]]

#### 1.0 War-Council Missive: The Carefree Warriors

The Flower Valley Folk stand as the deceptively serene guardians of a vital Chaos Zone trade hub, a role they fulfill as part of a sacred, divine mandate. Their entire societal structure—a complex blend of open hospitality and profound suspicion—is a direct cultural adaptation to their evolution in proximity to the chaos dragon Lophex, The Geometric Void. This origin has forged them into peerless masters of deception and subtle control, allowing them to maintain their prosperous society while fulfilling their true, hidden purpose.

At their core, the Flower Valley Folk embody the paradox of the "Carefree Warriors," a civilization built upon the twin foundational values of **Defense & Hospitality**. Their worldview has been indelibly shaped by the cunning dragon Lophex, whose particular brand of chaos is not overt destruction but **Sudden Ruin**—catastrophe delivered through targeted infrastructure failures, unexpected tidal shifts, and political backstabbing. Because the threat manifests not as overt force but as betrayal from trusted structures and allies, their entire defensive posture is built on social infiltration and preemptive intelligence. They counter political backstabbing with a culture of radical transparency and counter infrastructure failure by maintaining a ubiquitous, observant workforce that understands every logistical component of their home. Generations of exposure to this insidious influence have instilled in their culture a single, unshakable tenet: "never trust their eyes." This principle is the key to understanding their unique social hierarchy.

#### 2.0 The Three-Caste System: A Symbiosis of Deception and Defense

The society of the Flower Valley Folk is built upon a rigid, three-caste system that functions as the living fulfillment of a divine mandate. This structure is not merely a social hierarchy but a highly effective, integrated security instrument where each caste performs a distinct and vital role:

##### I. The Blossom Caste: Matriarchy and High Intrigue
The vibrant Blossom Caste serves as the rulers, merchants, and ambassadors of the valley, managing all overt interactions with outsiders. They utilize overwhelming hospitality and charm as a weapon, engaging in unseen, delayed maneuvering—a carefully planted whisper today might result in a political rival being exiled for fabricated crimes four months later.
* **The Matriarchal Estates:** The Blossoms are a strictly matriarchal society. A high-ranking Blossom woman will take multiple husbands, maintaining a different husband for each of her seasonal estates. Because there are often more men than there are seasons, competition is fierce.
* **The Husband Crews:** A Blossom does not marry for romance alone; she marries for operational efficiency. Each husband is given charge of an estate, effectively building a "crew" of skilled men to manage her vast affairs, finances, and lands while she engages in the high-stakes political and social dances of the capital.
* **The Weaponization of Wine and Silk:** The Blossoms are the premier secret brokers of Ostraka. They cultivate the finest crops, weave the world's most famous gowns, and utilize their legendary wines as the primary social lubricant to loosen the tongues of visiting diplomats.
* **The Scandal of the Hidden Truth:** Their culture actively rewards the public exposure of secrets. Blossom marriages are massive, highly secretive outings where the women deploy their networks to gather all possible intelligence. In this society, a husband keeping a secret is not just a betrayal of trust—it is a catastrophic social scandal. If a husband is discovered to know a truth that his Blossom wife failed to uncover herself, it implies she is losing her edge, inviting immediate social ruin.

##### II. The Shrub Caste: The Bureaucracy of Silence
While they project the appearance of simple laborers and servants to outsiders, the Shrub Caste are the true eyes and ears of the valley. They are, in fact, a massive, highly complex bureaucratic caste that quietly manages the state's logistics.
* **The Reversed Power Dynamic:** While the Blossoms gain power by exposing secrets, the Shrubs gain power by keeping them. For a Shrub clerk, successfully hiding a ledger or concealing an administrative truth is the ultimate mark of skill and bureaucratic supremacy.
* **Androgyny and Etiquette:** The Shrub Caste is entirely unbothered by gender. They are visually androgynous, and within their culture, it is considered deeply rude to ask a Shrub to define themselves as male or female.
* **Exponential Group Marriages:** Their familial structures are a logistical puzzle. Two Shrubs will marry, and that couple will then seek out another married pair to join with, forming a family of four. This group of four will then marry another group of four, creating a household of eight. This is the general cap, as achieving consensus among more than eight individuals is nearly impossible.
* **Communal Cultivation:** These eight-person households share all responsibilities. They have very low birth rates, relying on a complex, highly structured internal system to determine who pollinates whom, who acts as the primary parents, and who schools the offspring.

##### III. The Vine Caste: The Stoic Vanguard
Residing along the mountain foothills and jagged cliffs, the thorny Vine Caste serves as the military, the police, the heavy muscle, and the final line of defense for the Folk's sacred charge.
* **Lifespans of Stone and Timber:** They are incredibly long-lived, granting them the patience of ancient trees. They do not marry, and they eschew the social complexities of the other castes. While they gather for community meals and events, they remain eerily stoic and silent.
* **Tactical Spore Communication:** Their silence is not a lack of communication, but a tactical advantage. The Vine Caste operates on a doctrine of unseen deterrence, communicating complex military maneuvers and patrols entirely through the release of spores and seed fluffs. This creates an invisible, un-interceptable command network that makes their military maneuvers flawless and silent.

#### 3.0 Cultural Bedrock: The Celebration of Uncovered Secrets

The Folk weaponize their cultural rituals as a perpetual training apparatus, honing the skills necessary for their unique brand of unseen warfare. Having learned that surface appearances are always deceiving, they have built a culture that actively rewards the uncovering of secrets. This serves as a constant training ground for the entire populace, reinforcing the observational and deductive skills necessary for their survival. Even their physical organization reflects this, as they arrange themselves in "orderly gardens groups to avoid surprise."

This philosophy is most vividly expressed in their unique coming-of-age ritual, which is structured as an elaborate party and a high-stakes intelligence test. This ritual is the primary training ground for all castes:
* **The Premise:** The ritual centers on gift-giving. Of the twenty attendees at the party, five are secretly designated to bring gifts. These individuals, along with the nature of their gifts, are kept in the utmost secrecy. In the weeks leading up to the event, the intended recipient must endeavor to discover these secrets.
* **The Objective:** Success is not merely discovering the five gift-givers. To truly demonstrate their skill, the subject is expected to uncover other hidden information about the attendees. A future Blossom learns the art of social intelligence and manipulation. A future Shrub hones skills in quiet observation and pattern recognition. A future Vine practices the core tenets of infiltration and secret-gathering.
* **The Societal Impact:** This ritual has profound social consequences. These parties have famously exposed the secret affairs and clandestine dealings of powerful leaders. This outcome is culturally accepted as a **good thing**, even by those who are exposed. It reveals a radical cultural belief in enforced transparency, where the health and security of the society—and its sacred duty—are prioritized above individual secrecy. For the Flower Valley Folk, a secret brought to light is a threat neutralized.

#### 4.0 The Autonomous Minorities

The Flower Valley is not inhabited by flora alone. It serves as a sanctuary for several distinct animal-kin species. While these groups fall under the ultimate jurisdiction of the Flower Folk government and can petition officials with grievances, they possess no direct political representation. They are permitted to govern and manage their own communities autonomously in exchange for their specialized trades:
* **The Odonata (Dragonflies):** These ranchers operate within the wetlands, providing specialized labor and aerial capabilities.
* **The Eastern Butterflies:** Renowned agriculturalists who farm the valley's exquisite silks and cultivate its high-altitude teas.
* **The Small Cats:** Communities of short-haired and calico felines settled on the western edges of the territory. They maintain offshore fishing operations and farm small bovines and bisons for dairy production.
* **The Southern Otters:** Small enclaves of river-faring otters who manage the southern waterways, acting as a discrete buffer between the valley and the River Syndicate.
* **The Micro-Myconids (Small Mushroom-Folk):** These tiny fungal beings have integrated themselves deeply into the valley's service and entertainment industries.
    * **The Food & Forge:** They are the master bakers and butchers of the hub. Surprisingly, they are also peerless blacksmiths. Due to their minute size, they do not forge weapons; they forge intricate, impossibly fine metalwork—jewelry, fishing hooks, tiny nails, and surgical needles.
    * **The Theatrical Arts:** They possess beautiful, resonant voices. They have formed famous theater troupes and singing choirs that provide the primary evening entertainment for the valley's visiting dignitaries.

#### 5.0 Geopolitical & Cosmological Context

##### 5.1 The Schism of the Scar (Sylvan vs. Flower Valley)
During the early Feral Epoch, the botanical ecosystem was united. But as the supersonic, matter-shattering chaos of Magistar Vecelo intensified, a bitter ideological divorce occurred. The Sylvan Elders chose to uproot, beginning the eternal migration of Arbor-Prime to outrun the sonic vacuum. A splinter group viewed this as cowardice. They traveled to the "Engineer's Legs"—a high-tension point of the dead god's anatomy—and intentionally rooted themselves there to fulfill the divine mandate to "Guard the prisons". Mutating into the stationary Blossom, Shrub, and Vine castes, the Flower Valley Folk mastered hospitality as a weapon of defensive espionage, forever viewing the wandering Sylvan as cowards.

##### 5.2 The Global Web (Geopolitical Standing)
The strategic importance of the Flower Valley Folk is magnified by their sacred and perilous geographical location. They do not merely inhabit a valley; they reside within the very landscape formed by a slumbering god, placing them at the nexus of divine history and ongoing chaos. Their home is the sheltered valley formed by the outspread legs and knees of the slumbering god known as **"The Engineer,"** whose divine sacrifice was instrumental in containing the cataclysm that created the Shattered World. The life that grew in this sacred area was imprinted with the Engineer's final, fading duty: **"Guard the prisons."** Thus, the Flower Valley Folk are not just inhabitants; they are the living fulfillment of a divine mandate. Furthermore, their proximity to **"The Convergence"**—the ground zero of the world-shattering cataclysm and the epicenter of the world's chaos—places them on the frontline of reality's defense. 

By acting as avid traders, eager diplomatic facilitators, and offering their lands as a famous, luxurious vacation destination, the Flower Valley Folk willingly invite the most powerful, wealthy, and loose-lipped individuals in Ostraka directly into their parlor. They are connected to an intelligence web like no other faction. While the world believes they are simply enjoying the wine and the theater of the Mushroom-Folk, the Blossoms are mapping their weaknesses, the Shrubs are burying the ledgers of their debts, and the Vines are watching their every move from the cliffs. Espionage is not just their skill; it is their industry. Any geopolitical engagement that underestimates their deceptive capabilities or misunderstands their sacred duty is doomed to catastrophic failure.

# The Ailurus (The Mist-Walkers)

**Common Name:** Red Panda-Folk

**Scientific Designation:** _Ailurus Fulgens Sapiens_

**Role:** Tea-Masters, Spies, Chemical Agriculturalists

**Home City:** **The Lantern-Bough** (A tranquil district of bamboo platforms, steam-vents, and paper lanterns, perpetually shrouded in high-altitude mist).

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance

- **Physiology:** Short and stout, standing 3.5 to 4.5 feet tall. They have a distinct "tear-drop" marking of dark fur under their eyes.
    
- **The Coat:** Their fur is thick, russet-red, and water-repellent, evolved for the damp, misty upper-canopy.
    
- **Movement:** They possess thick, woolly paw-pads that act as natural mufflers. An Ailurus can walk across a creaky wooden floor without making a sound.
    
- **The "False Thumb":** Like their feral ancestors, they have a modified wrist bone that acts as a sixth digit. This allows for incredible dexterity in handling delicate tea-ware, needles, or lockpicks.
    

## 2.0 Psychology & Culture

- **The Philosophy of "The Empty Cup":** They believe that power is best exercised invisibly. They do not shout like Simians or threaten like Mungos. They serve tea, they listen, and they gently guide the outcome.
    
- **Weaponized Cuteness:** They are fully aware that other species find them non-threatening. They exploit this ruthlessly. An Ailurus server in a tea house is invisible to the eyes of a Fox or Wolf warlord, allowing them to gather the most valuable intel in the jungle.
    
- **Pacifism (with a caveats):** They view violence as a "spilled cup"—a mess that takes too long to clean up. They prefer to solve problems through blackmail, poisoning, or economic pressure.
    

## 3.0 Industrial Role: The Coolant Cartel

- **The Silver-Needle Crop:** They farm the **"Ice-Blood" Tea** bushes on the highest, coldest peaks of the Tangle.
    
- **The Monopoly:** This tea is the only reliable way to lower body temperature chemically without magic.
    
- **The Client:** **The Iron Caldera**. The foundry workers drink gallons of Ailurus tea daily. Without it, the Caldera's labor force would collapse from heatstroke. This gives the Ailurus massive leverage over the world's greatest military power.
    

## 4.0 Military Role: The Fog-Wardens

- **Style:** Denial and Disorientation.
    
- **Weaponry:**
    
    - **The "Boiling-Flask":** Pressurized ceramic gourds filled with super-heated tea or chemical fog.
        
    - **The Bamboo Staff:** A heavy, iron-shod cane used for blocking and joint-locks.
        
- **Tactics:** If attacked, they don't charge. They shatter "Fog-Pots" to create an instant white-out cloud, blinding the enemy. They then use their silent movement to slip away or strike nerve clusters with their staffs.
    

## 5.0 Social Quirks

- **Vegetarian Judgment:** They are strictly herbivorous (bamboo shoots, roots, tea leaves). While they serve meat to guests (because it’s profitable), they will often watch a carnivore eat with a look of mild, silent disappointment.
    
- **The Pour:** Pouring tea is a sacred language.
    
    - _Full to the brim:_ "You are welcome here."
        
    - _Half-full:_ "State your business and leave."
        
    - _Spilling a drop:_ A grave insult, equivalent to a slap in the face.
        
- **Startle Reflex:** Despite their calm demeanor, if surprised by a sudden loud noise, they instinctively throw their hands up (the "Surrender Pose") to look bigger. They are deeply embarrassed by this.

## Sloth Folk

#  The Bradypod (The Time-Keepers)
**Common Name:** Sloth-Folk / The Moss-Kin
**Scientific Designation:** *Bradypus Aeturnus*
**Role:** Textile Masters, Archivists, Spider-Ranchers
**Home City:** **The Slow-Vault** (A fortress-tree wrapped in layers of web and moss, where business is conducted at a glacial pace).

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **Physiology:** Lanky and long-limbed, standing 6 to 7 feet tall when fully upright (though they usually slouch). They are covered in coarse, wire-like fur that naturally cultivates symbiotic green algae and moss, giving them natural camouflage.
* **The Grip:** Their hands end in three massive, curved keratin hooks (claws) that lock into place. Once a Bradypod grabs something, they do not need to use muscle energy to hold it. They can hang from a branch (or hold a grapple point) indefinitely, even while sleeping.
* **Metabolism:** Extremely slow. They eat very little and move deliberately. This low energy output makes them difficult to track with thermal vision or "Life-Sense" magic.
* **Durability:** Their internal organs are protected by a thick, rubbery hide under the fur. They can survive falls and blunt trauma that would shatter a human's ribs.

## 2.0 Psychology & Culture
* **The Philosophy of "Stasis":** To a Bradypod, rushing is a sign of incompetence. If you have to run, you planned poorly. They value **Consistency** over speed. They believe that if you build something correctly, you only have to build it once.
* **The "Long-View":** They think in decades, not days. A "short-term loan" from a Sloth bank is 50 years. This makes them the most stable bankers in the Canopy, immune to the panic-selling of the Monkeys.
* **Trust:** Because they cannot run away with your money (physically), they are trusted implicitly with the storage of gold and high-value contracts.

## 3.0 Industrial Role: The Silk-Weavers
* **The Spider Ranches:** They are the only species capable of farming the **Weaver-Cows** (Giant Spiders).
    * *The Logic:* The spiders are predatory, reacting to fast movement. The Sloths move so slowly that the spiders do not register them as prey/threats. A Sloth can walk right up to a giant spider and milk its spinnerets without triggering an attack.
* **Textile Manufacturing:** They weave **"Iron-Weave" Canvas** and **Silk-Steel Cables**. These are the strongest materials in the setting, essential for airships and suspension bridges.
* **The Stasis-Vaults:** They sell "storage space" in their moss-lined vaults, which are alchemically treated to slow down rot and decay.

## 4.0 Military Role: The Living Wall
* **Style:** Static Defense / Grappling.
* **Weaponry:**
    * **The "Catch-Pole":** A long polearm with a mechanical man-catcher hook.
    * **Net-Guns:** Compressed air cannons that fire weighted spider-silk nets.
* **Tactics:** They do not charge. They hold ground. A unit of Sloths will lock their claws into the terrain and form an unbreakable shield wall. If an enemy gets close, the Sloth grabs them. Once grabbed, the enemy *stays* grabbed until the battle is over.

## 5.0 Social Quirks
* **The "Pause":** Conversations with Sloths are agonizing for Foxes and Monkeys. A Sloth may pause for 30 seconds in the middle of a sentence to chew a leaf or think. Interrupting them is considered a grave insult.
* **Moss-Grooming:** They take great pride in the moss growing on their fur. A Sloth with a flourishing garden of rare moss on their back is seen as distinguished and high-status.
* **Sleeping:** They sleep 18 hours a day. Most "meetings" happen while the Sloth is technically half-asleep. They are famous for negotiating complex trade deals without fully opening their eyes.

## Sciurini

# The Sciurini (The Sky-Jacks)

**Common Name:** Glider-Kin / Flying [[Squirrels]] **Scientific Designation:** _Pteromyini Velox_ **Role:** Couriers, High-Altitude Loggers, Scouts **Home City:** **Glider’s Roost** (A dizzying collection of platforms at the very crown of the forest, connected by zip-lines and updrafts).

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance

- **Physiology:** Small and aerodynamic, standing about 3 to 4 feet tall. They have large, dark eyes adapted for depth perception at high speeds.
    
- **The Patagium:** A furry membrane stretching from wrist to ankle. When limbs are extended, it forms a square "wing." It allows them to glide at ratios of 10:1 (10 feet forward for every 1 foot of drop).
    
- **Bone Structure:** Their bones are semi-hollow (avian-like), making them fragile in a brawl but incredibly light. A 4-foot Sciurini weighs less than a human child.
    
- **The Tail:** A flat, rudder-like tail used for mid-air steering and braking.
    

## 2.0 Psychology & Culture

- **The Philosophy of "Velocity":** To a Sciurini, the ground is a trap. Safety is found in speed. They are culturally uncomfortable with standing still. Their conversations are rapid-fire, often overlapping each other.
    
- **Adrenaline Addiction:** They don't just endure risk; they crave it. The "Roost" culture celebrates the closest calls. A scar from a branch-scrape at 60mph is a badge of honor.
    
- **The "Vertical Caste":** They look down on ground-dwellers (literally and figuratively). They refer to non-flyers as "Lead-Boots."
    

## 3.0 Industrial Role: The Ghost-Loggers

- **The [[Ghost-Teak]] Harvest:** They are the only ones agile enough to reach the **[[Ghost-Teak]]** trees—dead, petrified wood caught in the dangerous web of the upper canopy storm-layer.
    
    - _The Danger:_ Harvesting requires cutting massive logs while suspended 300 feet in the air, then "surfing" the falling log down to the processing yards.
        
- **The Courier Guild:** They run the "Flash-Post." If a contract needs to get from Vulpus-Prime to the Sump in an hour, you hire a Glider. They charge by the minute.
    
    - _Security:_ They are uncatchable. Bandits can't rob what they can't catch.
        

## 4.0 Military Role: The Drop-Troopers

- **Style:** Aerial Ambush.
    
- **Weaponry:**
    
    - **Drop-Darts:** Weighted steel flechettes dropped from above. Gravity does the work; they can punch through helmets.
        
    - **The "Cutter-Wire":** Monofilament wires strung between trees to de-capitate enemy cavalry or snare airships.
        
- **Tactics:** They never land. They dive-bomb, release their payload, and use the momentum to hook a branch and swing away. Fighting them is like fighting angry rain.
    

## 5.0 Social Quirks

- **The "Touch-Code":** In high winds, shouting is useless. They communicate flight paths by tapping rapidly on each other's shoulders or backs. They do this unconsciously even on the ground.
    
- **Sleeping Habits:** They cannot sleep on flat beds (it feels like falling). They sleep in "Sock-Hammocks" suspended over open drops.
    
- **Twitching:** They are constantly stretching their membranes or checking wind direction with a wet finger. It makes other species nervous.

## ---

**1.0 Overview: The Ultimate Butlers**

While [[Mice]] run the offices, the **[[Sciurini]]** run the *lives* of the powerful. They are the most exclusive, expensive, and capable servants in the world.

* **The Look:** Immaculate. Even in a swamp, a [[Sciurini]] butler wears a crisp tailcoat, a vest, and a perfectly polished monocle or pocket watch.  
* **The Slur:** Calling them a **"Nut-Nibbler"** is a grave insult. It’s the fastest way to get blacklisted by The Guild (meaning no butler will *ever* work for you).  
* **The Reputation:** To have a [[Sciurini]] Steward is the ultimate status symbol. It says you didn't just hire a servant; you passed **The Assessment**.  
  * They are found in the homes of Horse Nobles, Wolf Generals, Rat Directors, and even Koda (Captain of the *Undine*) has one.

## ---

**2.0 The Guild of Stewards**

They are not hired off the street. They are contracted through **The Guild**.

* **The Compound:** Their headquarters is a fortress-monastery located on a steep mountain steppe on the Northern border, near the [[Sylvan Empire]]. It looks like a finishing school but trains like a black-ops camp.  
* **The Assessment:** You don't interview a Butler; The Guild interviews *you*.  
  * They investigate a potential employer for weeks. They check your finances, your enemies, and your taste in wine.  
  * If they determine you are "Worthy," they assign you a Steward. The wage is non-negotiable and exorbitant.  
* **The Contract:** A [[Sciurini]] Butler is loyal to the **Client**, not the Law. If a Wolf General hires one, the Butler will help him bury a body without blinking—provided it doesn't stain the carpet.

## ---

**3.0 The Skillset: "I happen to know a little about that..."**

The legends are true. A Guild Steward is a one-person army disguised as a valet.

* **Standard Duties:** Impeccable valeting, bookkeeping, schedule management, and social navigation. They know every tradition, handshake, and faux pas in every culture.  
* **The "Extra" Services:**  
  * **Combat:** They are fierce bodyguards, using "gentlemanly" weapons like reinforced umbrellas or weighted serving trays.  
  * **Disguise:** They are masters of makeup and prosthetics. (Legend says a Steward once made a Raccoon look like a Wolf General to pass a checkpoint).  
  * **Medicine:** They can perform field surgery on a dining room table using silver cutlery and brandy.  
  * **Piloting:** They can fly an airship better than most certified pilots.

## ---

**4.0 The Secret: The Rite of the Star-Acorn**

Why are they so good? It isn't just training. It's **Biomancy**.

* **The Source:** In a hidden forest deep in the Northern Mountains, a specific tree produces the **Star-Acorn**.  
* **The Ritual:**  
  * A teenage Prospect is given a specially prepared Acorn (boiled in alchemical reagents) and left alone in a high treetop for three days.  
  * **The Effect:** The nut is a potent hallucinogen. It breaks the mind down. If the Prospect can hold their sanity together, the chemical connects them to the "Web of Life" around them.  
* **The Result:** It unlocks latent **Biomancy** (Body Magic).  
  * *Grace:* Superhuman reflexes and balance.  
  * *Cunning:* Accelerated processing speed (thinking 10 steps ahead).  
  * *Endurance:* The ability to go days without sleep while remaining sharp.  
* **The Cover:** The world knows the nut exists and thinks it's just a funky religious tradition or a rite of passage. They have no idea it fundamentally alters the Squirrel's biology because the "Preparation Recipe" is the Guild's deepest secret.

## ---

**5.0 The Network: The "Whisper-Web"**

The [[Sciurini]] operate the world's most effective intelligence network, right under the noses of their masters.

* **The Method:** Gossip.  
  * A Butler in Oakhaven mentions to another: *"The weather in the West is turning frightfully sour, isn't it?"*  
  * **The Code:** To a layperson, it's small talk. To the Guild, it means: *"The Ursine Army is mobilizing on the Western Border."*  
* **The Result:** A [[Sciurini]] Butler always knows the news before it happens.  
  * *"Sir, I have taken the liberty of packing your siege-armor. Why? Oh, just a feeling that war will be declared by tea-time."*

## Opallow

# Opallow

**Region:** The Wetlands bordering the [[Canopy Clans]] and the Great Lake **Role:** Hippo Homeland / The Retirement Paradise / The Vice Capital **Vibe:** Humid Luxury, Slow Fans, and "The Fix"

> **"The Bird watches the sky. The Hippo watches the river. And down here in the mud, we know where all the bodies drift."**

## 1.0 The City of Mud and Jade

Opal-Wallow is built _into_ the water. It is a sprawling Venice of wide, flat canals and buildings made of compressed, heat-treated mud bricks glazed with iridescent green ceramic (Jade-Mud).

- **The Climate:** Aggressively humid. Giant fans powered by water wheels constantly circulate moist air. To a Dry-Lander (like a Rat), it is suffocating; to a Hippo, it is heaven.
    
- **Architecture:**
    
    - **The Sunken Lounges:** Most buildings are half-submerged. The ground floor is actually a pool. You don't sit in chairs; you lounge in waist-deep warm water while servers float trays of drinks to you.
        
    - **The "Wide-Doors":** Every door is double-width to accommodate the massive bulk of the locals.
        

## 2.0 The Economy: Rice & Vice

- **The "Pearl-Rice":** The delta grows a unique, translucent rice that is highly prized by the **[[River Folk]]**.
    
- **The Bathhouse District:** The entire city is sitting on geothermal vents. The streets are lined with luxury spas.
    
    - **The "Cleanse":** Criminals and spies come here to "wash off the heat." By tradition, Opal-Wallow is **Neutral Ground**. No arrests, no assassinations. If you break the peace, the Bouncers (Retired Hippo Enforcers) will simply drown you.
        

## 3.0 The Crown Jewel: _The River-King's Shuffle_

A colossal Paddle-Wheel Casino Boat that is permanently moving.

- **The Route:** It sails a slow, endless loop around the Great Lake, never docking. You must take a water-taxi to board.
    
- **The Game:** **"Tiles."** A domino-like game of strategy and bluffing.
    
    - **The Legend:** It is rumored that the **[[Grand Auditor Keth]]** (The Owl Director) has a private suite on the top deck where he meets with the **Pirate Lords** of the Compass once a year.

## Kog Viomuth



---

### **Kog: The Engineer**

#### **Appearance & Mannerisms**

Kog is a massive **Monitor Lizard-Kin** with thick, **Midnight Blue/Purple** scales that have a matte finish from decades of industrial toil. His coloration lightens to a softer shade at his face, hands, and the tip of his tail. His most striking feature is the vertical **milk-white scar** over his left eye. This eye possesses heightened heat vision but lacks normal sight, rendering everything a white blur except in extreme cold or darkness.

To manage this, Kog wears custom-made **Forge Goggles** where each lens can be flipped independently. The left side features dark glass to filter out the "white blur" of daylight, allowing his heat vision to function; the right side features dark glass for protection and a series of magnifying lenses for fine clockwork, including a setting that acts as a long-range looking glass.

His tail is a powerful auxiliary limb, ridged with **pale yellow/ivory diamond-shaped spines**. These flat spines start small at the tail-tip and grow to hand-sized at the base of his neck, where they split into a "crown" before receding above his eyes.

He is perpetually clad in a charred, stiff, heat-resistant leather apron. It functions as a bandolier for **screwdrivers**, ranging from tiny precision tools to massive pry-bars. At his hip sits a large adjustable wrench with a hammer-head on the reverse side; on the other, a holster for a folding measuring stick. The apron's front pouch is a chaotic but organized hoard of nails, fasteners, and custom-made glues and patch-tapes he brews himself in a glass chemistry set.

Kog moves with the slow, deliberate inevitability of a landslide. He frequently "tastes" the air with his tongue—a habit of deep concentration that serves the practical purpose of detecting the metallic tang of an impending boiler blowout or a radiation leak from a cracked core. In quiet moments, he works on his hobby: a tiny, intricate clockwork airship suspended in a glass bottle that, when activated, creates the perfect illusion of a vessel at full clip in the sky.

#### **Backstory**

Kog’s talent was evident from birth; he survived his **Shadow Week** trial by hot-wiring a wreck to fly his clutch to safety. This feat earned him a spot in the **Heart Forge Guild**, the elite body responsible for the systems powering the civilization. Though brilliant, Kog was a social disaster. After embarrassing a VIP at a dinner party, his upward mobility was permanently severed.

He was assigned as a **Pressure-Master** to a secondary forge maintaining the capital's power. His superior was a **Noble-Born Guildmaster** who had been gifted the position through nepotism. During a high-stakes inspection, the Noble—desperate to put on a show—ordered Kog to release a specific valve and vent. Kog politely refused, knowing that the Noble’s chosen vent (Vent 3) would cause a catastrophic failure, whereas Vent 4 would provide the dramatic steam display the Noble craved.

Offended, the Noble struck Kog with a heavy ceremonial wrench. While Kog was dazed, the Noble engaged the wrong controls. As gauges redlined and metal resonated, Kog threw the Noble aside and reversed the controls. The console ruptured, badly injuring Kog and saving the sector from a blast that would have killed thousands.

The political fallout was swift: the Noble claimed Kog had sabotaged the forge, and visting dignitaries backed the story. Unable to prove his innocence, Kog was sentenced to death. He was transported to a historic execution site—a giant circle of glass-like stone known as the **Fossilized Blood**. However, the executioner, suspecting the truth, granted Kog a "Survivor’s Chance." He gave him a cup of water, a piece of fruit, and told him to vanish. Kog marched east across the desert for a week, eventually reaching the "dirty mines" of **Port Sunder**. There, he was found by young, lazy Diplomatic Corps officers who were happy to let the "old lizard" do all the work while they spent their days partying.

#### **Traits & Tendencies**

- **Thermal Comfort:** He detests the cold of high-altitude flight and is frequently found sleeping against the steam engine manifold to soak up the heat.
    
- **Outspoken Nature:** Kog has no concept of social etiquette. He "calls it as he sees it," often insulting those in power without realizing or caring.
    

#### **Strengths**

- **Structural Diagnosis:** He doesn't need gauges. A touch of his hand, a flick of his tongue, or a single glance at a bulkhead tells him exactly which bolt is about to snap and how many minutes the engine has left.
    
- **Extreme Heat Tolerance:** He can perform "hot-fixes" inside active engine rooms at temperatures exceeding **180°C**.
    

#### **Weaknesses**

- **Visual Limitations:** He has a massive blind spot on his left side and can be blinded by bright light if his goggles are not adjusted.
    
- **The "Nobility" Trigger:** Kog harbors a visceral, silent hatred for the aristocratic and the entitled. This makes him entirely useless in matters of diplomacy or tact, as he is prone to offending anyone with undeserved power.

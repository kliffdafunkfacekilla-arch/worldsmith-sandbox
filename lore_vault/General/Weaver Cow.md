# The Weaver-Cow (Velvet-Spider)
**Scientific Designation:** *Aranea Lactis*
**Primary Rancher:** The [[Sloth-Folk]] (Sloths)
**Farm Location:** The Web-Barns (Dark, humid hollows)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **Size:** About the size of a Jersey Cow. Bulbous, hairy abdomen and short, thick legs.
* **Appearance:** They look terrifying to outsiders, but they are covered in soft, velvet-like white fur. Their fangs have atrophied and are useless for biting.
* **Diet:** A nutrient slurry of ground bugs and sugar-water (Honeydew). They drink from troughs like cattle.

## 2.0 Industrial Utility
* **Silk:** They are not killed. They are "milked." A rancher gently stimulates the spinnerets, and the spider releases a continuous thread of thick, high-tensile silk.
    * **Silk-Steel:** When braided, this silk is stronger than steel cable and much lighter.
* **Chitin-Wax:** They secrete a wax to keep their fur clean. This is scraped off and used for waterproofing and candles.

## 3.0 Temperament & Danger
* **Aggression:** None. They are aggressively docile. They are essentially giant, eight-legged puppies that like to be scratched behind the pedipalps.
* **The Risk:** The only danger is getting stuck in their webbing if you wander into the barn without a solvent-spray.

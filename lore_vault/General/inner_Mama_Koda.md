### 🐻 Character Dossier: "Mama" Koda

_([[Hearthless]] Queen of the Undine)_

**Real Name:** Koda of the Snowpeak

**Title:** "Mama" (to her crew), "The Ice-Branded" (behind her back)

**Species:** Ursine (Polar Bear variant—massive, white fur, scars visible where fur hasn't grown back).

**Visuals:** She wears modified [[River Folk]] finery—silks and velvets—but drapes a massive, expensive fur cloak over her shoulders, a mockery of the warmth she was denied. The **Sigil of Ice** brand is clearly visible on her upper chest, turning the skin black against her white fur. She never hides it.

---

### 1. The Crime: The Cold Mercy

Koda wasn't just a citizen of [[Ursine Hegemony]]; she was a Captain in the **[[Wolverines]]**, the elite internal guard. Her job was to root out threats to the community.

Her life ended during the **Shadow Week** of her 30th year.

- **The Incident:** A severe blizzard hit Snowpeak Citadel during the eclipse. Tasked with guarding the ground gates by the pass Koda found a group of "Drifters" (Cactus-Kin refugees from the Dust Bowl) that were caught outside the gates, freezing to death. Ursine law is absolute: _The Hearth is for the Community. Resources cannot be wasted on outsiders during the Long Dark._
    
- **The Choice:** Koda was the Gate Warden. Hearing the children crying over the wind, she broke the seal and brought them into the Outer Vestibule, using the reserve coal to keep them alive.
    
- **The Judgment:** She didn't harm an Ursine, but she "risked the warmth of the Hearth" for strangers. To the Elders, this was a betrayal of the **Hearthguard's Vigil**.
    
- **The Brand:** She was brought before the Great Hearth. They branded the **Sigil of Ice** on her chest—marking her as [[Hearthless]]—and cast her out into the blizzard she had tried to save others from.
    

### 2. The Exile: The Iron Bear

Koda didn't die. She walked south until the snow turned to mud, fueling herself on pure rage. She ended up in the **River Delta**, starving and hated. [[River Folk]] don't typically like [[Ursine Hegemony]] (too big, too clumsy, too moral).

- **The Pivot:** To eat, she took a job as "muscle" with [[Crimson Corsairs]] on a smuggling barge.
    
- **The Rise:** Violence came easy to a trained Wolverine Captain. But Koda had something other pirates didn't: **Discipline**. She organized the chaotic crew. She rationed the rum. She cleaned the guns. When the Captain died (in a "verified accident" involving a loose cannon), the crew unanimously voted Koda into command.
    
- **The Reputation:** She became known as "The Iron Bear." She didn't raid for blood; she raided for _efficiency_. She hit [[Sump-Kin]] trains and[[Avian Empire]] ships with military precision. She became wealthy and well known, but she was still homeless. A pirate ship is not a Hearth.
    

### 3. The Bet: The Undying Hand

Five years ago, Koda docked her ship, _The Cold Snap_, at [[Undine's Undying Dance]]. At the time, the tavern was a "Scab"—rotting, dangerous, and run by **Felix the Rot**, a [[River Folk]] slumlord who let the legacy of the Winch Master fall into ruin.

Felix was drunk, arrogant, and losing money. He challenged the famous Pirate Queen to a game of **"Dead Man's Poker"** (a local variant played with tarot cards).

- **The Stakes:** Koda put up her entire fleet and her pirate hoard. Felix, laughing, put up the deed to the _Undine_. He thought she was a dumb brute.
    
- **The Play:** The final hand was tense. Felix held a "King of Coins." Koda revealed her hand: The "Tower" (Ruin) and the "Star" (Hope). In River rules, Ruin + Hope beats Money.
    
- **The Aftermath:** Felix tried to draw a hidden dagger. Koda didn't even stand up; she simply broke his wrist, took the deed, and threw him from the top floor far enough to clear the  whirlpool below.
    
- **The Declaration:** She stood on the table and roared at the terrified patrons: _"The undine is under new management! Round is on me! Drink up, and get out. I'm redecorating."_

### 4. The Transformation: A Hearth for the Hearthless

Koda didn't just want a tavern; she wanted the home she was denied. She poured her pirate fortune into the renovations.

- **The Philosophy:** The _Undine_ is now a sanctuary. It has rules.
    
    - _Rule 1:_ You pay, you stay.
        
    - _Rule 2:_ No "cold hearts." (Cruelty is punished by the bouncers).
        
    - _Rule 3:_ Leave your politics at the dock.
    
    - *Rule 4* Respect the Hearth or you get hurt
- **The "Mother" Role:** She adopted the title "Mama" not as a soft term, but as a challenge to the Ursine "Mama Bear" doctrine. The Ursine rejected her, so she became the Mother of Outcasts. Her crew (the staff) are fiercely loyal because she treats them with the protective ferocity of a bear.

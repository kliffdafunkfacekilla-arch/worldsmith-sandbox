## Crimson Clove

# Crimson-Clove (Numb-Spice)
**Scientific Designation:** *Syzygium Anestheticum*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Vulpines]] (Foxes)
**Grow Zone:** The Scent-Gardens (Mid-Canopy)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** A flowering tree with deep red, nail-shaped buds.
* **The Oil:** The buds are rich in eugenol and a mild neurotoxin.
* **Scent:** Overpoweringly spicy and metallic.

## 2.0 Industrial & Medical Utility
* **Anesthetic:** Chewing a single clove numbs the mouth instantly. Used for toothaches and minor surgery.
* **Preservative:** Meat rubbed with Crimson-Clove oil does not rot, even in the jungle heat. It kills bacteria on contact.
* **Culinary:** Used in small amounts to add a "tingling" heat to curries.

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** Picked by hand and dried in the Vulpine "Scent-Houses."
* **The Risk:** Prolonged skin contact with the raw oil causes permanent loss of sensation in the fingertips. Harvesters wear thick gloves.

### A Scholar's Compendium of the Shattered World's Fauna

*From the Archives of the Great Library, Snow Peak UniversityAuthored by: REDACTED, Lead Xenobiologist*

##### 1.0 Introduction: Life in the Wake of Chaos

In the millennia since [[The Shattering]], the surviving civilizations of this world have come to understand a fundamental truth: knowledge is [[Magistar Stagus]] against chaos. Here at Snow Peak University, our academic pursuits are not mere intellectual exercises; they are essential contributions to the continued survival of our peoples. Among the most critical fields of study is xenobiology—the comprehensive analysis of the world's fauna. Understanding the creatures that inhabit our lands, skies, and waters is paramount. From the docile herd animals that represent stable sources of food and materials to the chaos-warped monstrosities that defy natural law, each species presents a unique set of opportunities, threats, and lessons about the world's fractured ecology. A thorough grasp of this bestiary informs everything from the logistics of global commerce to the tactics of warfare and, most importantly, illuminates the lasting and pervasive impact of [[The Shattering]] itself.This compendium seeks to provide a foundational overview of the most significant creatures documented by our scholars. We shall begin by examining the native wildlife that has adapted to the stable zones, establishing a baseline for the world's natural order. From there, we will explore the domesticated and specialized fauna that have become the indispensable engines of civilization. The report will then touch upon the ambiguous lifeforms that blur the lines between flora and fauna, before concluding with a necessary, if brief, note on the terrifying and little-understood entities spawned directly from the [[Chaos Zones]].

##### 2.0 Native Wildlife of the Stable Zones

The vast, impassable [[Chaos Zones]] have created pockets of relative normalcy known as "stable zones." While far from tranquil by pre-Shattering standards, these regions are governed by a consistent, if harsh, set of natural laws. The creatures that have evolved here serve as our primary reference for the world's baseline ecology, having adapted to phenomena such as the broken moon's erratic tidal effects and the world's dramatically elongated seasons. Their resilience and unique survival strategies offer invaluable insight into the fundamental nature of life in our era.

###### *2.1 The Sky-Grazer*

* **Physical Description:**  The Sky-Grazer is a truly colossal herd animal, a living island of flesh and gas that floats serenely through the upper atmosphere. Its form is bulbous and buoyant, moving with a deceptive grace that belies its immense size.  
* **Behavior and Diet:**  These creatures are fundamentally docile, moving in large, slow-drifting herds. Their diet consists primarily of airborne vegetation—spores, pollen, and floating flora—and they are often observed consuming clouds rich in static energy. Their seemingly passive consumption of static-rich clouds is a fascinating area of metabolic study, suggesting a form of biological energy conversion not seen in terrestrial fauna. Their peaceful grazing is a common and reassuring sight for airship travelers.  
* **Ecological Role and Hazard Assessment:**  While individually harmless, a Sky-Grazer herd presents a significant navigational hazard. Like their terrestrial counterparts, they are prone to stampedes when startled. A panicked herd, thundering through the skyways, can easily collide with and destroy even the most robust airships or damage floating structures, making them a force of nature that all pilots must respect and avoid.

###### *2.2 The Gritbore*

* **Physical Description:**  The Gritbore is a remarkable example of adaptation to one of the world's harshest environments. It is a large creature, built low to the ground, with a unique physiology that blends rodent-like and reptilian features. Its body is protected by massive, overlapping scales of a rust and sandstone hue, giving it the appearance of a cross between a warthog and an armadillo. It possesses a set of large, curving horns reminiscent of cattle, and its prominent front teeth are perpetually stained from its unusual diet of gnawing on rocks to extract minerals. Its common name, 'Gritbore,' is a functional descriptor derived from its habit of surviving in the desert grit and boring into the earth for sustenance.  
* **Habitat and Domestication:**  Gritbores are native to the punishing desert landscapes surrounding [[Iron Caldera]]. Their ability to thrive in this volatile environment made them prime candidates for domestication. Today, they are herded nomadically by the Resource Management Guild of [[Iron Caldera]], serving as the faction's primary source of livestock.  
* **Ecological Role and Hazard Assessment:**  The Gritbore's unique survival adaptations are the key to its value. It is incredibly tough and self-sufficient, capable of finding sustenance where no other large creature can. While domesticated, its powerful build and formidable horns demand respect from its handlers.The remarkable resilience of these native species demonstrates life's capacity to endure even the most dramatic environmental upheaval. Yet, survival is only the first step; the intelligent peoples of this world have gone further, harnessing the power of specific fauna to build and sustain their civilizations.

##### 3.0 Domesticated & Specialized Fauna: The Engines of Civilization

In a world defined by vast distances and treacherous terrain, the domestication of animals has been a cornerstone of societal development. The various factions and cultures have demonstrated remarkable ingenuity in identifying and breeding creatures whose unique abilities can be leveraged for transport, warfare, and communication. These animals are not merely tools but living assets, their specialized functions making them indispensable to the survival and prosperity of their masters.

###### *3.1 Beasts of Burden and Transport*

**3.1.1 Terrestrial Hauling: The Sump-Galleon Turtle**The Sump-Galleon Turtle is the backbone of [[Sump-Kin]]'s logistics empire. These are tortoises of immense size, with shells as wide as a road, that are nonetheless surprisingly agile. Heavily armored and tireless, they are domesticated to serve as the living engines for the infamous "Turtle Trains." By strapping large, cylindrical metal wagons to their shells, [[Sump-Kin]] have created unstoppable convoys capable of moving heavy cargo across the continent. [[Sump-Kin]]'s domestication of this single species has effectively allowed them to monopolize terrestrial heavy freight, creating an economic empire built on reptilian resilience where traditional infrastructure is an impossibility.**3.1.2 Sub-Aquatic Propulsion: The Abyssal Snail**The enigmatic faction known as The Chain utilizes a creature of colossal power to drive its ships: the Abyssal Snail. These colossal mollusks are tethered to vessels via immense iron chains and swim deep beneath the surface. This method of propulsion generates an unstoppable torque, allowing the ships to completely ignore adverse weather like headwinds and storms, as well as powerful surface currents. The difficult and dangerous task of wrangling these deep-sea behemoths is handled exclusively by Walrus and Seal-kin.

###### *3.2 Mounts & Steeds of War and Peace*

A reliable mount confers universally recognized tactical and logistical advantages. Different cultures have tamed creatures perfectly suited to their specific environmental and military needs.

* **Giant Water Moccasins:**  The Rifle-Frogs of [[Sump-Kin]] ride these large, venomous snakes into battle. The moccasins serve as formidable cavalry mounts, presenting a dual threat to any adversary. The snake itself can deliver a lethal bite or a powerful tail-whip, while the frog rider provides fire with a lever-action rifle from its back.  
* **Sting and Manta Rays:**  The Current Riders, a renowned courier service associated with the Sunken Theocracy, have domesticated various rays for use as aquatic steeds and work animals. Their speed and maneuverability in the water are unmatched.  
* **Mountain Goats:**  For their land-based operations, the same Current Riders employ nimble mountain goats. While their precise origin remains undocumented, their presence among the aquatic-centric Current Riders suggests either a pragmatic trade with a mountain-dwelling faction or an isolated population uniquely adapted to the coastal highlands bordering Theocracy territory. Further study is warranted.

###### *3.3 Specialized Service Animals*

Beyond simple transport or combat, certain animals have been bred for highly specialized, mission-critical roles that require intelligence and unique biological traits.

* **Gardener Snakes:**  [[Sump-Kin]]'s Slither-Guard employs a unique subspecies of smaller snake distinguished by a remarkable mutation: a pair of functional arms. These "Gardener Snakes" are invaluable agents, serving as stealthy scouts, trackers, and lookouts who can move unseen through dense undergrowth to spot ambushes.  
* **Dragon-Flies:**  To coordinate complex and dangerous operations, particularly cargo runs through [[Chaos Zones]], [[Sump-Kin]] use Dragon-Flies as messengers. These insects are fast and reliable, allowing different units to synchronize their movements with precision, a critical factor when even a moment's delay can lead to catastrophe.

###### *3.4 Companions and Status Symbols*

* **Colorful Birds of [[Scute Confederacy1]]:**  The reptilian Scute Confederacy is famous for its relationship with the vibrant birds native to its tropical, jungle-like islands. These birds are renowned for their brilliant colors and are kept as cherished pets. Their cultural significance is profound; the Scute will never sell the birds themselves, though their naturally shed feathers are a common trade good. Given the Scute's history of vengeful justice and their rise from slavery (see  *The Black Scale 7, University Archives* ), the refusal to sell these birds may be rooted in a cultural belief that life and beauty cannot be commodified, a direct repudiation of their former masters' values.The intricate partnerships forged between the world's peoples and these specialized animals are a testament to co-evolutionary ingenuity. This symbiosis is a cornerstone of societal function, projecting power and enabling feats of logistics and warfare that would otherwise be impossible. However, not all life in this world is so easily categorized or controlled.

##### 4.0 Predatory Flora & Ambiguous Lifeforms

The scholarly lines between flora and fauna, between passive plant and active predator, are not always clear in the Shattered World. A unique ecological niche is occupied by lifeforms that, while rooted like plants, have evolved sophisticated and aggressive hunting methods. These predatory flora represent a distinct and often underestimated danger, employing ambush tactics that turn the very landscape against the unwary traveler. While the Grave-Root is the most well-documented specimen, field reports contain numerous unconfirmed accounts of other aggressive floral life, suggesting this is a wider, albeit poorly understood, ecological category.

###### *4.1 The Grave-Root*

* **Appearance and Mimicry:**  The Grave-Root is a master of deception. To the casual observer, it appears to be nothing more than a dead, withered tree—a common and unremarkable sight. This perfect mimicry is the key to its success as a stationary hunter.  
* **Hunting Method:**  This carnivorous plant is an ambush predator of terrifying efficiency. Its extensive root system spreads far beneath the soil, acting as a sensory organ that detects the ground vibrations of approaching prey. When a target comes within range, the Grave-Root lashes out with thorny, vine-like appendages, snaring its victim and dragging it towards a crushing maw hidden within its trunk.  
* **Threat Assessment:**  The Grave-Root poses a severe threat to any who travel through the wilderness. Its deceptive appearance means that even experienced explorers can be caught unaware. It stands as a prime example of the insidious dangers that can be present even within the relative safety of the world's stable zones, reminding us that constant vigilance is a necessity for survival.The calculated patience of a natural predator like the Grave-Root is a danger that can, with knowledge, be anticipated. This is a cold comfort when contrasted with the entities spawned from chaos, whose existence is a violent affront to natural law.

##### 5.0 A Note on Chaos-Spawned Monstrosities

This compendium would be incomplete without acknowledging the greatest unnatural threat in the Shattered World: the creatures born from raw chaos. The impassable [[Chaos Zones]] are not merely dangerous landscapes; they are environments where the fabric of reality is warped and unstable. It is within these zones that raw energy can coalesce into monstrous beasts—entities that are not evolved, but manifested. These "Chaos Anomalies" are a direct and terrifying byproduct of [[The Shattering]].While specific bestiary entries for these creatures are exceptionally rare due to the mortal danger involved in their study, their existence is well-documented through harrowing second-hand accounts. From what our scholars have gathered, these monsters appear to be drawn to loud noises and signs of distress, a behavioral trait exploited by [[Sump-Kin]]. Their notorious "Bait & Switch" tactic for traversing [[Chaos Zones]] involves using prisoners as live bait, whose screaming and panic are used to lure the anomalies away from the main cargo train, clearing a path through the maelstrom.In conclusion, while the documented fauna of the stable zones are increasingly understood, providing resources and partnerships that allow civilization to thrive, the creatures of chaos remain a terrifying and largely unknown variable. They are a constant, visceral reminder of the world's shattered state and the unceasing vigilance required to survive within it. Therefore, continued, albeit perilous, research into the nature and potential classification of these anomalies remains one of the University's most pressing long-term objectives.

## Ghost Teak

# Ghost-Teak (Float-Wood)
**Scientific Designation:** *Tectona Spiritus*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Sciurini]] (Gliders)
**Grow Zone:** The Crown-Layer (Dead/Petrified Trees)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** These are the skeletons of massive ancient trees that died but remained standing in the upper canopy.
* **The Wood:** Over centuries, the sun bleaches the wood bone-white, and the sap evaporates, leaving vacuum pockets inside the grain.
* **Properties:** It is incredibly strong but weighs almost nothing. A child can lift a log the size of a telephone pole.

## 2.0 Industrial Utility
* **Aviation:** The primary material for building Airship gondolas and glider-frames.
* **Instruments:** Used by the Ants to make "Void-Lutes" because the hollow wood resonates perfectly.

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** "Sky-Jacking." Gliders attach ropes to the dead branches, cut them loose, and "surf" the falling log down to the collection nets.
* **The Risk:** Extremely high fatality rate. If the wind catches the log wrong, it acts like a sail and drags the cutter into the sky or slams them into the trunk.

## Grave Berry

# Grave-Berry (The Memory-Fruit)
**Scientific Designation:** *Solanum Manes*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Eastern Hounds]] (Wolf-Nomads)
**Grow Zone:** The Bone-Fields (Burial Mounds & Battlefields)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** A creeping, thorny vine that refuses to grow in healthy soil. It only takes root in soil rich in calcium and decaying organic matter. It is most commonly found growing over ancient cairns or unmarked graves.
* **The Fruit:** Small, pale white berries that look like porcelain beads. When squeezed, they bleed a thick, crimson juice that resembles fresh blood.
* **Growth Cycle:** They bloom at night and rot instantly if touched by direct sunlight. They must be harvested under moonlight.

## 2.0 Mystical & Medical Utility
* **Mnemosyne-Wine:** The Hounds ferment these berries into a potent, bitter wine.
    * **Effect:** It is a psychoactive "Memory-Enhancer." In small doses, it helps the Amnesiac Hounds recall flashes of their forgotten history. In large doses, it allows the drinker to experience the memories of the *creature buried beneath the roots*.
* **Embalming Fluid:** The juice is a powerful preservative. It arrests decay in flesh. The Hounds use it to preserve their dead (and their food stocks) for long journeys.
* **Pain-Killer:** A paste made from the seeds is a powerful numbing agent, used to treat battlefield wounds where the patient needs to remain conscious but feel no pain.

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** "The Rite of Asking." Hounds believe you must ask permission of the dead before picking the berries. If you do not, the fruit turns to ash in your mouth.
* **The Risk:** **"The Echo."** Consuming too many Grave-Berries causes "Soul-Rot." The user begins to confuse their own memories with the memories of the dead. They may forget their own name and believe they are an ancient ancestor, leading to madness.

## Lunar Ghost Blooms

**Core Concept:** Biological "Chaos-Sponges" that act as batteries for ambient magical radiation.

## I. Biology & Habitat

### 1. The "Anti-Photosynthesis"

- **Mechanism:** These plants lack chlorophyll. They do not feed on sunlight (which they hate); they feed on **"Moon-Haze"** (Ambient Chaos Radiation).
    
- **Appearance:** Pale, translucent stalks that look like fungal-orchids. They glow faintly only during the "Purple Sky" phase of the moon.
    
- **Growth:** They act like trees, growing forever but capping at **3 Meters** in height.
    
    - _Old Growth:_ A flower found deep in a cave might be centuries old and incredibly potent.
        

### 2. The Habitat: "The Dark Dry"

- **Conditions:** They require a specific, paradoxical environment: **Warm, Dry, and Pitch Black.**
    
- **Locations:**
    
    - **The Dust Bowl Canyons:** The primary source.
        
    - **[[Iron Caldera]] Lava Tubes:** Rare, high-heat variants.
        
    - **[[Hive Commonwealth]] Undersides:** Lower quality, shaded by cliffs.
        

### 3. The Defense Mechanism: "The Mutation Cloud"

- **The Battery:** Because they absorb chaos, they are physically unstable.
    
- **Fire Hazard:** You cannot burn a field of Ghost Flowers. Doing so releases the stored chaos as a **Radioactive Mutagenic Cloud**. A brushfire in a flower patch creates a localized biological disaster.
    

## II. The Harvest: Logistics of the Dead

Harvesting is a specialized industry dominated by the **[[Dust-Husk Riders]]**.

### 1. The Method

- **Transport:** The flowers wilt/discharge if exposed to direct sunlight or humidity.
    
- **The Cactus-Kin Solution:** The Riders use the hollowed-out spines or water-sacs of their **Cactus-Kin** mounts to transport the delicate blooms. The interior of a cactus provides the perfect "cool, dark, dry" stasis chamber for transport across the desert.
    

## III. The Derivatives: Medicine & Vice

The Ghost Flower is the raw material for three distinct industries.

### 1. The Cure: "Aether-Clear" (Medicine)

- **The Process:** Fermented with **[[Ozone-Milk]]** (Avian source) by the **Quill-Kin** (Porcupines).
    
- **The Effect:** It is the only known treatment for **"Corruption Sickness"** (Chaos Mutation). It halts the spread of physical warping and clears the "Mental Fog" of chaos exposure.
    
- **The Monopoly:** The Quill-Kin keep the fermentation ratio a secret. If mixed wrong, it becomes an explosive poison.
    

### 2. The Trap: "Void-Cream" (Cosmetics)

- **The Market:** Highborn Horse Nobles and Hive Queens.
    
- **The Promise:** When processed into a topical cream, the chaos-absorbing properties "absorb" wrinkles and age, restoring youthful appearance.
    
- **The Cost:** It is astronomically expensive.
    
- **The Side Effect:** Overuse causes **"The Porcelain Curse."** The user's skin becomes too smooth, eventually turning translucent. They lose the ability to micro-express emotion, leaving them looking like beautiful, ageless dolls.
    

### 3. The Narcotic: "The 4-Year Seed"

- **The Cycle:** The flowers only seed once every 4 years.
    
- **The Drug:** The seeds are a potent hallucinogen.
    
- **The High:** Users claim to see "The Architecture of the Seals"—visions of the mathematical laws holding the world together, or glimpses of the "World That Was" before [[The Shattering]].
  
  **The Habitat:** They require **Warm, Dry, and Dark** conditions. This makes them distinct from the swampy flora of the [[River Folk]]. They thrive in the deep canyons of the **Dust Bowl**, the lava tubes of the **[[Iron Caldera]]**, and the shadowed undersides of **[[Hive Commonwealth]]** cliffs.

• **The Physics:** They absorb _ambient_ Moon-Haze (Chaos Radiation) rather than the concentrated flow. This makes them "Chaos Batteries."

    ◦ _The Danger:_ Because they store chaos, burning a Ghost Flower field wouldn't just produce smoke; it would release a cloud of raw mutation. This makes harvesting them extremely hazardous.

• **Growth Cycle:** They grow forever but cap at 3 meters. This implies they become denser and more potent with age. An "Old Growth" Ghost Flower found in a deep Dust Bowl cave is a fortune waiting to be plucked.

## Shadow Truffle

# Shadow-Truffle (Fungal Chocolate)
**Scientific Designation:** *Tuber Umbra Dulcis*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Simians]] (Monkeys)
**Grow Zone:** The Root-Systems (Deep Canopy/Underside)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** A parasitic fungus that grows in large, fist-sized nodules on the roots of the Iron-Wood trees.
* **Appearance:** The skin is black and rough like coal. Inside, the flesh is a rich, dark purple.
* **Flavor Profile:** It tastes like 90% dark chocolate mixed with espresso and a hint of savory umami (mushroom). It is much richer and heavier than traditional cocoa.

## 2.0 Industrial & Culinary Utility
* **Caloric Density:** Extremely high energy. One truffle contains the calories of a full steak dinner.
* **Ration-Bricks:** It is pressed into dense, non-perishable bricks for military rations.
* **Luxury Confectionery:** Refined with sugar and milk, it becomes the most expensive dessert in the world ("Black-Gold Mousse").

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** Monkeys must climb *down* into the dark underside of the canopy to dig them out of the bark.
* **The Risk:** The underside is the domain of spiders and bats. Harvesters often fight for their haul.

## Silver Needle Tea

# Silver-Needle Tea (Ice-Blood)
**Scientific Designation:** *Camellia Cryogenica*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Red Pandas]] ([[Red Pandas]])
**Grow Zone:** The Cloud-Peaks (High Altitude, Misty)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** A stunted, hardy bush that grows only on the highest peaks of the giant trees, where the air is thin and cold.
* **The Leaves:** The leaves are covered in fine, silver fuzz (trichomes) that traps moisture. When crushed, they feel cold to the touch.
* **Growth Cycle:** Slow-growing. It takes 5 years for a bush to mature.

## 2.0 Industrial & Medical Utility
* **The "Coolant" Effect:** The plant contains a natural endothermic compound. When brewed into tea, it lowers the consumer's body temperature by several degrees.
* **The Market:** It is the lifeblood of the **[[Iron Caldera]]**. Factory workers and soldiers in heavy armor drink gallons of it to prevent heatstroke.
* **Preservation:** The dried leaves are used to pack perishables, acting as a mild natural ice-pack.

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** Hand-picked by [[Red Pandas]] at dawn when the frost is still fresh.
* **The Risk:** The altitude is dizzying, and the plants often grow on sheer vertical bark-cliffs.

## Stone Root

# Stone-Root (Petrified Lumber)
**Scientific Designation:** *Radix Petra*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Manis]] (Pangolins)
**Harvest Zone:** The Deep Mines (Sub-Canopy)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** Fossilized roots of the "Ancestor Trees" that died thousands of years ago.
* **Properties:** It is technically a mineral, but retains the grain and workability of wood.
* **Appearance:** Grey-brown, streaked with quartz. Heavy and cool to the touch.

## 2.0 Industrial Utility
* **Fireproof Construction:** It cannot burn. It is the only building material allowed in the explosive factories or the [[Iron Caldera]] foundries.
* **Gun Stocks:** Used by the [[Tarsiers]] for rifle stocks because it absorbs recoil better than normal wood and doesn't warp in humidity.

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** Mining. Pangolins dig deep tunnels to find the root veins and chisel them out.
* **The Risk:** Cave-ins and hitting pockets of explosive "Swamp-Gas" trapped in the soil.

## Sun Melon

# Sun-Melon (Nature's Canteen)
**Scientific Designation:** *Cucumis Durus*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Simians]] (Monkeys)
**Grow Zone:** Upper Canopy (Full Sunlight)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** A thick, woody vine that strangles other branches to reach the sun.
* **The Fruit:** Football-sized yellow melons with a rind as hard as oak wood.
* **The Interior:** Contains a liter of clear, electrolyte-rich water and sweet, firm yellow meat.

## 2.0 Industrial Utility
* **Hydration:** It is the standard water-source for travelers. Because the shell is so hard, it doesn't break when dropped and doesn't spoil for months.
* **The Shell:** Once eaten, the empty halves are used as bowls, helmets for children, or ladles.

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** Requires a saw or a heavy machete to cut the stem.
* **The Risk:** Falling fruit. A Sun-Melon dropping from 100 feet up can crack a skull. "Helmet-Checks" are mandatory in melon groves.

## Whisper Berry

# Whisper-Berry (Sparkle-Fruit)
**Scientific Designation:** *Vitis Aeris*
**Primary Cultivator:** The [[Simians]] (Monkeys)
**Grow Zone:** Wind-Tunnels (Mid-Canopy)

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance
* **The Plant:** A delicate shrub that thrives in high-wind areas.
* **The Fruit:** Clusters of translucent purple berries.
* **The Reaction:** The berries naturally trap pressurized gas inside their skins. When bitten, they pop and fizz on the tongue (Natural Carbonation).

## 2.0 Industrial Utility
* **Wine Making:** Used to produce "Canopy-Champagne." The carbonation is natural, so it doesn't need secondary fermentation.
* **Luxury Trade:** Sold to the Avian Nobility as a delicacy. The sensation of "eating air" is considered very fashionable.

## 3.0 Harvest & Danger
* **Harvest Method:** Extremely delicate. If handled roughly, the berries pop and become worthless.
* **The Risk:** They grow in high-wind zones where gusts can blow a harvester off the branch.

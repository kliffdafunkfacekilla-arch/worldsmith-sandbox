# The Somoan Wood-Hen

**Common Name:** Screamer-Hen / Camp-Badger **Scientific Designation:** _Gallus Vigilante_ **Role:** Living Alarm System, Pest Control **Status:** Semi-Domesticated

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance

- **Physiology:** A round, stout flightless bird roughly the size of a large turkey. They have iridescent black feathers and a bright, blood-red wattle.
    
- **Temperament:** They possess zero self-preservation instincts and extreme territorial aggression. They are essentially bundles of rage and feathers.
    
- **Diet:** Carnivorous. They hunt the scorpions, spiders, and venomous snakes that attempt to crawl into the Hounds' sleeping yurts.
    

## 2.0 Utility

- **The Siren:** Their primary value is noise. If an unfamiliar creature (human, monster, or spirit) crosses the camp perimeter, the Wood-Hens erupt into a deafening, shrieking frenzy that wakes the entire camp instantly.
    
- **The "Mine-Sweeper":** Hounds often release a flock of Hens into a suspicious area before entering themselves. If the Hens start screaming or explode (triggering a trap), the Hounds know where the danger is.
    

## 3.0 Cultural Significance

- **The Taboo:** Eating a Wood-Hen is considered bad luck, like eating a guard dog. They are only consumed during absolute famine.
    
- **Folklore:** Hounds believe the Hens can see ghosts, which is why they sometimes scream at empty air in the middle of the night.

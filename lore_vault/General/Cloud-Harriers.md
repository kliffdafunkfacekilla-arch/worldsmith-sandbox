**Core Concept:** Avian Roughnecks and High-Altitude Ranchers. **Archetype:** "Sky-Western." They are gritty, dangerous, and distinct from the polished diplomats of the Capital.

## I. Identity & Location

### 1. The Territory: The Northern Outer Cliffs

- **The Environment:** The windiest, coldest, and most treacherous section of the Engineer’s Mountain Range.
    
- **The Logic:** This is a natural wind-tunnel where the Sky-Grazer herds are forced to migrate. It is the only place where the herds fly low enough to be intercepted from the ground.
    

### 2. The Culture: "True Aim over Technical Truth"

- **The Split:** While Capital Avians value "Truth" in ledgers and contracts, Harriers value "True Aim" and "True Grit."
    
- **Relationship:** They view the city-dwelling Avians (Bankers/Diplomats) as soft. However, the Empire relies entirely on them for **[[Voltaic Fleece]]**, giving the Harriers immense political leverage (The "Grease-Monkey" Leverage).
    

## II. The Industry: The "Frame-Drop" Harvest

They do not use airships to hunt (too fragile for the cliff winds). They use gravity and geology.

### 1. The Trap

- **The Drivers:** Small, agile Harrier gliders spook the migrating herd, driving them into narrow "chutes" along the cliff face.
    
- **The Clamp:** Massive, counter-weighted steel frames hang from the cliffs. When a Grazer passes underneath, the frame drops.
    
- **The Pin:** The frame clamps the beast against the padded cliff wall. It uses the animal's own buoyancy to lock it in place.
    

### 2. The Pit Stop

This is a race against time.

- **The Shear:** Harriers repel down the cliff face using **Obsidian Blades** (glass doesn't conduct electricity). They strip the wool in seconds.
    
- **The Milk:** High-suction hoses drain the udder tanks.
    
- **The Release:** The counter-weights disengage, launching the confused Grazer back into the slipstream unharmed.
    

## III. The Economy: Commodities

The Harriers control two vital global markets.

### 1. Voltaic Fleece (The Safety Monopoly)

Sky-Grazer wool is naturally conductive but heat-resistant.

- **Storm-Grade (High Value):** Harvested _before_ Shadow Week when static charge is high.
    
    - **Utility:** It is the **only material** capable of insulating at such high temps Without this wool,   forges would overheat. its conductive propertys make great for protection on air ships in storm weather  to avoid lightning electrocuting the ship's crew.
        
    - _Global Impact:_ The Cloud-Harriers effectively decide who flies.
        
- **Shadow-Grade (Low Value):** Harvested _during_ Shadow Week (The Static Famine).
    
    - **Utility:** Flat, lifeless wool. Sold cheap for winter coats.
        

### 2. Ozone-Milk (The Stimulant)

A glowing, blue-tinged liquid charged with bio-electricity.

- **Raw ("The Baker's Lightning"):**
    
    - Sold to bakeries for **"[[Thunder Buns]]"** (Static Leavening).
        
    - Consumed by laborers for a non-narcotic energy kick (like "Cocaine-Coffee").
        
- **Refined ("[[Aether-Clear]]"):**
    
    - Sold exclusively to the **Quill-Kin**.
        
    - Fermented with **Ghost Flowers** to create the world's only Anti-Chaos medicine.
        

## IV. The Mystery: The "Tagging" System

Because [[Sky-Grazers]] "Fall Upward" when they die (drifting into space), there are no bodies to study.

- **The Role:** The Harriers are the only ones who physically touch the herds.
    
- **The Research:** They tag the animals with paint or ribbons.
    
- **The Data:** They are the primary source of intel on "The Vanishing," reporting when specific animals disappear near Dragonstone Prisons, fueling the theory that the Dragons eat the stragglers.
  
  **The "Cloud-Harriers" (The Cliff-Shearers)**

These aren't your typical Avian bankers or diplomats. These are the roughnecks of the Empire.

• **Location:** The **Northern Outer Cliffs**. This is the windiest, coldest, and most dangerous part of the Engineer’s mountain range.

• **The Culture:** While the capital Avians are obsessed with "Truth" in ledgers and contracts, the Cloud-Harriers are obsessed with **"True Aim"** and **"True Grit."** They are the cowboys of the sky. They likely view the soft, city-dwelling Avians (Owls/Condors) with mild disdain, even though they supply the wool that keeps the Empire rich.

**2. The Resource: "[[Voltaic Fleece]]" and "[[Ozone-Milk]]"**

You established that the wool is "zappy" because [[Sky-Grazers]] feed on static-charged clouds. This makes the resources incredibly unique.

• **[[Voltaic Fleece]] (The Wool):**

    ◦ **Properties:** It is naturally conductive but also highly resistant to Aetheric burn.

    ◦ **The "Zap":** Shearing it releases stored static charge. A shearer who isn't careful gets knocked unconscious or stops their own heart.

    ◦ **Utility:** This is the _only_ material capable of insulating the **Aetheric-Reaction Engines** (Chaos-Boilers) used by all airships. Without this wool, the engines would overheat or electrocute the crew. The Cloud-Harriers effectively control the safety of global flight.

• **[[Ozone-Milk]] (The Milk):**

    ◦ **Properties:** A glowing, blue-tinged liquid. It creates a tingling sensation when drunk.

    ◦ **Utility:** It is a potent **bio-electric stimulant**. It doesn't just wake you up; it sharpens reflexes. It is likely the base ingredient for the "combat tonics" used by the **[[Iron Caldera]]'s** Forge Dancers or the **[[Scute Confederacy]]'s** warriors.

**3. The Method: The "Frame-Drop" Maneuver**

Your description of "parking" the beast is a fantastic mechanical visual.

• **The Chute:** The Harriers use small, agile gliders to spook the herd, driving them into natural narrow canyons or "wind-tunnels" along the cliff face.

• **The Trap (The Clamp):** Massive, counter-weighted steel frames hang from the cliffs. When a Grazer passes underneath, the frame drops.

    ◦ _The Lock:_ It doesn't hurt the animal; it clamps over the bulbous body, using the animal's own buoyancy to pin it against the padded cliff wall.

• **The "Pit Stop":**

    ◦ This is a race against time. The Grazer is panicked and thrashing.

    ◦ **Shearers** repel down the cliff face. They use **Obsidian Blades** (glass doesn't conduct electricity) to strip the wool in seconds to avoid the shock.

    ◦ **Milkers** attach high-suction hoses to drain the udder tanks simultaneously.

• **The Release:** The frame counter-weights release, launching the Grazer back into the air stream, lighter and confused, but unharmed.

**4. The Cycle: The Seasonal Intercept**

• **Frequency:** You mentioned 4 times a year. This aligns with the seasonal shifts of the **Warden’s Reckoning** (Shadowburn, GreenSpan, Highreach, Dimfreeze).

• **The Danger Season:** The harvest during **Shadowburn (Summer)** is the most dangerous because the static charge in the air is highest, making the wool deadly to touch without grounding suits.

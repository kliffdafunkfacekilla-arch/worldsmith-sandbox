**🦝 Faction Profile: The Ring-Tail Guild**

*(The Hustlers / The Night-Shift / The Climbers)*

"Yeah, I can fix that roof. I can also trim your beard, sell you a vintage coat, and get you a bottle of cider that'll make you see God. But cash up front, pal. And don't mind the shades; the sun's a bit loud today."  
— Sean "Shades" O'Malley, Barber & Roofer

## ---

**1.0 Overview: The Engine of Commerce**

While the Rats own the banks and the Otters own the boats, the **Raccoons** run the shops. They are the ubiquitous middle-class and working-poor of the Heartland.

* **The Vibe:** *1920s New York Irish Immigrants*.  
  * They are scrappy, loud, hard-working, and talk with a fast, melodic patter.  
  * They are "Night Owls." Their shops often open at noon and stay open until 4 AM.  
* **The Look:**  
  * **Daylight:** They always wear dark-tinted round sunglasses or goggles because their eyes are sensitive.  
  * **Attire:** Flat caps, suspenders, and aprons for the men; flowing, patterned "Bohemian" shawls and skirts for the women.

## ---

**2.0 Gender & Trades: A Split Culture**

### **The Men: The Climbers & Pitchmen**

Male Raccoon culture is focused on **Service** and **Heights**.

* **The Climbers:** Because they are natural climbers, they monopolize the "High Trades." Roofing, window washing, steeple-painting, and chimney sweeping. If it requires a ladder, a Raccoon does it better without one.  
* **The Salesmen:** They are natural talkers. They work the counters of high-end jewelry stores (often selling goods for Rat investors) or run chaotic **Bazaars** and Flea Markets full of second-hand treasures.  
* **The Barbers:** Raccoon Barber Shops are legendary. They are "Men Only" social clubs where the gossip is hot, the shaves are close, and the black-market whiskey is poured into coffee cups.

### **The Women: The Bohemian Matrons**

Female Raccoon culture is distinct—earthier and more "Hippie."

* **The Style:** Long, braided hair, bangles, and loose, colorful clothing. They have a "Mother Earth" vibe but with a sharp business edge.  
* **The Trades:**  
  * **Tree-Farming:** They cultivate orchards in the upper canopies of giant trees.  
  * **Brewing:** They make the best **Hard Cider** in the world. It’s thick, sweet, and dangerous.  
  * **Hospitality:** They run the best Inns and Bakeries. Their establishments are cluttered, cozy, and smell like cinnamon and yeast.

## ---

**3.0 The Economy: Fronts & Moonshine**

**The "Front" System**

* **The Deal:** Many Raccoon shops are actually owned by the **Rats** (The Golden Warrens).  
* **The Role:** The Raccoon is the "Face." They run the pawnshop or the boutique legitimately, while the Rat moves illicit money through the back books.  
* **The Skill:** Raccoons are excellent at "Upselling." They can convince a Horse Noble that a rusty watch is a "vintage antique."

**The Tree-Stills (Moonshine)**

* **The Operation:** Along the dense Southern Border, Raccoons run illegal alcohol stills high up in the hollows of ancient trees.  
* **The Product:** "Pine-Needle Gin" and "Stump-Water Whiskey."  
* **Distribution:** They use their migrant farm work as cover to move the booze across the country.

## ---

**4.0 Society & Social Mobility**

**The Migrant Workforce**

* In harvest season, thousands of young Raccoons travel the countryside picking fruit for the [[Deer farmers]]. They are the "Migrant Hands"—hard-working, singing songs in the fields, and sleeping in barns.

**The "Street Urchins" (Youth)**

* **The Apprenticeship:** Young Raccoons are often used by the Crime Families (Rats/Otters) as lookouts, pickpockets, or cat-burglars because they are small and quiet.  
* **The Ceiling:** Most stop doing this when they get older. The Crime Families rarely let Raccoons rise to the top (they aren't "Made"), so most Raccoons eventually settle down to open a legitimate shop.  
* **The Legend:** There are whispers of a global **"Guild of Thieves"** that recruits the very best Raccoon burglars, but no one admits to belonging to it.

**The Nobility (The Lucky Few)**

* **The New Money:** Through sheer salesmanship and business acumen, two Raccoon families (The **O'Dooles** and the **Fitzroys**) have achieved Noble Status.  
* **The Stigma:** The Horse Nobles look down on them as "Nouveau Riche," but the Raccoons don't care—their mansions are just as big, and their parties are louder.

## ---

**5.0 Religion: The Shadow-Saints**

Raccoons share a strange religious bond with the **Bats**.

* **The Faith:** They worship **"The Lady of the Lantern"** (A deity of Night, Secrets, and Safe Passage).  
* **The Connection:** Most Raccoons attend the Official Church, but their services are held at dusk.  
* **Magic:** Raccoons who show magical potential (Clerics/Paladins) almost always join the Church hierarchy. They are the "Lantern-Bearers" who protect travelers in the dark.

## ---

**6.0 The Military Quirk**

A small number of Raccoons join the Wolf Army.

* **The Visual:** A Raccoon in a uniform looks... wrong. The helmet is usually too big, and they constantly fidget.  
* **The Role:** They don't march in the line. They are **Quartermasters** (Supply) or **Sappers** (Combat Engineers/Climbers). They are the guys who can get you a fresh pair of boots or a bottle of wine in the middle of a war zone.

## ---

**1.0 Overview: The Swamp-Water Spies**

To the public, the Otters are the **Barge-Pilots** and **Dockworkers**. They are the smiling, hard-working immigrants who keep the grain moving.

* **The Reality:** They are the unofficial Intelligence Agency of the Heartland. They handle the "Wet Work"—smuggling, extraction, and surveillance.  
* **The Vibe:** *Bayou Noir* meets *Folklore*.  
  * They operate out of smoky, lantern-lit **River Taverns** built on stilts.  
  * They are deeply superstitious. They wear charms, believe in omens, and fear "The Drying."  
* **The Look:** Long waterproof trench coats (to hide weapons and wet fur), wide-brimmed hats adorned with river-bird feathers.

## ---

**2.0 The Rare Magic: "The Fog-Singers"**

Most Otters are not magical; they are just skilled, slippery fighters. However, a rare few are born with **"The Delta Gift."** These individuals are not leaders; they are **Specialist Assets** (Fixers) used for high-stakes missions.

### **The Rank: "The Singer"**

* **Role:** They are the Syndicate’s heavy artillery. A Krewe might only have one or two Singers.  
* **The Power (Atmospherics):** They don't throw fireballs. They hum low, vibrating dissonant chords that interact with the water.  
  * **The Shroud:** They can call up a thick, unnatural **River Fog** in minutes to cover an escape or a raid.  
  * **The** Current: They can control the ebb and flow of currents capsizing a vessel or beaching it. Escape on the waterways is no use.

### **The Tools: Folk-Alchemy**

For the non-magical Otters, they rely on **Charms** and **Alchemy** derived from the Old Country.

* **"Dock-Oil":**  a grease used to water treat timbers that can make a Aquatic Very uncomfortable when covered in it making them hydrophobic. Being placed in water without getting wet makes them start talking everytime. Especially when a dry mummified body is pulled from the waterways.  
* **"Whisper-Pearls":** Rare, ritually attuned pearls. If you hold one and whisper, the matching pearl vibrates. It’s a short-range, magical walkie-talkie used by infiltration teams.

## ---

**3.0 Structure: The Krewes**

**The Papa / The Mama (The Bosses)**

* They are not kings; they are community leaders. They hold court in the back rooms of Taverns, eating crawfish and listening to requests.  
* **Authority:** They rely on respect and superstition. A Papa doesn't need to threaten you; he just reminds you that the River Spirits are watching and they always get their mojo.

**The Pilots (The Captains)**

* The masters of the barge. They know the **"Sunken Roads"**—the hidden currents and deep channels that allow them to move contraband faster than any patrol boat.

**The Runners (The Muscle)**

* The foot soldiers. They wear newsie caps and work the docks. They are expert knife-fighters and swimmers.

**The Frogmen (The Elite)**

* The "SEAL Team" equivalent. They wear black neoprene and rebreathers made of hollow reeds.  
* **The Mounts:** They use large crawfish species as under water cargo movers or  personal propulsion units.   These aren't magical monsters; they are just trained beasts of burden for silent underwater transport.

## ---

**4.0 Traditions & Superstition**

**The "Gris-Gris" (Charms)**

* Every Otter wears a **Mojo bag** around their neck. It contains dried mud,  specific bones, and herbs.Collected and prepared by a priest or priestess(often a matriarch in the family)  
* **Belief:** They believe this protects them from bad luck. If an Otter loses their bag, they will refuse to continue until it is replaced. They often make new ones  before big jobs or events following recipes given to them during a divination session.

**The Tavern Culture**

* **The Venues:** They run **"Dens"**—wooden structures built half-over the water. The air is thick with smoke from "Marsh-Weed" pipes.  
* **The Music:** A solitary Otter playing a harmonica or a "Gut-Bucket" bass in the corner. It’s soulful, sad, and quiet. It signals that "Business is happening."  
* **The Legit Investing:** They often front taverns  and inns or even street carts selling food or Flowers.

**The Oaths**

* **"The Brackish Bond":** An oath sworn on a skin of salt water over fresh water. To break it is to invite the "Salt-Rot" into your lungs, and have the drowned ones come for you soul.

## ---

**5.0 Operations: The Deep State**

**The "Ghost Barges"**

* They run barges that don't officially exist. They are painted matte black and have reeds and bits of foliage and fake bits of turf, so they look like part of the shallows or bank.   Moving only at night, often guided by a **Singer** who keeps them shrouded in fog.  Keep them hidden in secret alcoves and overhangs they have mapped in their head , each Krewe has their own stretch they use and know , it's an insult to ride another's currents or use their screens or bluffs.  
* **Cargo:** They move the things the [[Wolves]] need but can't ask for: weapons for black-ops, exiled spies, and illicit gold.

**The "Sink-Holes"**

* If a body needs to disappear, the Syndicate has specific spots in the river with dangerous undertows.  
* **The Saying:** *"He went to checkThe currents."* (He was thrown in the river with weights).

## ---

**6.0 Relationships**

**Vs. The Rats (The Golden Warrens)**

* **The Dynamic:** *The Mystic vs. The Materialist.*  
* **The Conflict:** The Rats think Otter superstitions are "quaint nonsense." The Otters think the Rats are soulless.  
* **The Balance:** The Rats hire the Otters for small non narcotics smuggling or passengers. Just enough to show respect and keep the peace but they have their own pipelines in which the Otters are dying to figure out but take the jobs and pretend they're not looking.

**Vs. The [[Beavers]] (The Iron-Tail Lodges)**

* **The Dynamic:** *The Old Rivals.*  
* **The Interaction:** A Beaver Engineer builds a dam. An Otter Shaman hangs a chicken bone on it to "bless the flow." The Beaver rolls his eyes but leaves it there, *just in case*. And maybe leaves an iron grate only half bolted and might find a gift in his toolbox instead of poisonous snakes the next day.

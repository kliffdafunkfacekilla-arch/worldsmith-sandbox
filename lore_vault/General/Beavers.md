**🦫 Faction Profile: The Iron-Tail Lodges**

*(The Mud-Masons / The Grand Architects / "Local 404")*

"A Wolf can kill a man. A Rat can bankrupt him. But if you want to stop a river that has been flowing for ten thousand years? You call a Beaver. Now, pass me that rune-chisel and tell the General to move his tank; he's parked on a ley-line."  
— Grand Foreman "Stone-Tooth" Miller, Keeper of the Western Sluice

## ---

**1.0 Overview: The Geomancers of Infrastructure**

The Beavers are the backbone of the Heartland. They combine advanced civil engineering with **"Runic-Binding"** magic.

* **The Role:** They maintain the Great Dam (The Valerius Wall), the Canal Locks, and the Fortresses.Maintain all the complex irrigation and sewer systems. Repersentedd by an elected Grand Master who sits in on senate meetings and organizes the work orders. Without them, the Heartland floods in a day.  
* **The Vibe:** *Deep-Magic Union Hall*. They are gruff, independent contractors who treat magic like a trade skill. They don't chant in robes; they shout commands while wearing enchanted leather aprons and drinking heavy stout.  
* **The Philosophy:** **"The Flow Must Be Managed."** They view the chaotic forces of the world (Water, Magic, Politics) as things that must be channeled through rigid structures.

## ---

**2.0 Structure: The Lodge System**

Beaver society is built on **The Lodge**. It is a mix of a Trade Union, a Local Pub, and an Ancient Secret Society.

### **The Surface Lodges (The Union Halls)**

* **Function:** Public meeting houses found in every town. This is where contracts are signed, grievances are aired, and the "Amber-Sap Lager" flows freely.  
* **The Independent Crews:** Every third Beaver runs their own "Company."  
  * *A company consists of 3 to 100s of workers led by master builders and build captains.*  
  * They fiercely argue and negotiate for contracts through the lodges but unite instantly if a non-Beaver tries to interfere.

### **The Deep Lodges (The "Shriners")**

* **Location:** Ancient, air-filled stone chambers hidden deep beneath the lakes and riverbeds, accessible only by underwater tunnels.  
* **The "Old Families":** Membership is hereditary. These are the descendants of the First Builders.  
* **The Function:** This is the Shadow Government of the Infrastructure.  
  * They wear ceremonial sashes and heavy stone rings.  
  * They control the **"Master Blueprints"** of the entire nation.  
  * They decide who gets the "Prime Contracts" and which Wolf General gets his permit denied for "unspecified zoning violations."

## ---

**3.0 Magic & Tech: The Art of "The Anchor"**

* **The Tail:** By slapping their tails on the earth or water, they send **Geotremors**.  
* **Utility:** A Foreman can signal a crew through the ground.  
* **Combat:** An angry Beaver is fierce and likely to use loud tail slaps to disorient an injury with powerful bites or trip with tail swings that can snap ankles. Where there's one there's usually many. The saying goes”Angry Beavers are a flood you can't swim to safety from”  
* **The Craft:** the master builders are experts of runic magic passed down through the generations. Practical magic that uses complex sigils that focus latent and natural energy  requiring no talent with magic to use. They carve "Binding Runes" directly into the foundations of buildings, making them magically resistant to siege damage. There overall designs incorporate this in the very structure most times without realizing it.  
* **The Trait:** Their teeth are reinforced with natural iron deposits.  
* **The Chew:** They chew on **"Focus Sticks"** (Ironwood dowels).Helps them with concentration.

**3\. The Trowel & Apron**

* **The Tool:** Their flat trowels are often heirlooms passed down for generations. They can smooth stone and mud into a seamless, glass-like surface.  
* **The Gear:** They wear **"Lodge Builders Leathers."** water proof Heavy aprons or vests that absorb impacts and have many loops and pockets. Protecting them and acting as a tool belt. Also a status symbol the more elaborate the vest the more senior the builder.

  4.0 Culture: Small-Town Dynasties

### **The Matriarchs (The Lodge Mothers)**

While the men posture about "Union Rules," the women run the social fabric.

* **The “Fur":** They style the fur and spend lots of time at “salons”. They wear excessive amounts of pearl jewelry (bought from Otters)Fancyier the hair and bigger the pearls the more power the Beveress has. Salon meetings and Salad buffets are where much of the decisions are made, whether the men admit it or no. Many lodge decisions are rendered meaningless by the manipulation of the “Wives”  
* **The Power:** They broker power through **Casseroles**.  
  * If a Lodge Mother brings a "Hot Dish" to a Wolf General's wife, it means a political alliance has been formed.  
  * If she ignores you at the grocery market, your social standing is destroyed.  
* **The Matchmaking:** Arranged marriages are "forbidden," but somehow, the son of the Concrete Magnate and the daughter of the Timber Baron always end up "accidentally" locked in a supply closet together during the Summer Picnic.

### **The Grudge (Bureaucratic Warfare)**

* **The Weapon:** The Permit.  
* **The Scenario:** A Raccoon Lawyer tries to evict a Beaver family. Suddenly, the Raccoon's office is declared a "Seismic Hazard Zone." The road leading to the office is closed for "Emergency Paving" that takes six years.  
* **The Motto:** *"We don't get mad. We get Busy."*

### **The Teenage Rebellion**

* Young Beavers often find Lodge life suffocating. They dream of being Bards or Duelists. They hang around the docks or “Rat Town” , some  getting into some minor criminal activity. Others work the Barges and travel, Some end up finding a place elsewhere  as their work is respected everywhere and many cities fund small crews of Beavers for their sewer and aquifer work if they can manage.   
* **The Return:** By age 30, the "Call of the Mud" hits. They return home , and take over the family business, complaining about "kids these days" within a week.

**5.0 Relationships (The Ecosystem)**

**Vs. The Otters (The Syndicate)**

* **The Dynamic:** *The Odd Couple.*  
* **The Friction:** Beavers build walls; Otters want holes in them.  
* **The Peace:** They grew up in the same river.  
  * A Deep Lodge Foreman will agree to install a "Maintenance Hatch" (Smuggling Tunnel) in a new dam, provided the Otter Krewe pays for the Lodge's new roof and keeps the cheap narcotics away from the Beaver kids. They play ball but refuse anything dangerous or harmful to the community.

**Vs. The Rats (The Golden Warrens)**

* **The Dynamic:** *Labor vs. Management.*  
* **The Friction:** The Rats finance the construction projects and constantly demand cost-cutting. The Beavers refuse to compromise safety for profit.  
* **The Interaction:** A Rat in a  robe making demands at a Beaver in a hard hat is a daily sight. The Beaver usually just nods and  ignores the Rat whenl he leaves.

**Vs. The [[Wolves]] (The Military)**

* **The Dynamic:** *The Contractor vs. The Client.*  
* **The Friction:** [[Wolves]] view forts as strategic points. Beavers view them as their babies.  
* **The Quote:** *"General, you cannot mount a cannon on that turret. The vibration will crack the lintel. I don't care if the enemy is coming; You fight them and ill fix this"*

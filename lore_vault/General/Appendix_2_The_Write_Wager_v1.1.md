## Captain Jax

*Source file: `People/Captain Jax.md`*

### **Captain Jax: The Pilot**

#### **Appearance & Mannerisms**

Jax is a massive **Catfish-Kin** with slick, slate-grey skin that feels like wet leather. He carries a permanent scowl due to a **brass plate** bolted to the left side of his face; the hardware consists of a D-shaped hinge at the jaw-point with a narrow strip along the jawline, forming a "P" shape lying on its back. The metal pulls taut at his skin, a "gift" he received as a youth that he claims he "never got to say thanks for."

His long, sensitive whiskers are his true tell, twitching and swaying to betray his emotional state even when his face remains a mask. Usually shirtless, he wears a salt-crusted leather vest bulging with odds and ends—hooks, pocket knives, or a sextant—which he produces with practiced ease. Most notably, he keeps a stash of hard candies in those pockets, tossing them to any children he encounters with a wink and a sharp "shush" before anyone can witness his softness.

He wears a round, tarred sailor’s hat adorned with a **solid purple ribbon**. This is a striking anomaly, as traditional ribbons use patterns of silver, white, and gold to denote rank and vessel; Jax’s purple ribbon is tied directly to the mystery of his jaw injury. He is rarely seen without an ornate, jet-black carved pipe clenched in his teeth. He punctuates matter-of-fact statements by striking a match and staring through puffs of smoke. Even when destitute, he never seems to run out of tobacco or matches, and is apt to be found lighting up even when soaking wet, usually just after saying, "I told you so."

#### **Backstory**

The son of a peg-legged Navy ferryman and a craftsman mother who made traditional river stone accessories, Jax grew up in the **Lake Town** docks. Drawn to the "shady" blue-collar life, he dropped out of school to work for an under-crew of the **Otters** (the River-Folk syndicate).

His life took a turn when he was asked to assist an outside smuggling crew as a favor. During the delivery, Jax realized the cargo was explosives destined for terrorists attacking the Heartlands. Refusing to be a party to "Bad Mojo," Jax used his longboat’s wake to shove the terrorists' barge into a whirlpool. In a moment of conscience, he rescued the drowning driver, but a beaded bracelet Jax wore—a common tradition—linked him to the scene.

Though the Otters protected him from official blame, the smuggler's son—an arrogant "boy playing a man's game"—confronted Jax on a quiet pier. A fight broke out, and as Jax stepped back to avoid further violence, he tripped on a loose board. The smuggler’s son lunged with an ornate knife, catching Jax in the jaw. Jax fell into his boat, unconscious and bleeding, and drifted into the night.

He was found by officials and, to avoid smuggling charges, took a "service instead of jail" plea deal. He eventually rose to the rank of **Barge Captain**, the highest his criminal record allowed. His final fall from grace occurred when he "accidentally" rammed a **Corsair** ship, crippling their rudder and "losing" his cargo barge so a starving village could scavenge the grain. His crew found him passed out at the wheel—a ruse to cover his tracks—but River-Folk merchant law valued profit over his silent heroism. He was hit with a "dishonorable discharge for drunkenness," stripped of his merchant earrings (denoting rank and wealth), and abandoned at **[[Port Sunder]]** with nothing but a bottle of Scute fire-brandy.

#### **Quirks & Personality**

- **Vocalizations:** He subvocalizes in a low growl when he senses "bad weather"—environmental or social—muttering words like "fool" or "idiot."
    
- **Functionalism:** He remains a "functional" alcoholic, claiming the swill helps him navigate around the "bad weather" of life.
    

#### **Strengths**

- **Sailor’s Intuition:** He can feel a **Chaos Surge** in his whiskers minutes before instruments detect it.
    
- **Precision Pilot:** He is a master of heavy-vessel maneuvering, capable of threading massive ships through impossible gaps.
    

#### **Weaknesses**

- **Deep-Seated Cynicism:** Jax struggles to trust authority figures.
    
- **The Protector’s Rashness:** He is prone to making dangerous, impulsive decisions if he feels the vulnerable are being exploited by the powerful.

### **Celandine Valeroot : The Whisperer**

#### **Appearance & Mannerisms**

Celandine possesses the unshakable poise of the high-canopy estates. Her skin is pristine, alabaster **white birch bark** that feels like fine parchment, contrasted by a cascading "headdress" of **drooping tropical petals** in sunset hues. She carries herself with a rigid, elegant posture, her movements fluid and deliberate, as if moving through a ballroom even when walking across a grease-slicked deck.

She wears a high-collared, multi-layered hoop gown of **Pollen-Silk**. Though the edges are frayed and stained by the industrial soot of [[Port Sunder]], she maintains the garment with obsessive care. She is never seen without her **silver-handled lace parasol**, which she uses to maintain a "polite distance" between herself and the unwashed world. Her voice is like honey—smooth and slow—masking a razor-sharp wit. She is visibly pained by the lack of etiquette on _The Write Wager_, often sighing at Kog’s table manners or Hazard’s unbuttoned jacket.

#### **Backstory: The Gilded Wilt**

- **The High-Born Lineage:** Born into one of the **Great Floral Houses**, Celandine grew up in a culture where information is the only true currency. She was trained from birth in the **"Art of the Ear"**—the ability to track rumors, parse gossip, and weaponize secrets to maintain social standing.
    
- **The Social Architect:** As a master seamstress and virtuoso of the **Sap-Harp**, she was the center of high-society salons. She used her position to weave "narrative webs," making or breaking reputations with a single whispered sentence during a dress fitting.
    
- **The Great Embarrassment:** Her downfall was orchestrated by a lover who feigned a high-stakes courtship. Celandine was tricked into believing she was being courted by a high-ranking official, only to find it was a total fabrication designed to humiliate her. For a "Whisperer," being blind to a secret is cultural death. To avoid the pity and mockery of the court, she arranged a "self-exile" under the guise of a trade diplomat post abroad.
    
- **The Descent:** She fled with trunks of finery and a staff of servants, but her elitism and naivety proved disastrous on the road. Bandits and offended locals stripped her of her belongings until only her carriage and her childhood footman—an old shrub servant—remained. When the carriage broke an axle, they sold it and journeyed on foot to [[Port Sunder]]. Upon arrival, she discovered the "diplomatic post" was another cruel trick; it didn't exist.
    
- **Survival in the Gutter:** Defeated and alone, she freed her footman from his duty and survived by cutting up her own finery to make dresses for the few wealthy women in the mid-class district. These clients kept her secret to protect their own reputations, but in the process, Celandine rebuilt her gossip network. She began leveraging secrets to help the people around her, earning a quiet respect. She joined Clary’s expedition by choice, seeing it as a way to escape the filth and prove her skills on a global stage.
    

#### **The Whisperer’s Logic**

Celandine doesn't see people; she sees **"Reputations"** and **"Vulnerabilities."**

- **The Gossip’s Weaponry:** She views every rumor as a tool. She can dismantle an enemy’s confidence simply by mentioning a specific "forgotten" debt or an old, hidden affair.
    
- **The Lady’s Burden:** She has taken it upon herself to make Clary a "Proper Lady." She constantly critiques Clary’s posture, grammar, and "vulgar" habit of whittling in public. She is a relentless source of annoyance to the crew, demanding high-brow decorum even during life-and-death repairs.
    

#### **Quirks**

- **The Sap-Harp:** A fragile, seemingly grown instrument with strings made of glass-like sap. Its music is hauntingly beautiful but requires a clean environment to resonate properly.
    
- **The Handkerchief:** She frequently dabs at her nose with a lavender-scented silk handkerchief to block out the "stink of the commoners."
    

#### **Strengths**

- **Social Sabotage:** She can talk the crew into or out of almost any situation. Her ability to read power dynamics is unmatched.
    
- **Environmental Sentinel:** As a biological plant, she is a living barometer. She can feel the health of the local ecosystem.
    
- **The "Uncivilized" Shift:** While usually poised, she loses all composure when witnessing wanton abuse of nature. If she sees someone poisoning a spring or hacking a sacred tree, her charm vanishes, replaced by a terrifying, primal ferocity.
    

#### **Weaknesses**

- **Sensory Overload:** High pollution and chemical smog physically wilt her, making her lethargic and "grey."
    
- **High-Society Blindness:** She often refuses to believe that someone "low-born" could outwit her, leading to a dangerous sense of superiority.
#### **Character Dynamics**

- **With Clary:** She views the fox as a "diamond in the rough." She is determined to turn Clary into a "Fox of Status," much to Clary’s exhaustion, though she has developed a deep, genuine fondness for her.
    
- **With the Crew:** She treats Jax and Hazard like "difficult servants." She is the only one who can make the massive Kog feel self-conscious about his hygiene or tail-tapping.

## Clary, The First Archivist

#### **1.0 Biographical Summary: From Ash to Ink**

### **The Origin of the First Archivist: The Weight of Chaos**

Clary’s history is not recorded in the archives she built. It is thought she was born in a nameless village in the **Heartlands**, a place that no longer exists on any map. Her home was not destroyed by war or fire, but by the **Aetheric Tension** itself. During a particularly violent **Shadow Week**, her village was suddenly surrounded by a **Chaos Zone**.

With their village warped and dangerous, Clary and her father left in search of a new home. Neighboring villages were no better off; the roads were full of refugees. Upon reaching the capital, they fared no better, and her father wasted no time searching elsewhere. For a year they traveled, surviving through his skills as a carpenter or whatever hard labor could get them a meal and a barn to sleep in. In these farms and small towns, Clary first started collecting stories. She noticed the other children all told slightly different variations of the folklore in the area and found that often the differences fit together—like they all had a piece, but none had all of it.

She realized early on that the "myths" people sang about were actually fragmented data points—patterns of moon-phases and gravity-shifts hidden in folk songs. Asking her father about it, he said, "That's often the way of it; never have the whole truth, but almost always enough to get it wrong." During their long walks, her father would try and answer her questions about the world as best he could. He showed her what he did know: silly rhymes and poems, a few old folk songs his grandpa used to sing, and tricks like carving wooden toys, throwing knives, and juggling.

Once, a farm wife was so impressed by Clary’s juggling that she shared lunch with them. Listening to Clary’s stories, the woman offered to let them stay and refused to let her father work for the privilege. This soon became their way of life: juggling and showy acrobatics, with storytelling as it got late, became their bread and butter.

They never did find a permanent place to call home. The towns and villages were friendly, but it didn't take much to see the loose clothes and bony statures—food was scarce and winter was coming. They kept moving and avoided the larger cities, as those were worse off. As the fall grew long, they found themselves by the **Mill River** and met a group of traveling **Ursines** passing through to their hearths from a trading trip. Being friendly, they shared a meal, and by morning the head merchant asked them to join the caravan for company.

They traveled in a large wagon with a **Hearth** built in the center. The grey-furred merchant loved sharing stories with Clary and even agreed with her about there being missing pieces, showing her his own journal of stories collected on his travels. As they neared the **Ursine** border, the merchant spoke with her father while Clary slept. He sympathized with their situation and offered to take them into his hearth. Her father could work as a groundskeeper and furniture maker, and most importantly, his "clever girl" could study in his library. He promised to help her enter the **Hall of Scholars** once she passed the testing. He insisted, saying his own family was gone now, and a hearth deserved a family.

It was agreed, and Clary was excited. But a few nights later at the border, they were set upon by a group of **Worm Cultists**, misshapen and horrifying. The night filled with screams. Scared, the merchant set Clary in a chest, chained it shut, and hid her. Peering through a crack, she watched as the gentle merchant took off his vest and glasses, suddenly becoming a ferocious, unstoppable force of destruction. As the cart rocked, the chest tipped over, and she could see no more.

The remains of the group buried the dead and moved on. Clary was silent for a long time until they reached her new home. She spent the next few years in the manor, studying in the library and learning woodcraft from the groundskeepers and the **Signal Fire Code** from the **Hearth Masters**. She collected stories from anyone she could. As she grew to a young adult, the merchant set up her exam to enter the school. She was excited, but the night before the exam, an unexpected eclipse caused a **Chaos Shift**. A section of the mountain destabilized, causing an avalanche that destroyed the manor and the surrounding homes.

Slightly injured, Clary survived, but the merchant did not. Watching everyone devastated by the disaster, she grew angry—angry that she couldn't predict when it would happen. Once she healed, she left with nothing but a fragmented journal and her knack for storytelling, spending years as a nomadic performer before finding the trader in the tavern.

1. **The Carpentry Influence:** Her father was a carpenter explains why she views the world as a **Mechanical Structure** (Clockwork) rather than a magical one. To her, reality is a house that was built poorly.
    
2. **The Ursine "Hearth" Legacy:** She learned the **Signal Fire Code** and the value of a "Center." This is why she wants to map the world—to find a stable center for everyone who is "Hearthless."
    
3. **The "Unexpected" Trauma:** Twice, her life was destroyed by events she didn't see coming (the village drift and the avalanche). Her "anger" is her primary industrial fuel. She isn't researching for "wonder"; she is researching to ensure she is **never surprised by chaos again.**

####**2.0 The Port Sunder Wager**

Clary’s transition from entertainer to power-player occurred in **Port Sunder**. While staying in a grimy silt-dock tavern, she observed a trader whose fleet was grounded because his **Phase-Charts** had "rotted."

Using her cross-referenced notes on the ** **Cruorbus** Moon**, Clary wagered her last coin that she could predict the exact torque of the local **Chaos Zone**. When the ground physically shifted 1 degree to the west exactly as she predicted, causing a nearby road to veer and a fault line to elevate slightly, she didn't just win a pouch of gold—she won the attention of the world’s three most dangerous factions.

---

#### **3.0 The Triple-Hustle: A Dangerous Equilibrium**

Clary realized that if a single power owned the ability to predict the land's movement, they would use it to enslave the rest. To prevent this, she successfully convinced three rival groups to fund a "purely academic" expedition:

|**Investor**|**Alias Used**|**Promised Data**|**Funding Provided**|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Gilded Compass**|Heartland Wind-Steward|Wind & Airflow Patterns|A heavy bag of **Aetherium**|
|**Sky-Barons**|Canopy Mineralogist|Earth & Mineral Deposits|**The Write Wager** (Ship)|
|**Crimson Corsairs**|Reliance Tide-Watcher|Stars & Aetheric Tides|Cracked **Dragonstone** & Gear|

By promising each group a different "piece" of the truth, she ensured they would keep her alive, never realizing that she was using their combined resources to solve the mystery of the **Twelve Dragons**. Stringing them along feeding them part of but never the whole truth she traveled for many years piecing together almost everything we know about the world , and she use that knowledge to form the Order of the Clockwork , in the hopes of fixing what others broke.
#### **4.0 #### **1. The Lex-Binder (The Combined Journal)**

- **The Construction:** The book is a mismatched behemoth. Half of it is bound in thick, scarred **Ursine** leather (the Merchant’s side), and the other half is bound in utilitarian **Heartland** carpenter’s hide (Clary’s side).
    
- **The Content:** It is filled with two different handwritings. The Merchant’s script is flowing and poetic; Clary’s is cramped, scientific, and surrounded by geometric diagrams of moon phases.
    
- **The Industrial Function:** Clary uses it to "anchor" her thoughts. In a world where gravity and geography shift, having a physical record of "What was true yesterday" is her only way to maintain her sanity.
    

#### **2. The Throwing Knives (The Carpenter’s Edge)**

- **The Gear:** She doesn't carry elegant daggers. She carries **Carpenter’s Marking Knives**—flat, single-bevel steel blades designed for scoring wood.
    
- **The Hidden Threat:** She keeps one tucked into her sleeve and three more in a bandolier hidden under her duster.
    
- **The Logic:** Having been in a chest during a cultist massacre, Clary has a biological imperative to never be "trapped" or "defenseless" again. She is small, so she relies on range and precision—skills she honed juggling.
    

#### **3. The Subconscious Whittling**

- **The Habit:** When Clary is stressed or deep in calculation (like during the **Shadow Week** drift), she pulls out a scrap of **Ghost-Teak** and a small chisel.
    
- **The Output:** She doesn't even look at her hands. By the end of a navigation briefing, she has carved a small, perfect figure—usually a **Bear**, a **Wolf**, or a **Gear**.
    
- **The Tell:** These figures are the "physical debris" of her thinking process. The crew often finds these little wooden icons left on the bridge or in the engine room.

#### **5.0 Personal Philosophy: Logic as a Shield**

Clary does not believe in "Magic" as a mystical force. To her, the world is a broken machine—the **Celestial Clockwork**—that has slipped its gears. Her mission is to gather enough data to perform a "Grand Repair."

_"The stories say the gods left us. My data says they simply fell asleep at the wheel. I’m not here to pray for them to wake up; I’m here to take the wheel myself."_ — **Clary, Entry 001**

### **The Songbird: The Navigator**

#### **Appearance & Mannerisms**

Songbird is a **Red-winged Blackbird Avian**. His plumage is naturally a deep, glossy jet-black, though years in Port Sunder have matted his feathers with an oily, charcoal-grey soot. His most striking features are his brilliant crimson-and-yellow shoulder epaulets (the "Red-wings"), once vibrant symbols of status that are now ruffled and dull. His primary flight feathers have been ritually notched and clipped, leaving him permanently flightless.

His attire is a "Ghost of a Uniform"—a high-collared **Compass Navy** dress jacket worn unbuttoned and wrinkled. It lacks official medals and pomp, hanging loose on his frame to create a rakish, "Handsome Rogue" silhouette. He wears a weather-beaten tricorn hat with the **"Prime of Moons"** card—the luckiest or unluckiest card in the deck depending on the game—tucked into the hatband. He often tilts the brim low to hide his eyes when bluffing.

When deep in thought, he produces a pouch to hand-roll a thin smoke, which he enjoys through a long holder. When forced to read star-charts or a hand of cards, he reluctantly pulls out delicate, gold-rimmed reading glasses. He is deeply embarrassed by them, viewing them as a sign of aging and "grounded" weakness; he will quickly pocket them if a woman (especially **Leaf**) enters the room, even if it leaves him squinting blindly. When startled, scared, or caught in a lie, his feathers physically puff up, doubling his visual size and utterly ruining his "cool" persona.

#### **Backstory: The High-Born Heart**

- **The First Love & The Year of Ill-Luck:** In his youth, Songbird was the golden child of the **Gilded Compass**, betrothed to a high-born songstress. Before the wedding, he departed on a three-day mapping expedition. In his haste, he forgot to leave a tail feather with his betrothed—an ancient tradition for voyagers. His shipmate was mortified, warning of certain doom, but Songbird laughed it off as superstition.
    
    On the second day, while executing an order to return home, a stray sunbeam reflected off a puddle and hit the Captain’s badge of rank, blinding him for a split second. The ship scraped a cliffside, causing a minor hull breach. By sheer chance, the only items lost were the specific tools needed for repair. This triggered a year-and-a-half-long chain of impossible coincidences—broken wheels, torn jackets, and freak snowstorms—that delayed his return until his lady had forgotten him and married another. He has never forgiven himself for "Luck" failing him that day.
    
- **The Rogue’s Mask:** He remained in service for years, barely avoiding court-martials as he transitioned into a reckless gambler. This culminated in a scandalous affair with a **Compass Admiral’s wife**. To preserve the Admiral’s dignity, he avoided an execution but was subjected to a dishonorable discharge and **Social Erasure**. His name was struck, his wings were clipped, and he was exiled. He eventually gambled his way to the "Gutter" of Port Sunder, hoping to win enough in the High-Roosts to buy passage to the **Hippo’s Run** riverboat casino tournament.
    

#### **The Card Player’s Logic**

Songbird plays the Ostrakan equivalent of Poker using a deck of **12-suited Fae Cards**.

- **The Honest Bluff:** Biologically unable to tell an "Open-Face Lie" as an Avian, he has mastered the **Incomplete Truth**. He tells you exactly what cards he holds, but in a manner so "suspicious" that opponents are convinced he is bluffing. While ladies often view him as a silver-tongued devil, men find him infuriating to play against, leading to frequent trouble.
    
- **The Drunk’s Deception:** When intoxicated, his brain "flips." He finds it hilarious to tell the most obvious, ridiculous lies (e.g., _"I am actually the King of the Heartlands"_), genuinely believing his deception is incredibly subtle.
    

#### **Quirks**

- **Card Shuffling:** He shuffles his deck with hypnotic speed when showing off or negotiating.
    
- **The Long Holder:** His hand-rolled smokes are a constant companion during navigation.
    

#### **Strengths**

- **Celestial Musicology:** He "hears" the vibrations of the **Aether**. He navigates _The Write Wager_ by tuning into these rhythms, trusting luck whenever the math is unsure.
    
- **Insider Knowledge:** He possesses intimate knowledge of the **Gilded Compass’s** inner workings and protocols.
    

#### **Weaknesses**

- **The Great Correction:** He is a slave to "Luck." He will risk the ship on a 50/50 gamble if he feels the universe "owes" him a win, or abruptly change course because someone is wearing the "wrong" color scarf.
    
- **Grounded Vertigo:** Despite his skills, he is terrified of the open sky. He must stay inside the cabin with the shutters closed; seeing the ground far below him triggers a total sensory shutdown.

### **Kog: The Engineer**

#### **Appearance & Mannerisms**

Kog is a massive **Monitor Lizard-Kin** with thick, **Midnight Blue/Purple** scales that have a matte finish from decades of industrial toil. His coloration lightens to a softer shade at his face, hands, and the tip of his tail. His most striking feature is the vertical **milk-white scar** over his left eye. This eye possesses heightened heat vision but lacks normal sight, rendering everything a white blur except in extreme cold or darkness.

To manage this, Kog wears custom-made **Forge Goggles** where each lens can be flipped independently. The left side features dark glass to filter out the "white blur" of daylight, allowing his heat vision to function; the right side features dark glass for protection and a series of magnifying lenses for fine clockwork, including a setting that acts as a long-range looking glass.

His tail is a powerful auxiliary limb, ridged with **pale yellow/ivory diamond-shaped spines**. These flat spines start small at the tail-tip and grow to hand-sized at the base of his neck, where they split into a "crown" before receding above his eyes.

He is perpetually clad in a charred, stiff, heat-resistant leather apron. It functions as a bandolier for **screwdrivers**, ranging from tiny precision tools to massive pry-bars. At his hip sits a large adjustable wrench with a hammer-head on the reverse side; on the other, a holster for a folding measuring stick. The apron's front pouch is a chaotic but organized hoard of nails, fasteners, and custom-made glues and patch-tapes he brews himself in a glass chemistry set.

Kog moves with the slow, deliberate inevitability of a landslide. He frequently "tastes" the air with his tongue—a habit of deep concentration that serves the practical purpose of detecting the metallic tang of an impending boiler blowout or a aetheric leak from a cracked core. In quiet moments, he works on his hobby: a tiny, intricate clockwork airship suspended in a glass bottle that, when activated, creates the perfect illusion of a vessel at full clip in the sky.

#### **Backstory**

Kog’s talent was evident from birth; he survived his **Shadow Week** trial by hot-wiring a wreck to fly his clutch to safety. This feat earned him a spot in the **Heart Forge Guild**, the elite body responsible for the systems powering the civilization. Though brilliant, Kog was a social disaster. After embarrassing a VIP at a dinner party, his upward mobility was permanently severed.

He was assigned as a **Pressure-Master** to a secondary forge maintaining the capital's power. His superior was a **Noble-Born Guildmaster** who had been gifted the position through nepotism. During a high-stakes inspection, the Noble—desperate to put on a show—ordered Kog to release a specific valve and vent. Kog politely refused, knowing that the Noble’s chosen vent (Vent 3) would cause a catastrophic failure, whereas Vent 4 would provide the dramatic steam display the Noble craved.

Offended, the Noble struck Kog with a heavy ceremonial wrench. While Kog was dazed, the Noble engaged the wrong controls. As gauges redlined and metal groaned, Kog threw the Noble aside and reversed the controls. The console ruptured, badly injuring Kog and saving the sector from a blast that would have killed thousands.

The political fallout was swift: the Noble claimed Kog had sabotaged the forge, and visting dignitaries backed the story. Unable to prove his innocence, Kog was sentenced to death. He was transported to a historic execution site—a giant circle of glass-like stone known as the **Fossilized Blood**. However, the executioner, suspecting the truth, granted Kog a "Survivor’s Chance." He gave him a cup of water, a piece of fruit, and told him to vanish. Kog marched east across the desert for a week, eventually reaching the "dirty mines" of **Port Sunder**. There, he was found by young, lazy Diplomatic Corps officers who were happy to let the "old lizard" do all the work while they spent their days partying.

#### **Traits & Tendencies**

- **Thermal Comfort:** He detests the cold of high-altitude flight and is frequently found sleeping against the steam engine manifold to soak up the heat.
    
- **Outspoken Nature:** Kog has no concept of social etiquette. He "calls it as he sees it," often insulting those in power without realizing or caring.
    

#### **Strengths**

- **Structural Diagnosis:** He doesn't need gauges. A touch of his hand, a flick of his tongue, or a single glance at a bulkhead tells him exactly which bolt is about to snap and how many minutes the engine has left.
    
- **Extreme Heat Tolerance:** He can perform "hot-fixes" inside active engine rooms at temperatures exceeding **180°C**.
    

#### **Weaknesses**

- **Visual Limitations:** He has a massive blind spot on his left side and can be blinded by bright light if his goggles are not adjusted.
    
- **The "Nobility" Trigger:** Kog harbors a visceral, silent hatred for the aristocratic and the entitled. This makes him entirely useless in matters of diplomacy or tact, as he is prone to offending anyone with undeserved power.

# **Pipin Pringle: The Cook** 

#### **Appearance & Mannerisms**

Pipin Pringle is a massive, twelve-segmented **Caterpillar-Kin** with pale mint-green, velvety skin. He wears a custom-tailored, pristine white **button-up chef’s jacket**. While he isn't nimble, he is "steady." His underside is lined with thousands of microscopic hooked "prolegs," allowing him to walk up walls and across ceilings with a rhythmic, undulating pace—often cooking upside down to maximize space in a cramped galley.

He has six pairs of hands and is never without a silver tasting spoon or a spatula. He possesses the uncanny ability to "produce" a **heavy steel cleaver** or a razor-sharp carving knife from behind his body segments as if pulling them from another dimension. He is also capable of **spinning silk**—a strong, adhesive thread he uses to secure pots during turbulence or to "web" an annoying intruder to the bulkhead.

#### **Backstory: The Revenge of the Palate**

- **The Stalled Change:** A mystery of the **Canopy Clans**, Pipin has refused to pupate for decades, preferring the "Crawl" and the sensory world of food over the rigid logic of the butterfly elders.
    
- **The Incident at The Black Rot:** Pipin was kidnapped and forced to cook for the **Obsidian Syndicate**, a group of brutal racketeers looking to impress high-ranking "Big Shots" from the Upper Roosts. They treated him like a tool, not an artist.
    
- **The Insult:** During the main course, the Syndicate leader demanded Pipin use **Iron-Root Pepper** in a delicate **Moon-Cream Soufflé**—a culinary sin that would ruin the flavor profile. Pipin refused; they threatened his life.
    
- **The Dish Served Cold:** In a fit of "Gourmet Outrage," Pipin complied. He made the dish taste like a miracle—the Big Shots wept with joy at the first bite. However, Pipin had utilized his knowledge of "Incompatible Gastronomy." Within an hour, the Syndicate and their guests were hit with **debilitating, embarrassing, and loud gastrointestinal distress** that lasted for three days.
    
- **The Flight:** He didn't just ruin their dinner; he ruined their reputations. Now, the Syndicate has a "Kill-on-Sight" order for the "Mint-Green Terror." Pipin is eager to leave Port Sunder because his face is currently on every bounty board in the Gutter.
    

#### **The Cook’s Logic**

Pipin is a perfectionist who views the lack of quality ingredients as a personal affront.

- **The Grumbling Gourmet:** He is perpetually annoyed by the ship’s pantry. _"I’ve stabilized the flavor as best I could,"_ he’ll grumble, _"but without a Three-Sided Ginger-Root to bridge the acidity, this is little more than pig-slop."_ He then serves a **five-star, seven-course feast** that makes the crew feel like royalty.
    
- **The Wall-Walking Counselor:** Pipin often "drops in" on crew members—literally. He’ll be walking across the ceiling of the engine room or the bridge, upside down, and start a conversation. He doesn't give advice; he just asks, _"Does this sauce need more salt, or are you just crying into your boots for no reason?"_
    

#### **Quirks**

- **Silk-Rigging:** He uses his silk to create "hammocks" for his ingredients, hanging them from the ceiling so they don't bruise during Chaos Surges.
    
- **The Impossible List:** He carries a scroll of ingredients he "requires," including things like **"Morning Dew from a Silent Valley"** and **"Salted Wind."**
    

#### **Strengths**

- **Morale Officer:** His **Thunder Buns** provide the crew with sustained energy and hope.
    
- **Vertical Utility:** His ability to walk on walls means he is never "in the way" in the galley; he just cooks on the ceiling while the crew eats below.
    

#### **Weaknesses**

- **The Wanted Man:** His past with the Syndicate means he can’t go into certain ports without a heavy disguise.
    
- **Ingredient Greed:** He will jeopardize a silent mission to "whisper-harvest" a rare herb if he smells it nearby.

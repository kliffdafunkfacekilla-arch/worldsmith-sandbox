### The Ghost Flotilla
##### 3.0 Operational Doctrine: The "Ghost Flotilla" Strategy

The "Ghost Flotilla" is the tactical manifestation of the Sky-Barons' ideology. This is not a formal, standing fleet that can be tracked or engaged in conventional warfare. Rather, it is a strategic doctrine of utilizing a network of hidden, temporary, and camouflaged bases to maintain strategic unpredictability and operational independence. This represents a highly effective model of asymmetrical power projection, allowing them to vanish from the maps of their adversaries A core component of this strategy is the deliberate avoidance of established, controlled ports. Their nomadic nature demands anchorages that are beyond the reach and surveillance of Major Factions. Their favored temporary bases include:

* Remote, high-altitude plateaus, often shrouded in cloud cover.  
* Sheared cliff faces hidden within deep, inaccessible canyons.  
* Small, uncharted floating islands with unique atmospheric conditions that make them difficult to detect. The central and most unique tactic of this doctrine is the tradition of  **"ghosting" old, abandoned airship wrecks** . Baron crews are adept at locating these derelicts in remote regions and transforming them into temporary, camouflaged bases. These wrecks form a widespread, unseen network of safe havens and supply caches, allowing Baron ships to disappear for extended periods and re-emerge unexpectedly. The strategic advantage of this doctrine is a form of  **strategic denial** . By refusing to concentrate their forces or rely on fixed infrastructure, they deny larger, more conventional powers a viable target. This renders conventional military doctrines of blockade and direct assault largely ineffective, making them nearly impossible to track, attack directly, or subject to economic coercion. The Ghost Flotilla is the cornerstone of their survival, enabling a relatively small confederation to remain a persistent and influential force in the Shattered World.

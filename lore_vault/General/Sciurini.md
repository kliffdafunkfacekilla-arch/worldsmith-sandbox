# The Sciurini (The Sky-Jacks)

**Common Name:** Glider-Kin / Flying [[Squirrels]] **Scientific Designation:** _Pteromyini Velox_ **Role:** Couriers, High-Altitude Loggers, Scouts **Home City:** **Glider’s Roost** (A dizzying collection of platforms at the very crown of the forest, connected by zip-lines and updrafts).

## 1.0 Biology & Appearance

- **Physiology:** Small and aerodynamic, standing about 3 to 4 feet tall. They have large, dark eyes adapted for depth perception at high speeds.
    
- **The Patagium:** A furry membrane stretching from wrist to ankle. When limbs are extended, it forms a square "wing." It allows them to glide at ratios of 10:1 (10 feet forward for every 1 foot of drop).
    
- **Bone Structure:** Their bones are semi-hollow (avian-like), making them fragile in a brawl but incredibly light. A 4-foot Sciurini weighs less than a human child.
    
- **The Tail:** A flat, rudder-like tail used for mid-air steering and braking.
    

## 2.0 Psychology & Culture

- **The Philosophy of "Velocity":** To a Sciurini, the ground is a trap. Safety is found in speed. They are culturally uncomfortable with standing still. Their conversations are rapid-fire, often overlapping each other.
    
- **Adrenaline Addiction:** They don't just endure risk; they crave it. The "Roost" culture celebrates the closest calls. A scar from a branch-scrape at 60mph is a badge of honor.
    
- **The "Vertical Caste":** They look down on ground-dwellers (literally and figuratively). They refer to non-flyers as "Lead-Boots."
    

## 3.0 Industrial Role: The Ghost-Loggers

- **The [[Ghost-Teak]] Harvest:** They are the only ones agile enough to reach the **[[Ghost-Teak]]** trees—dead, petrified wood caught in the dangerous web of the upper canopy storm-layer.
    
    - _The Danger:_ Harvesting requires cutting massive logs while suspended 300 feet in the air, then "surfing" the falling log down to the processing yards.
        
- **The Courier Guild:** They run the "Flash-Post." If a contract needs to get from Vulpus-Prime to the Sump in an hour, you hire a Glider. They charge by the minute.
    
    - _Security:_ They are uncatchable. Bandits can't rob what they can't catch.
        

## 4.0 Military Role: The Drop-Troopers

- **Style:** Aerial Ambush.
    
- **Weaponry:**
    
    - **Drop-Darts:** Weighted steel flechettes dropped from above. Gravity does the work; they can punch through helmets.
        
    - **The "Cutter-Wire":** Monofilament wires strung between trees to de-capitate enemy cavalry or snare airships.
        
- **Tactics:** They never land. They dive-bomb, release their payload, and use the momentum to hook a branch and swing away. Fighting them is like fighting angry rain.
    

## 5.0 Social Quirks

- **The "Touch-Code":** In high winds, shouting is useless. They communicate flight paths by tapping rapidly on each other's shoulders or backs. They do this unconsciously even on the ground.
    
- **Sleeping Habits:** They cannot sleep on flat beds (it feels like falling). They sleep in "Sock-Hammocks" suspended over open drops.
    
- **Twitching:** They are constantly stretching their membranes or checking wind direction with a wet finger. It makes other species nervous.

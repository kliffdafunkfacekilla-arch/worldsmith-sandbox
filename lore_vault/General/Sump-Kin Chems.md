## 1. The Philosophy: "Dirty Science"

[[Sump-Kin]] are not Alchemists; they are Industrial Biologists. They view the body as a wet machine.

- **The Doctrine:** "Effective, Not Safe."
    
- **The Logic:** If the machine has an infection (rust), apply a solvent. If it has pain (grinding gears), apply grease. If the solvent burns the skin or the grease causes addiction, it is immaterial—as long as the machine keeps running for the duration of the shift.
    

## 2. The Purity Index: Class-Based Refinement

[[Sump-Kin]] don't just sell drugs; they sell _grades_. The difference between a worker’s stimulant and a noble’s cognitive enhancer isn't the effect—it's the toxicity.

### **Grade C: "Sludge-Grade" (The Fodder's Choice)**

- **Consumer:** Laborers, low-level criminals, desperate mercenaries, and [[Chaos Corps]].
    
- **Characteristics:** Unfiltered and volatile. Sourced directly from Vaneer industrial runoff.
    
- **The Cost:** It gets the job done instantly but comes with visible rot: yellowing eyes, weeping skin lesions, or the "Sump-Shake" (permanent tremors).
    

### **Grade B: "Iron-Grade" (The Soldier's Standard)**

- **Consumer:** [[Iron Caldera]]’s Alchemical Warfare Corps, Guild Enforcers, and Ship Captains.
    
- **Characteristics:** Chemically scrubbed. It provides the desired effect (aggression or stamina) with managed risks.
    
- **The Cost:** The "Crash." A user will be incapacitated for days after use (migraines, exhaustion), but their organs remain mostly intact.
    

### **Grade A: "Baron's-Choice" (The Gilded Vial)**

- **Consumer:** Highborn Senators (Horses), Rat Directors, and the Toad Barons.
    
- **Characteristics:** Pure, distilled elegance. Scented with musk or spices to mask the chemical smell.
    
- **The Cost:** Money. It is 100x the price of Sludge-Grade. It provides hyper-focus or euphoria with zero immediate physical cost.
    

## 3. Military Doctrine: "Burner" Units & The Leash

Professional armies ([[Wolves]]/Ironborn) avoid addiction because it breaks discipline. Therefore, chemical enhancement is treated as a "Deployment Protocol."

### **The "Burner" Units (Elite Vanguard)**

- **The Alchemical Dragoons ([[Iron Caldera]]):**
    
    - **Mechanism:** Injectors are locked into their armor. The soldier cannot activate them; they are triggered remotely or by a commanding officer when combat is imminent.
        
    - **The Flush:** Post-combat, these soldiers must undergo a painful "Chemical Flush" (field dialysis) to strip the toxins before addiction sets in.
        
- **The Haze-Walkers (Mercenaries):**
    
    - **The Drug:** A combat-variant of _Black-Comb_. It suppresses all pain and fear.
        
    - **The Result:** "Hollow Men." They walk through gunfire because they cannot feel it. They are often considered "single-use assets" because they fight until their bodies physically fall apart.
        

### **The Control: "The Alchemical Leash"**

Used for [[Sump-Kin]] slaves and untrusted mercenary companies.

- **Binary Compound:**
    
    - **Part A (The Power):** Gives the strength/speed. It is cheap and slowly toxic.
        
    - **Part B (The Stabilizer):** Neutralizes the toxin of Part A. It is incredibly expensive.
        
- **The Trap:** If a soldier defects, they stop receiving Part B. They die a horrifying death within 48 hours.
    

## 4. The Product Catalog: "Mud-Tech"

### **Explosives: "Sump-Ignis" (The Unstable Gel)**

- **Nature:** A volatile, gelatinous sludge refined from swamp methane.
    
- **Utility:** A "Wet Explosion." It clings to surfaces and burns underwater. Ideal for Vaneer Deconstructors leveling terrain.
    
- **Risk:** Extremely unstable. A jar dropped on a dock levels the pier.
    

### **Pharmaceuticals: "The Kill-or-Cure"**

- **Antibiotic ("Caustic-Cure"):** An acidic syrup that cauterizes infection instantly. It leaves "Sump-Tracks" (chemical burn scars) inside the veins.
    
- **Painkiller ("The Grey-Out"):** Disconnects the brain from the body. The user is conscious but feels nothing. Addiction comes from the fear of "re-connecting" to the pain.
    

### **Corrosives: "Scale-Strip"**

- **Nature:** Industrial solvent capable of eating organic matter and soft stone.
    
- **Use:** Legal use for cleaning clockwork slag. Illegal use by [[Crimson Corsairs]] to melt bank vaults and ship hulls.
    

### **Combat Drugs: "Viper-Haze"**

- **Nature:** A time-dilation combat drug and highly addictive narcotic sourced from the skin-secretions of the swamp-dwelling **Ranidae** (Frog-folk) of the Delta Wetlands.
    
- **Utility:** Chemically alters the nervous system to warp and slow the user's perception of time, allowing a combatant to react to high-speed projectiles or perform intricate mechanical movements with impossible speed.
    
- **Risk:** Extreme addiction risk. Regular users suffer from rapid neurological burnout and sensory desensitization—once the drug wears off, the normal passage of time feels agonizingly slow and empty, leading to severe depression and permanent lethargy ("the Haze-Stare").
    

### **The Rumor: "The Bloat-Mist" (Alchemical Weapon)**

- **Nature:** Aerosolized, hyper-accelerated fungal spores.
    
- **Effect:** Inhaling it causes rapid fungal growth in the lungs. The victim drowns on dry land in minutes.
    
- **Status:** [[Sump-Kin]]'s "Nuclear Option." Unused, but threatened.

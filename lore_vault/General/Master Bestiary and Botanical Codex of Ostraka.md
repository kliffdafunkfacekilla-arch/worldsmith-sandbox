##### 2. High-Altitude and Atmospheric Fauna

###### The Sky-Grazer ( Bovinus Aetheris )

A high-volume atmospheric processor. These units float through the upper tiers feeding on airborne vegetation and clouds of static-charged vapor.

**Voltaic Fleece:**  A biological heat-sink consisting of hollow, keratin-like strands filled with a conductive gel.

**Capacitance:**  Functions as a "Dry Aether-core." Raw fleece is a high-volatility explosive hazard if not grounded.

**Insulation:**  Once drained of its charge, it serves as the premier shielding against Aetheric "Blowouts."

**Harvesting Protocol:**  Metallic tools cause lethal electrical arcing. Requires  **ceramic or obsidian tools** . Panicked units release a "Spark-Stampede," incinerating all personnel within a 50-meter radius.

###### Glimmer-Wings

Aggressive interceptor insects native to chaos-warped sectors. Their wings are composed of shifting light, functioning as active camouflage for ambush protocols.

##### 3. Terrestrial Livestock and Scavengers

###### The Gritbore

Rodent-like reptilian units with high-density scales. They function as the primary biomass fuel for labor-units during  **Shadow Week** . They are capable of consuming mineral matter (rocks) to sustain caloric output.

###### The Ash-Bison ( Bison Cinereus )

Bovines 1.5 times the size of standard oxen, utilized for heavy biomass extraction.

**Concrete Coat:**  Wallows in volcanic ash and mud, creating an arrow-proof, stone-like armor plating.

**Spine-Leap Protocol:**  A standardized combat-readiness evaluation for Ursine youth; requires jumping onto a galloping unit and penetrating the spinal armor gap.

###### The Fur-Wyrm ( Serpens villosus )

A 4-foot scavenger occupying the coyote-niche.

**Locomotion:**  Side-winding on snow or slinking through rock-fissures.

**Behavioral Hazard:**  Prone to thermal failure if stationary; they will infiltrate sleeping bags or campfires to steal heat. They are predatory opportunists that target the wounded or stationary.

##### 4. Apex Predators and Specialized Combatants

###### The Obsidian Razor-Strider ( Struthio obsidianus )

An 8-foot flightless predator capable of outpacing steam-engines.

**Plumage:**  Biological volcanic glass.

**Glass-Storm Tactic:**  Units body-check targets at high velocity, using quills like a cheese-grater to shred the prey's flanks.

**Economic Utility:**  Molted quills serve as "Feather-Knives"—single-use biological scalpels for surgeons and assassins, sharper than forged steel.

###### The Titan-Boa

Massive cattle-eating serpents processed in the Coil-Yards.

**Culling Day:**  An industrial harvesting event where  **Mungo**  (Mongoose-folk) utilize hyper-reflexes and stunning-batons to neutralize these 2-ton predators for leather and meat reclamation.

###### Somoan Wood-Hen (Screamer-Hen)

Flightless avian units with zero self-preservation instincts.

**Utility:**  Acts as a biological siren system.

**Mine-Sweeper:**  Utilized as sensors to detect traps or "Flux-Shifts" in high-risk zones.

##### 5. The Botanical Codex: Strategic and Parasitic Flora

###### The Sacred Groves

"Reality Spikes" containing Dragonstone elementals.

**Outer Shell:**  Sector of localized physics failure.

**Boundary:**  High-friction "Storm Wall" of kinetic resistance.

**Core:**  A state of  **Impossible Serenity** —ancient Fae cities preserved in absolute stasis by the anti-chaos energy of sacrificed Elders.

###### Grave-Roots

Carnivorous plants that mimic stone-mimic timber or dead trees. They utilize root-based vibration sensing to strike prey avoiding high-risk "Flux-Shifts."

###### Stone-Root

Fossilized mineral-plant essential for heavy construction and firearm furniture. Its high-molarity mineral content makes it entirely fireproof.

###### Ghost Flowers

Essential Aether-engine stabilizers.

**Containment Protocol:**  Highly susceptible to  **"Dry Rot"**  (spiritual and physical desiccation). To prevent petrification into useless mineral matter, they must be transported within the internal water-reservoirs of  **Cactus-Kin** .

###### The Strangler Fig

A parasitic biological saboteur utilized by the  **Cult of the Silent Echo** . It roots into the joints of the "Ancients" of Arbor-Prime, using accelerated growth to physically paralyze the walking city and expose it to supersonic lead-chaos destruction.

##### 7. Biological Engines and Bio-Engineering

###### Abyssal Snail

House-sized mollusks providing unstoppable torque for  **Snail-Draught**  convoys. In the  **River-King’s Shuffle** , they are tethered to surface barges by iron chains, ignoring surface weather and Aetheric surges.

###### Weaver-Cow (Giant Spider)

Ranching monopoly held by the  **Bradypods** . Due to their slow metabolism, Bradypods do not trigger the spider's vibration-response. Produces  **Silk-Steel (Iron-Weave)** , the strongest material for airship rigging.

###### Titan-Aphid (Sugar-Tick)

Dog-sized insects farmed by the  **Formicidae**  (Ants). Latch onto the inner sap-veins of  **Iron-Wood trees**  to produce "Honeydew" sweetener/mead.

###### Cloud-Cutter Ray (Derived Reptile)

Advanced high-altitude mounts for  **Storm-Lancer**  units.

**Chroma-Skin:**  Tactile communication and active camouflage.

**Chaos-Gland:**  A tail-stinger that injects volatile chemical fluid, causing rapid necrosis or combustion.

**The Red Ghost Phase:**  Triggered by a rider's death. The Ray locks into a permanent  **Blood Red**  state. These units are solitary, aggressive, and sire the strongest offspring.  **Killing a Red Ghost is a capital offense.**
